Steckler Michael S. Steckler I am a geophysicist who works mainly on sedimentary systems with projects related both to tectonics and earthquakes, and to sea level and stratigraphy. A particular interest is vertical motions of the Earth’s surface and their preservation in the sedimentary record. My main field area is currently Bangladesh where I work both on the heavily-sedimented IndoBurma subduction zone (IBSZ), including the earthquake hazard, and on deltaic processes in the Ganges-Brahmaputra Delta (GBD), including sea level rise, land subsidence, sedimentation and river avulsions. I have worked in Bangladesh, and adjacent parts of India and Myanmar, for 20 years, taking part in and leading a number of field campaigns. I am also currently working on tectonics in Western Turkey, and on fresh and salty groundwater in both Bangladesh and offshore New Jersey. Fields of Interest Sedimentary basin formation and development, sea level change and its preservation in the stratigraphic record, tectonic uplift and subsidence of earth's surface, tectonics of extension, lithospheric flexure and isostasy, thermal history of rifts and basin, stratigraphic record of global change at continental margins, natural hazards of sea level rise and coastal subsidence, subduction tectonics and earthquake hazard.
