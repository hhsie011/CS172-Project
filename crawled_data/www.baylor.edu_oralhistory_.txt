The Institute for Oral History has recorded and preserved oral histories since 1970, earning along the way a strong reputation for multidisciplinary outreach to both academic scholars and community historians by providing professional leadership, educational tools, and research opportunities. Explore Search Our Collection Over 4000 of our interviews are available online! Grants Learn more about our grant opportunities. TOHA Learn more about the Texas Oral History Association. Workshops Learn more about our ongoing series of workshops. Survivors of Genocide The Institute for Oral History conducted fourteen oral histories with survivors of the genocides in Cambodia, Rwanda/Burundi, Bosnia, and Darfur. This work was contracted through a grant from the Texas Holocaust and Genocide Commission in order for survivors who now live in Texas to tell their stories of escaping the horrors of their homelands and finding a new life in the United States. Learn More Advancing Scholarship With Grants The Institute for Oral History currently provides three major research grants. Waco History lets you explore the people, places, and moments that have shaped our communityâs history. Learn about the region through layered, map-based, multimedia presentations, use social media to share your stories, and experience curated historical tours of Central Texas. Learn More Tattooed on My Soul: Texas Veterans Remember WWII For 45 years the Baylor University Institute for Oral History has dutifully gathered the eyewitness testimonies of WWII veterans living in the state of Texas. Now seventeen of the most compelling narratives are available in the most recent BUIOH publication "Tattooed on My Soul: Texas Veterans Remember World War II." Learn More Advanced Workshops Institute staff provide guidance and education by executing several oral history workshops each year. These workshops range from introductory to specialized, from privately-arranged to those where registration is literally open to the world.
