Resources Schedule Builder Schedule of Classes » Withdraw After the Start of the Semester It is important that prior to withdrawal from all classes that you look at the academic calendar for withdrawal and refund deadlines. In addition, there are a number of items to consider before withdrawing. Effective for Spring 2020 Term: If you completing a full term withdrawal from one of these programs please use the online Withdrawal Form. During this time the Office of the University Registrar will work with your School if additional approval for dropping or the full term withdraw is necessary. Please regularly check your University of Kansas email for important communications in regards to your drop/withdraw. When your form has been processed you will receive a confirmation email. Procedures for Students in: Pharmacy Undergraduate Engineering Undergraduate Architectural Engineering Applied English Center Law Submit the withdrawal form to Enrollment and Financial Aid Services at the KU Visitor Center (1502 Iowa St.) or the reception desk at the Edwards campus. Withdrawals are not official until submitted and entered into Enroll & Pay. They must be submitted before the office closes on the deadline date. After the Drop (DRP) hold has been applied (first day of the Second Period Drop), procedure for students in: Architecture Education (Undergraduates only) Social Welfare Obtain approval from your school and the no drop hold will be removed. Students may then withdraw online via Enroll & Pay until 11:59 p.m. the same day the drop hold was removed. Students may not withdraw online or by paper after the deadline date. All Other Students Instructions for withdrawing from all classes online can be found at https://sis.ku.edu/withdraw-all-classes. Re-enrolling for the Next Semester If you withdraw on the first day of class or after, you will be assigned an enrollment appointment time for next semester. If you wish to return to the University of Kansas, log on to Enroll and Pay before main enrollment (mid-March for Summer/Fall enrollment; mid-October for Spring enrollment) to view your appointment time. Follow the continuing student enrollment instructions. This is valid ONLY for the semester immediately following the semester in which you withdraw.
