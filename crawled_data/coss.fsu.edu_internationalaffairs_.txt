Our programs feature fields of study which may be individually tailored to fit each student’s unique career aspirations. In these programs, students also can intern and study abroad, furthering their experience with firsthand, real-world scenarios. Students select approved courses from the participating departments of Anthropology Economics Geography History Modern Languages Philosophy Political Science Public Administration Religion Sociology Urban and Regional Planning We help prepare students for international careers in the public and private sectors, such as positions in government, business, non-governmental and non-profit organizations, journalism, research, and teaching. Additionally, each program may serve as a vehicle for a more specialized doctoral program, usually within one of the disciplines represented by the participating departments.
