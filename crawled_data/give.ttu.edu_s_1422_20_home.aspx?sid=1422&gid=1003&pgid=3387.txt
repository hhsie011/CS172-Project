President Lawrence Schovanec As Texas Tech nears its 100th anniversary, the university's efforts along with President Lawrence Schovanec's priorities are guided by its strategic plan: A Foundation for the Next Century | A Pathway to 2025. Within the strategic plan are three goals to move the university forward and solidify its position as a premier public research university. Read A Foundation for the Next Century | A Pathway to 2025 >> Educate and Empower a Diverse Student Body Undergraduate and graduate scholarships draw quality students to Texas Tech and influence retention and graduation numbers by lessening financial barriers. The General Scholarship Fund enables Texas Tech to offer need- and merit-based scholarships, including Presidential Endowed Scholarships. Give Now to the General Scholarship Fund >> Learn More about Giving to Texas Tech Through Scholarships >> Enable Innovative Research and Creative Activities Having quality faculty is one of the best ways to increase innovative research. Increasing the number of endowed chairs and endowed professorships is one of the strategies to achieve this priority. Learn More about Giving to Texas Tech Through Supporting Faculty and Research >> Public-private partnerships with the university provide an additional way to expand strategic research collaborations, shown recently with Premier Oilfield Group in Midland. Read about Premier Oilfield Group's Public-Private Partnership with Texas Tech >> Transform Lives and Communities Through Strategic Outreach and Engaged Scholarship Building infrastructure that enables professional development and facilitates engagement and collaboration within communities means supporting a variety of campus initiatives and programs. Your gift to the President's Fund for Excellence allows the president to use funds where seen fit to accomplish these strategies and for what benefits campus most.
