Events Past Event H24: 24 Hours, 24 Women, 24 Stories Share Add to Calendar: Google Yahoo March 8, 2022 12:00 PM - 2:30 PM Cinéma l'Arlequin, 76 rue de Rennes, 75006 Paris In-person event | 6:00 p.m. (Paris) RSVP REQUIRED H24 is a series of 24 short films by 24 female writers, performed by 24 women actors, all based on women’s real life experiences of sexism, harassment and violence. A diversity of female voices and talent from across Europe come together for a series that tackles head on the everyday brutality experienced by women. The screening will be followed by a conversation in English with producers Justine Henochsberg and Julie Guesnon Amarante. H24 is an ARTE production (2021). In various languages with English subtitles. This event is co-organized with the Columbia Institute for Ideas and Imagination and Lost in Frenchlation. Speakers: Trained as a lawyer, Justine Henochsberg worked for five years at the Cinémathèque Française as an administrator (Cinémathèque de la Danse) where she produced several events and documentaries. She was then responsible for the development of a new concept of film clubs for children, La Lanterne Magique, for the group of independent cinemas Etoile Cinémas. After journalism school (L'Ecole Supérieure de Journalisme) and obtaining an advanced degree in “Cinema and Anthroplogy,” Julie Guesnon Amarante was production manager and then executive producer for more than eight years with La Génerale de la Production. In particular, she worked on documentary productions for French TV (Arte, France Télévisions, LCP, Planète, TV5...), international TV, and cinema. Justine Henochsberg and Julie Guesnon Amarante co-founded Les Batèlieres Productions in 2013. A film and audiovisual production company, Les Batelières Productions has an editorial line that is built around social, political, and cultural projects as well as projects for young audiences.
