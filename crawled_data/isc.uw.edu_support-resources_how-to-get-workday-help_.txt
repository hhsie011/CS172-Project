Holidays in Workday Integrated Service Center Support Resources How to Get Workday Help How to Get Workday Help Last updated Tuesday, January 25, 2022 Trying to figure out who to contact when you need assistance with HR, payroll and benefits questions or issues? Select the appropriate option below to determine where to start when you need Workday help. If You Are a UW Employee... Refer to the ISC’s self-service Support Resources. If you still have a question, contact your HR administrator or manager. Lastly, at the suggestion of your HR Admin/Manager, contact the ISC. Refer to the Tips To Help Us Help You page for guidance. If You Are a Former UW Employee... Refer to the Resources For Former Employees page for guidance. If You Are a Workday Role-Holder or Unit Change Lead... This category applies if you hold security roles in Workday such as (but not limited to): – Academic Partner – Costing Allocations Coordinator – HCM Initiate 2 – HR Partner – I-9 Coordinator – Time and Absence Approver/Initiate Refer to the ISC’s self-service Support Resources. Then, if you still have a question, call/contact your Named Support Contact (NSC). If your NSC cannot resolve your issue, work with them to contact the ISC. Refer to the Tips To Help Us Help You page for guidance. If You Are a Named Support Contact... If the issue will (or might) affect employee pay or benefits on the upcoming paycheck, contact the ISC; refer to the Tips To Help Us Help You page for guidance. Otherwise, refer to the ISC’s self-service Support Resources. If you still have a question, contact the ISC – as a Named Support Contact, you have the option to open a ticket, or speak directly with your HCM Service Partner.
