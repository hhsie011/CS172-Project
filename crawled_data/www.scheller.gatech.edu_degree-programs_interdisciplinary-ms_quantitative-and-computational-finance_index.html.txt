M.S. in Quantitative & Computational Finance (QCF) Faculty Curriculum Admissions & Application Career Development FAQs For Employers Master of Science in Analytics at Georgia Tech The Master of Science degree program in Quantitative and Computational Finance (QCF) is an interdisciplinary STEM degree program between three of Georgia Tech's most prestigious units: the Scheller College of Business, the H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial & Systems Engineering, and the School of Mathematics. Apply today before our final round deadlines: March 15th for International Students and May 1st for US Citizens/Permanent Residents. What is QCF? Quantitative A strong foundation of quantitative skills for complex mathematical modeling. Computational Computational skills to implement these models using multiple statistical techniques and programming languages. Finance A solid, practical understanding of Finance theory and institutional details. Admissions & Application The QCF program admits for both the spring and fall semesters, with start dates in January and August respectively. Decisions are typically released 4-8 weeks after a complete application is submitted. Curriculum Learn from a regimen designed to give you the tools for a successful professional career. The QCF curriculum pairs a comprehensive core to give you the foundation you will need, with flexible elective options, to customize the program to your specific career goals. Career Development The QCF program is professionally-focused to prepare you for a career that is practical. You will learn skills useful in structuring transactions, managing risk, constructing investment strategies, and analyzing analytical data.
