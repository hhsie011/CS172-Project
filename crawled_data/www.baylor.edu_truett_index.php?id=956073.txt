Truett Seminary>Students>Student Organizations Student Organizations Truett Women in Ministry Mission/Purpose Statement: The mission of Truett Women in Ministry is to equip, educate, and encourage women as they pursue the call to ministry, as well as to educate men so they may better empower the women in their communities to pursue calls to ministry. Activities: Each fall, Truett Women in Ministry hosts a joint movie night and panel discussion with the Truett Black Student Association as well as a panel discussion of women in ministry from the Waco community. The organization also leads the Truett Women in Ministry chapel service every Spring. Advisor(s): Julie Covington, Jo Ann Sharkey Reinowski, and Dr. Gaynor Yancey Contact: truettwomeninministry@gmail.com Click here to view the TWIM 2021-22 Year-in-Review Report Truett Black Seminarians Association Mission/Purpose Statement: Truett Black Seminarians Association exists to connect and encourage God-called Black women and men for gospel ministry in and alongside Christ's Church by the power of the Holy Spirit. Activities: Monthly, TBSA meets for a time of sharing, celebration, and education with Black pioneers in ministry. Each Fall, TBSA hosts a joint movie night and panel discussion with the Truett Women in Ministry. The organization also leads the TBSA Truett Chapel service every Spring. Advisor(s): Dr. Stephen Reid and Dr. Stephanie Boddie Contact: TruettBSA@gmail.com Watch & Walk Truett Mission/Purpose Statement: Watch & Walk Truett is the Truett Seminary Chapter of Watch and Walk Ministry. The mission of Watch and Walk Ministry is to proclaim the gospel of Jesus Christ and promote holistic discipleship through Christian art and capacity building programs. Activities: Each year, Watch & Walk Truett hosts a variety of events like “I Testify” (music, spoken word, and testimonies) and “My World” (music and conversation on creation care). The organization also hosts a monthly prayer meeting called “Watchers’ Moment” both in the Fall and Spring semesters. Advisor(s): Dr. Michael W.
