#weRUnited One year after New Jersey’s first COVID-19 case, we continue to find the best in ourselves and in Rutgers. A Community Moving Forward Over these past 12 months, a new reality has demanded ideas and solutions that no one thought possible, but together we found a path forward: from the rapid transition to online learning and a virtual community to discovering ways to detect an unknown virus and distributing life-saving vaccines. There’s still more work to do, but—united—we’ve already come so far. Highlights Whether testing new and effective vaccines, putting graduates to work when we need them most, or teaching a new generation of detectives to isolate the disease, Rutgers is helping us all today, and tomorrow. A COVID-19 Game Changer Rutgers professor Andrew Brooks' revolutionary test changed the game, enabling more people than ever to be tested quickly and easily. The Nation’s Largest Study of Health Care Workers Critical data from experts in the field will help determine new treatments, new testing, and invaluable long-term health tracking. Medical, Pharmacy and Nursing Students Graduate Early This forward-thinking decision empowered vitally needed health care personnel to take the next step toward joining the front lines in the fight against COVID-19. Johnson & Johnson Vaccine Clinical Trial Rutgers' recruitment of vaccine trial volunteers was the second highest globally for the phase 3 clinical research study to evaluate the safety and efficacy of a COVID-19 vaccine candidate. Contact Tracing Training Corps Rutgers School of Public Health served an integral role with contact tracing as part of Governor Murphy’s The Road Back recovery plan. Moderna Vaccine Clinical Trials Rutgers was selected as a site for Moderna COVID-19 vaccine clinical trial.
