About Us Visit Explore About Collections Give to ABL Research & Instruction Visit A Browning Music Concert The Spring Baylor Chamber Singers concert features musical selections from Armstrong Browning Library & Museum of the poetry of the Brownings set to music. This concert will be held in the beautiful Foyer of Meditation on the main floor of the library. Tickets are free and available from the McCrary Ticket Office or by phone. Learn More Mar. 24, 2022 ABL Visiting Scholar to Present Lord Byron's Visions of Venice On Friday, April 8, 2022, at 3:00 p.m. CDT in the Hankamer Treasure Room of Armstrong Browning Library, Dr. Mark Sandy, Professor of English, Durham University (UK) and Armstrong Browning Library Three-Month Research Fellow, will present, "She hath a Spell Beyond Her Name: Byron's Poetic Reflections on Venice, Subjectivity, and Temporality." This event is free and open to the public and will be livestreamed on Zoom. Read More Mar. 20, 2022 Recent Gift Enhances Harriet Martineau Resources at Armstrong Browning Library Professor Deborah A. Logan, professor emerita of Victorian literature at Western Kentucky University, recently gifted Baylor's Armstrong Browning Library and Museum with her personal Harriet Martineau collection. This donation includes over 100 volumes that will fuel future research for visiting scholars. Read More Oct. 28, 2021 Browning Scholar Enjoys Long-Awaited Research Visit In late 2019, Marta Gimenez Orti, a doctoral student from Spain, received a scholarship from Universidad Complutense de Madrid. The award enabled her to conduct dissertation research for a semester at Armstrong Browning Library & Museum at Baylor University, which is the international center for research on the lives and works of Robert and Elizabeth Barrett Browning. However, as she began to make travel plans it soon became clear her Baylor visit would be delayed due to COVID-19.
