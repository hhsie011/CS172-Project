Faculty-led Programs AAA Faculty-Led Programs Promoting Study Abroad Faculty Led Programs University Trips Advisor Resources Forms The information provided in this section of our website is intended for faculty who, with the support of their department and college/school, would seek the assistance of the Study Abroad Office in the development and administration of a Faculty-Led study abroad program. Benefits of UM Faculty-Led Programs For faculty: Significantly enhances oneâ€™s teaching, research, and service Provides global competence which can then be passed on to students Affords an opportunity to connect with students in a more meaningful way as opposed to large lecture-style courses Adds experiential learning objectives to your courses Provides a fully-supported method for one to travel and conduct academic work abroad For students: Classes are taught by UM faculty Students earn credit towards their major, minor and/or degree program Programs are pre-planned and structured Programs are sponsored by UM departments/schools/colleges Students have the opportunity to build strong connections with UM faculty members Responsibilities of Faculty Directors Complete and submit the Intent to Propose Form, and once approved submit the Program Proposal Form Arrange and manage program academics (syllabus, textbooks, international partners, guest lecturers, etc.) Actively promote program to obtain target enrollment Review applications and select participants Attend faculty pre-departure orientation with other faculty and the Study Abroad Office Facilitate a site-specific pre-departure orientation with students Provide an on-site orientation upon arrival in host country Respond to student misconduct, behavioral/personal problems Remain on-call 24/7 for
