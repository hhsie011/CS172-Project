window) | Print-Friendly Page (opens a new window) Facebook this Page (opens a new window) Tweet this Page (opens a new window) Bachelor of Arts in Ethnic Studies (120 UNITS) The Bachelor of Arts in Ethnic Studies includes courses for the option, General Education, all-university requirements and free electives. Option in Asian American Studies (36 units) Core Courses (15 units) ASAM 101 - Introduction to Ethnic Studies (3) ASAM 201 - The History of Asian Pacific Americans (3) ASAM 300 - Introduction to Asian Pacific American Studies (3) ASAM 307 - Research and Writing in Ethnic Studies (3) ETHN 490 - Ethnic Studies Senior Seminar (3) Topical Requirements (12 units) In consultation with an adviser, students may focus their studies on Asian American Ethnic Groups or topics in Asian American Studies, such as literature, art, communication, families, psychology or law. Specific Asian American Ethnic Groups (3 units) ASAM 220 - Vietnamese Communities in the U.S. (3) ASAM 360 - Multiple Heritage Asian American and Pacific Islanders (3) ASAM 362A - Filipina/o American Experience (3) ASAM 364 - The South Asian American Experience (3) ASAM 366 - The Korean American Experience (3) ASAM 370 - The Chinese American Experience (3) HIST 382 - World War II Japanese American Evacuation (3) or ASAM 382 - World War II Japanese American Evacuation (3) Topics in Asian American Studies (9 units) ASAM 308 - Asian American Women (3) ASAM 320 - Asian Pacific American Cultural Studies (3) ASAM 322 - Asian Pacific Americans and Religion (3) ASAM 325 - Asian American Film and Video (3) ASAM 327 - Asian American Literature (3) ASAM 340 - Asian American Communication (3) ASAM 342 - Asian Pacific American Families (3) ASAM 344 - Asian Pacific American Identities (3) ASAM 346 - Asian American Psychology (3) COMM 438T - Specialized Reporting (3) HUSR 318 - Human Services for Immigrants and Refugees (3) Comparative Cultures Breadth Requirements (6 units) Non-Asian Ethnic Groups and Inter-ethnic Relations in the U.S. (3 units) AFAM 310 - Black Women in America (3) AFAM 311 - Race and Relationships (3) AFAM 320 - Black Political Thought (3) AFAM 322 - Psychology of African Americans (3) AFAM 335 - History of Racism (3) AMST 301 - American Character (3) AMST 377 - Prejudice and American Culture (3) CHIC 220 - Chicana/o Identities (3) CHIC 303 - Chicano/Mexican Cultures (3) CHIC 337 - Contemporary Chicana/o Literature (3) CHIC 340 - Mexican/Chicano Intellectual Thought (3) CHIC 345 - History of the Chicano (3) SOCI 357 - Race and Ethnic Relations (3) WGST 302 - Introduction to Women’s Studies (3) Asian Language or Asian Cultures and Social Institutions (3 units) Asian Languages Courses include, but are not limited to the following: CHIN 100 - Introduction to Chinese Conversation (3) CHIN 101 - Fundamental Chinese-A (5) CHIN 102 - Fundamental Chinese-B (5) JAPN 101 - Fundamental Japanese-A (5) JAPN 102 - Fundamental Japanese-B (5) VIET 201 - Vietnamese for Vietnamese Speakers-A (3) VIET 202 - Vietnamese for Vietnamese Speakers-B (3) Any 200 or higher level course in Chinese or Japanese Asian Cultures and Institutions Courses include, but are not limited to the following: ANTH 347 - Peoples of the Pacific (3) CPLT 380 - Introduction to Asian Literature (3) CPLT 382T - Topics in Asian Literature (3) GEOG 340 - Asia (3) HCOM 492T - Proseminar in Speech Communication (3) HIST 463C - Themes in Korean History (3) HIST 464B - History of Southeast Asia, 1800-present (3) HIST 465A - History of India (3) HIST 465B - History of India (3) PHIL 350 - Asian Philosophy (3) PHIL 352 - Philosophy of Asian Martial Arts (3) POSC 434 - The Asia-Pacific in World Affairs (3) RLST 270T - Introduction to the Asian Religions (3) RLST 354T - Topics in Buddhism (3) Community Research//Engagement Requirement (3 units) All Asian American Studies majors must complete ASAM 495 , ASAM 496 or ASAM 499 .
