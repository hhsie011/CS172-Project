They interact with and learn from students of diverse backgrounds, expanding both their worldview and their social networks. They take on many kinds of leadership positions that develop their decision-making and time management skills. And they expand their cognitive abilities through courses and projects that make connections across the disciplines. At its best, a Brown education produces a person who is not just smart but truly interested in other people, comfortable with the unknown, and above all resilient.
