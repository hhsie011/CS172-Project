Rutgers Experience Rutgers Biomedical and Health Sciences (RBHS) Rutgers Biomedical and Health Sciences Leading the Way in Academic Health Care Rutgers University is an academic health leader that is shaping the future of human health, pioneering breakthroughs, and integrating care to keep the world well. Through the activities of Rutgers Biomedical and Health Sciences (RBHS), Rutgers stands as one of America’s largest and most comprehensive university-based centers for studying and improving human health and health care. Rutgers takes an integrated approach to educating students, providing clinical care, and conducting research with the goal of improving human health. $450 Million Spent annually on life sciences research and development 1,500+ Full-time faculty 470+ Clinical trials underway 6,700+ Students enrolled in RBHS programs Discover Rutgers Biomedical Health Sciences Health Education at Rutgers The schools of Rutgers Biomedical and Health Sciences comprise New Jersey's largest and most influential constellation of academic institutions devoted to educating the full complement of health care professionals. More than 600 doctoral degrees are awarded each year by RBHS schools, from MD and DMD to doctor of physical therapy and PhD. Hundreds more master's level degrees are awarded annually in diverse fields including biomedical informatics, clinical nutrition, and nursing. Research Through basic, translational, and clinical research—spanning the life sciences, physical sciences, and social sciences—Rutgers Biomedical and Health Sciences drives education, medical breakthroughs, and economic development, while improving lives at home and in the global community. Collaboration and Innovation Rutgers is home to a vibrant research community that advances knowledge and makes new discoveries. Rutgers leverages its size to tap into a vast network of research experts and top facilities. Patient Care and Clinical Health As New Jersey’s largest academic health care provider, Rutgers leads by emphasizing approaches to health and wellness that fully engage patients. Rutgers patients receive care from top physicians and health professionals, including dentists, nurses, psychologists, pharmacists, and other allied health providers—whose observations and expertise contribute to a collaborative approach to patient care. So many viewpoints—together with opportunities to improve on these perspectives—ensures that Rutgers puts patients first.
