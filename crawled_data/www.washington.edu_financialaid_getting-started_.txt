contribution Net price calculator Student budgets Prior year budgets Husky Promise Eligibility Most of the financial aid programs we offer do have some common eligibility rules. To qualify for aid you must meet all of the following conditions. Learn more How need is determined There is no “income cutoff” or other simple method of determining whether you will qualify for need-based financial aid. If you think you need help paying for college, we encourage you to apply. Learn more Expected family contribution All college students are expected to contribute towards their education costs. How much you and your family will be expected to contribute depends on your financial situation — your Expected Family Contribution (EFC). Learn more Net price calculator The Net Price Calculator can be used to help estimate the types and amount of financial aid undergraduate students may receive at the Seattle, Bothell or Tacoma campuses. Learn more Student budgets Your student budget is the amount of money we estimate you will need for tuition, books, and living expenses for the 9-month academic year. Learn more Husky Promise Husky Promise is our guarantee to Washington state students that we will not let financial challenges stand in the way of discovering their potential or achieving a UW degree. Federal School Code Our Title IV federal school code is 003798. This code is used for Seattle, Bothell and Tacoma campuses. About us Our mission is to provide educational access and opportunity for all students. We promote participation in higher education by providing programs of assistance to help bridge the gap between the cost of attending the University and the family’s own resources. We believe students from all economic backgrounds should have the opportunity to attend the University of Washington. Finances should not be a barrier to getting a world-class education.
