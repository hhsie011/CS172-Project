EuDML: The European Digital Mathematics Library The purpose of the library is to provide a Digital Mathematics Library (DML) for the worldwide scientific community as a public service with the aim to help scientists locate the information that is distributed in various digital repositories, discover information related to their work in optimal way and be exhaustive and comprehensive in the field of mathematics. Mathematics at arXiv.org Mathematics at JSTOR Mathematics Subject Classification MathSciNet Searchable database of reviews, abstracts and bibliographic information for much of the mathematical sciences literature. MathSciNet tutorial webinar In this recorded webinar, Michael Jones provides an overview of MathSciNet. Project Euclid Project Euclid offers access to a growing platform of high-quality, peer-reviewed journals, monographs, and conference proceedings in the fields of theoretical and applied mathematics and statistics. SIAM Digital Library zbMATH zbMATH Open (formerly known as Zentralblatt MATH) is the world's most comprehensive and longest-running abstracting and reviewing service in pure and applied mathematics. It is edited by the European Mathematical Society (EMS), the Heidelberg Academy of Sciences and Humanities, and FIZ Karlsruhe. eBooks American Mathematical Society (AMS) Books Online Click on "My Holdings" to determine what Dartmouth has access to. AMS Open Math Notes A repository of freely downloadable mathematical works in progress hosted by the American Mathematical Society as a service to researchers, teachers and students. These draft works include course notes, textbooks, and research expositions in progress. They have not been published elsewhere, and, as works in progress, are subject to significant revision. Visitors are encouraged to download and use these materials as teaching and research aids, and to send constructive comments and suggestions to the authors. Lecture Notes in Mathematics SIAM eBooks Synthesis Lectures on Mathematics and Statistics The basic component of the library is a 50- to 100-page electronic book that synthesizes an important research or development topic, authored by a prominent contributor to the field. Theorem Provers Lean and its Mathematical Library The Lean mathematical library, mathlib, is a community-driven effort to build a unified library of mathematics formalized in the Lean proof assistant. The library also contains definitions useful for programming. Coq Coq provides interactive proof methods, decision and semi-decision algorithms, and a tactic language for letting the user define its own proof methods.
