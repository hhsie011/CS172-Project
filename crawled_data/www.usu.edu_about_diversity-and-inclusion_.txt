State University is committed to cultivating an environment of respectful engagement and respectful discourse at every level of our society. As Aggies, we commit to USU’s Principles of Community — our institutional Aggie pledge to Diversity, Human Dignity and Social Responsibility. National Ranking for Contributions to Public Good - Washington Monthly, 2020 10 Ranking in Recruiting and Graduating First-Generation and Low-Income Students (Social Mobility) - Washington Monthly, 2020 4 Our Commitment Diversity and inclusion have been priorities of USU — especially since President Cockett assumed office in 2017. From creation of inclusive environments and centers, like the Latinx Cultural Center and the Center for Intersectional Gender Studies and Research, to funding and staff increases for USU’s Inclusion Center, the Veterans Resource Center, and the Office of Equity, USU is dedicated to making sure the campus community is welcoming for all. USU is a community leader in these efforts and provides outreach through events like the Inclusive Excellence Symposium, the Community Diversity Dinner, the Utah Women & Leadership Project, and other initiatives under Aggies Think, Care, Act.” Office and Organizations Office of Equity Inclusion Center Interfaith Initiative Center for Intersectional Gender Studies and Research Latinx Cultural Center Office of Global Engagement Aggies Think, Care, Act Initiative Resources Disability Resource Center Free Speech at USU Inclusive Excellence Symposium Utah Women & Leadership Project Veterans Resource Center Campus Diversity Survey Want to learn more about USU?
