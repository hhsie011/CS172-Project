The University of Nevada, Reno offers a variety of living options for students who choose to live on campus. Take a virtual tour of our residence halls We're excited to offer virtual tours of our residence halls. Please select the residence hall of your choice to explore floor plans, view room dimensions and take an interactive look inside the residence hall. Undergraduate housing options Argenta Hall Number of residents: 750 Price: $ Room type: Triples Room configuration: Door opens to hallway Bathroom: Private, in room Open to: Freshmen and above Special fact: The Downunder dining commons is on the first floor. Canada Hall Number of residents: 223 Price: $$ Room type: Doubles Room configuration: Suite-style Bathroom: Shared, in suite Open to: New transfer students and current students, sophomores and above Special fact: Each suite has a small kitchen and living room. Great Basin Hall Number of residents: 400 Price: $$$ Room types: Singles, Doubles Room configuration: Suite-style Bathroom: Shared, in suite Open to: Freshmen and above Special fact: Great Basin Hall is home to several identity-based Living Learning Communities. Juniper Hall Number of residents: 78 Price: $-$$$ Room types: Singles Room configuration: Suite-style Bathroom: Communal Open to: Freshmen and above Special fact: Most of the building are single rooms with views of Manzanita Lake. Manzanita Hall Number of residents: 104 Price: $-$$$ Room types: Singles, Doubles Room configuration: Room opens to hallway Bathroom: Communal Open to: Freshmen and above Special fact: Built in 1896 and renovated in 2019, residents feel the history of this building. The Nevada Living Learning Community Number of residents: 320 Price: $$$ Room types: Singles, Doubles Room configuration: Suite-style Bathroom: Shared, in suite Open to: Freshmen and above Special fact: Small and large group study and social lounges are available throughout the building. Nye Hall Number of residents: 555 Price: $ Room type: Doubles Room configuration: Room opens to hallway Bathroom: Communal Open to: Freshmen and above Special fact: Recently renovated in 2020, it traditionally promotes a strong sense of community among its residents. Peavine Hall Number of residents: 410 Price: $$$ Room types: Doubles Room configuration: Suite-style Bathroom: Shared, in suite Open to: Freshmen and above Special fact: Peavine Hall is an LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) Gold certified building. Sierra Hall Number of residents: 297 Price: $$-$$$ Room types: Singles, Doubles, Triples, Quads Room configuration: Room opens to hallway Bathroom: Private, in room Open to: Freshmen and above Special fact: The first floor offers a recreation room and TV lounge. Housing for graduate/professional students, faculty & staff One and two bedroom apartments are available for graduate students, faculty and staff at Ponderosa Village, located on the University of Nevada, Reno campus.
