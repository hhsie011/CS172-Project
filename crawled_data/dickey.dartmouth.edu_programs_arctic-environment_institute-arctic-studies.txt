CloseGo Search Institute of Arctic Studies The Institute of Arctic Studies (IAS) at the Dickey Center is Dartmouth's crossroads for multidisciplinary Arctic scholarship and global policy dialogs that center inclusion, justice, equity, and Indigenous Knowledge in solutions to Arctic and global challenges. PreviousNext About the Institute of Arctic Studies Since its founding in 1989, the Institute of Arctic Studies has prioritized an holistic approach to scholarship and policy, recognizing the connected environmental, cultural, and political dimensions of the North and the immediate impacts of rapid climate change. IAS offers undergraduate and graduate students guidance on a diversity of educational and research opportunities as well as access to policy seminars and public events.Read More Research Dartmouth faculty and students are involved in diverse and interdisciplinary polar research on a diversity of topics ranging from glacial dynamics and ecosystem change to sustainable development, health equity, fisheries governance, Indigenous rights, and gender equality, to name just a few.Read More Courses Dartmouth offers many undergraduate and graduate courses encompassing issues of the environment, polar science diplomacy, Arctic Indigenous Knowledge systems, inclusive governance, and the consequences of rapid environmental change on the North and our planet.Read More Partners The Institute of Arctic Studies works with a diversity of U.S. and international organizations on scientific research and policy development, hosting conferences and workshops, and creating inclusive knowledge networks for multidisciplinary, informed solutions.Read More JSEP The Institute of Arctic Studies at Dartmouth leads a project funded by the National Science Foundation for high school students focused on polar science education and outreach in Greenland, the Joint Science Education Project, or JSEP.Read More JASE The Institute of Arctic Studies at Dartmouth leads a project funded by the National Science Foundation for youth STEM education in Antarctica, the Joint Antarctic Science Expedition, or JASE.Read More Arctic & The Environment Institute of Arctic Studies About Research
