How to change your password Gather all of your mobile devices. If you have a desktop iMac, we recommend that you change your password using System Preferences on your assigned Campus iMac. To change your password use the CSUF Employee: Change Password site. On mobile devices, wait for password prompts. To change your administrative account password, use Instructions on how to change password site Update Security Questions We strongly encourage you to update your password security questions so that you can reset your password if you forget. Log into the Campus Portal In the menu you will see a Settings link which will open your Profile Settings. Under the Employee Account Settings there is an option for Password Reset Security Questions. Press the Edit link at the end of that option and it will display the current security questions for you to change. Password Requirements Should be between 12 and 20 characters Contain 3 of these 4 requirements: Uppercase letters Lowercase letters Numbers Symbols: ! @ $ % & * - _ + . No part of your name can be used You cannot use your last 24 campus passwords Campus Password Guides Change Password Quick Guide Password Guidelines Forgot Your Password? Update Password on Mobile Devices After you have changed your campus password, you will need to update the email and WiFi passwords on any mobile devices that are connected to your campus account. So be sure to have them close by. Find Out How! Do I Need To Change My Password? You will need to change your password if you are: Faculty and Staff Student Employee "ZZ accounts" (including Graduate Assistants) Volunteers Teaching Associates Contractor Visiting Scholar Administrative Accounts (conference rooms, labs, and department accounts) Emeriti Contact the IT Help Desk at 657-278-7777 if you are unsure if your account requires a password reset.
