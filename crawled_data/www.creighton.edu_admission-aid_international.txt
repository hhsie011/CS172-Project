Creighton students come from countries across the globe, from China to the Dominican Republic, Nigeria to Russia. Our diverse community celebrates the talents, ideas, and stories that our international students bring to the table, and we want you to join us. Pack your bags—our faculty, staff, and students will make you feel right at home here. Make sure to connect with us on social media, Whatsapp, WeChat, and Skype! How to Apply Read the FAQs I-20 and Visa Information Diversity: The sum of us. Diversity means a lot of things: It’s about where you came from, and your culture. It’s about your ideas and one-of-a-kind perspective. It’s about the stories and experiences you share with others. When you’re part of a diverse community, everybody is better for it. At Creighton, we’re committed to building a diverse community—and ensuring that all our students feel welcome and safe. Committed to Diversity, Equity and Inclusion Creighton Intercultural Center Image Go global. If you want to learn about the world’s most pressing issues and the many cultures found in Omaha, join our Global Ambassador Program. You’ll join other freshmen from the U.S. and beyond, as you take trips to community gardens, go hiking and participate in multicultural celebrations, such as the Lunar New Year. Image Scholarships & Financial Aid When you graduate from Creighton, you’ll find a world of opportunities in front of you. That’s one reason why U.S. News & World Report considers us a Best Value School. But we’re also affordable. As an international student, you’re eligible for merit-based scholarships and an interest-free payment plan. Learn About Scholarships and Financial Aid Located in a City Loaded with Opportunities to Succeed There’s no place quite like Omaha. Accessible from all major airports, Omaha is a vibrant, metropolitan city with a hard-working, hospitable spirit. Four Fortune 500 company world headquarters are located less than 2 miles from campus. Omaha is also growing rapidly, which translates into an abundance of networking and internship opportunities for our students. In fact, we have more internships than we have students to fill them.
