School of Music The Stage is Yours Mastery of craft is just the beginning. At YSM, you have the freedom to dream big and the space to carve your own path. Study Music at YSM Here, at the only school of music in the Ivy League, students engage their curiosities and discover their voice. YSM maintains a highly selective admission process, each year admitting approximately 100 students who come from prestigious American and international conservatories and universities to study with Yale’s distinguished faculty. A full tuition award and living stipend is given to all students who are admitted to the School. Music is everywhere at Yale. Students perform and attend concerts in the University’s historic concert halls and world-class art galleries and libraries, collaborate meaningfully with peers across disciplines, and engage with New Haven’s creative community. With unsurpassed resources and state-of-the-art facilities, Yale helps students activate their imaginations and design their promising futures. What we hope for our students is an experience that allows them to discover their own musical voice. Robert Blocker, Henry and Lucy Moses Dean of Music See what YSM has to offer The Yale School of Music is one of 12 graduate-professional schools on the Yale University campus, where students are immersed in an intellectually nourishing community of scholars. Learn More Students, Faculty + Alumni Winners of the 2022 Woolsey Hall Concerto Competition announced This year’s Woolsey Hall Concerto Competition, which took place on April 2, yielded three winners: oboist Soo Min Ha ’21MM ’22MMA, who performed Vaughan Williams’ Oboe Concerto in A minor, IRV 50; violinist Anna Lee ’22MMA, who performed Beethoven’s Violin Concerto in D major, Op. 61; and cellist Jakob Taylor ’22MMA, who performed Prokofiev’s Sinfonia concertante, Op. 125. Each competition winner will perform as a soloist with the Yale Philharmonia during the 2022-2023 season.
