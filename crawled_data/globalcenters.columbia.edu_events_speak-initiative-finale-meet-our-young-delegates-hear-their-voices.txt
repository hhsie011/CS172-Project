Our Young Delegates, Hear Their Voices Events Past Event “Speak Up” Initiative Finale: Meet Our Young Delegates, Hear Their Voices Share Add to Calendar: Google Yahoo July 21, 2020 7:00 PM - 10:00 PM Online Please join us for the final webinar of "Speak Up" Initiative and listen to the voices of young people! Thirty-one outstanding delegates will use their critical thinking skills and youth leadership to support sustainability and many living creatures who cannot speak for themselves. About "Speak Up" As the world is experiencing unprecedented challenges, Columbia Global Centers | Beijing, in partnership with China Youth Climate Action Network (CYCAN), proudly launched the “Speak Up” Initiative aimed at youth aged 18 to 26 who are interested in biodiversity issues, sustainable development, and youth leadership to explore the role of youth in protecting biodiversity and promoting the sustainable development of human society. In 2020, we received applications from more than 200 enthusiastic and talented young men and women from the universities in China, U.S., U.K., Canada, Mexico, and Australia. Six Columbia students from Columbia College, Barnard College, Columbia Engineering, Columbia SIPA, and Teachers College were selected to share their voices and continue their efforts to make a powerful difference to a greener living. All delegates will attend the 12th International Youth Summit on Energy and Climate Change. Agenda 7:00 - 7:10 p.m. Opening remarks 7:10 - 8:10 p.m. Speech by each delegate Topic: How to view the relationship between social and economic development and wildlife habitat protection? 8:10 - 9:10 p.m. Impromptu discussion Theme: The causes of biodiversity loss and how to promote more sustainable production and consumption patterns through youth leadership. 9:10 - 9:30 p.m. Presentations by chief delegates 9:30 - 9:45 p.m. Q&A session between delegates and judges 9:45 - 10:00 p.m. Awards ceremony Awards: Best Delegate, Best Essay Writing, Outstanding Delegates About the Judges Dr. Binbin LI is the Assistant Professor of Environmental Sciences of the Environmental Research Center at Duke Kunshan University. She holds a secondary appointment with Nicholas School of the Environment at Duke University. Her research focuses on loss of biodiversity, endangered and endemic species conservation such as giant pandas, priority setting and management of protected areas, and promotion of innovative technology, markets and policies to solve conservation problems and local community development. Dr. Siu-tai TSIM is the Associate Professor and Programme Director of Environmental Science and Course Coordinator of Environmental Awareness in Whole Person Education Office in BNU-HKBU United International College, Zhuhai, China. His research interests includes: species conservation and management, habitat fragmentation, seagrass ecology etc.; teaching subjects include: conservation ecology, resources and the environment, sustainable environmental management, green business management, and the sixth mass biodiversity extinction etc. Reminders This webinar will be livestreamed at 7:00 p.m. in Beijing Time (7:00 a.m. in NY Time) and conducted in English.
