The Warren Alpert Medical School students are empowered to tailor their education to their interests and inspirations. Through research, service—and leisure, too—students are able to bring their diverse backgrounds to bear on their medical school experience. Brown has a longstanding commitment to creating and fostering a diverse, inclusive, and culturally competent environment for students, faculty, and trainees; our Office of Diversity and Multicultural Affairs supports that mission and also promotes social responsibility, active engagement, and the well being of our communities in the pursuit of health equity. Check out the medical school's Life in Providence page to see what life is like for our students and what Rhode Island has to offer. Life in Providence Rhode Island's capital is one of New England's most populous cities and holds no shortage of exciting and unique options for exploration and discovery. Student Life The Warren Alpert Medical School offers both the small, tight-knit community as well as the opportunity and activity of an Ivy League School in an exciting city.
