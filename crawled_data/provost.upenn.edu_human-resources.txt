Please see the pages on the left for information on various human resources related topics. If you have any questions, please contact your Human Resources Director. New Position or Additional Pay Requests If a department would like to add a new permanent position (full-time or part-time that is not filling an established position), or request an acting rate or additional pay for a staff member, the request must be submitted through the New Position or Additional Pay Request Process. Please visit our Position (New and Reclass) and Compensation Requests page (PennKey Required) for details and to submit your request. Exemption Requests from PAA Talent Acquisition Process To fill any Full Time or Part Time Regular Staff positions outside of the Office of Provost Administrative Affairs Talent Acquisition Process, you must complete this form. Please ensure you provide responses to all of the questions to support the exemption request. Your request will be routed for review, and an email will be sent to the person submitting the request when review is complete. Important Links Workday@Penn will transform how we deliver human capital management-related services, such as staff recruitment, faculty and staff benefits administration, payroll, time management, and more. Penn Employee Solution Center is available to help with your HR/payroll questions. Knowledgeable Solution Center Specialists are available Monday through Friday, during business hours, to answer your questions about retirment, health benefits, tuition benefits, and more. Onboard@Penn provides consistent, efficient, and friendly first-day services for new hires. It is part of Penn’s initiative to enhance our people-centered services. myHR is a bi-weekly newsletter sent out by Penn's Division of Human Resources that shows you how to make the most of the benefits, programs, and services they provide. Penn's Division of Human Resources is the Central HR office for the University. The Manager's Toolkit provides information and resources you need to effectively manage staff at all stages of their career. Careers@Penn is where you can find new opportunities as your career develops—from your first job out of school to your first leadership role, and all points in between.
