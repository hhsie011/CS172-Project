MD-ScM Combined Degree | Primary Care–Population Medicine Program The Primary Care–Population Medicine Program is a four-year curriculum that prepares students for a career in medicine while providing comprehensive, longitudinal training in population medicine. The program includes a Longitudinal Integrated Clerkship (LIC) in the third year and clinical applications of ScM coursework. MD/PhD Program Students interested in the MD/PhD Program may apply as PLME undergraduates or as current medical students to any of the graduate programs in the Division of Biology and Medicine. Graduate work typically begins after Year 2 or Year 3 at The Warren Alpert Medical School. MD/PhD students typically take a hiatus from their medical studies of 3 to 5 years to complete the requirements for their doctorate. MD/MPH Program The 5-year MD/MPH Program is open to all medical students. The Brown School of Public Health offers seven master’s degree tracks: Generalist, Behavioral and Social Sciences, Biostatistics, Environmental Health, Epidemiology, Global Health, and Health Services. Though the sequencing of coursework is flexible, the fifth year is required to complete the MPH thesis.
