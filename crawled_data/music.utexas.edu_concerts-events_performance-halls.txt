Musician Pre-College Programs Piano Project Contact Parent Resources Faculty & Staff Schedule Application FAQ String Project Musical Lives Performance Halls Homer Rainey Hall The Butler School has four performance halls across the university campus. Bates Recital Hall 2406 Robert Dedman Drive Located on the third floor of the music building, Bates Recital Hall is the main venue for our large ensembles, from orchestras and big bands, to chamber ensembles and solo recitals. Bates is also home to the school's Visser-Rowland organ, one of the largest tracker organs in the country. Located in Music Recital Hall (MRH) Seats 652 12 wheelchair and companion chair spaces, located on a platform built out from the stage and in the rear of the hall Directions & Parking Recital Studio 2406 Robert Dedman Drive Located inside the music building, the Recital Studio doubles as a lecture hall and chamber performance space, hosting student recitals, chamber groups, master classes and guest lectures. It also hosts many smaller ensemble concerts and guest recitals. Music Recital Hall (MRH) room 2.608 Seats 175 No wheelchair seating is available Directions & Parking Jessen Auditorium 201 W. 21st St. Located across campus from the Butler School in Homer Rainey Hall, near the UT tower and the Littlefield Fountain, Jessen Auditorium is the school's original performance hall. With seating for 325 and stunning acoustics, Jessen is an ideal hall for chamber music and solo recitals. Homer Rainey Hall (HRH) room 2.104 Seats 325 Wheelchair accessible in the front or rear of the hall Directions & Parking McCullough Theatre 2375 Robert Dedman Drive Operated by Texas Performing Arts, and conveniently located just south of the Butler School, McCullough Theatre is home to the Butler Opera Center.
