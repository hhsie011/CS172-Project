Princeton has been at the forefront of computing since Alan Turing, Alonzo Church and John von Neumann were among its residents. Our department is home to 48 tenure-track and 12 teaching faculty, with strong groups in theory, networks/systems, vision/graphics, architecture/compilers, programming languages, security/policy, machine learning, natural language processing, human-computer interaction, robotics, and computational biology.
