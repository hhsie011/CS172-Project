Dr. Bourjeily's clinical expertise is in pulmonary disease and pregnancy, and her research focuses on understanding sleep disordered breathing in pregnant women and the impact of the condition on cardiovascular and metabolic outcomes of pregnancy, and its fetal and neonatal implications. Dr Bourjeily's research has advanced the field of pulmonary disease and sleep in pregnancy. She has served in leadership roles at numerous national and international societies, has received many national research and leadership awards, and has been invited speaker at national and international venues. Some of her research has focused on the pregnancy, obstetric and fetal outcomes of sleep disordered breathing in pregnancy as well as understanding predictors of symptoms of SDB. Research Statement Dr. Bourjeily's laboratory's goal is to improve the knowledge and the care of pregnant women with medical disorders through research and education. Dr. Bourjeily's team has worked with trainees at the undergraduate, graduate, and post-doctoral levels, with trainee's projects resulting in many national and international presentations by trainees as well as pulications. Funded Research Dr Bourjeily has received multiple foundations grants, including two grants by Chest Foundation of the American College of Chest Physicians. She is currently funded by NICHD, NHLBI and NCCIH Scholarly Work Dr Bourjeily has served as co-edtor of a textbook on pulmonary problems in pregnancy, guest editor on similar topics for leading journals, and serves as associate editor or on editorial boards on many journals.
