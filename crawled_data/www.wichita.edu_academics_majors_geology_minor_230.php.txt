Admission requirements Inside the Program Field study gives Shocker students practical experience Geology students can gain hands-on expereince before graduation. Eighteen undergraduates from the Geology Department spent three weeks in Wyoming as part of Geology Field Camp, an intensive, four-week capstone course in the Bighorn Basin and Yellowstone region. Students had the opportunity to combine classroom lessons with field experience in sedimentology, mineralogy and structural geology. They also learned how to map and interpret rocks, which involved critical thinking and communication skills. Curriculum To complete the 15-credit-hour geology minor, you'll choose from two basic courses: Earth Science and the Environment (with lab) or General Geology. Then, you'll select from at least 11 additional credit hours of geology courses, based on your interests.
