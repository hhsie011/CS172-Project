Texas Tech University Directory Raiderlink A-Z Index Texas Tech Today Menu Search TTU Texas Tech Today Students to Participate in Spring 2022 Commencement Katelynn Horton May 13, 2022 Texas Tech will hold its spring commencement ceremonies May 13-14 at United Supermarkets Arena. Texas Tech University will celebrate its largest graduating class in university history at its spring commencement ceremonies Friday (May 13) and Saturday (May 14). Approximately 4,700 undergraduate and graduate students will receive their diplomas and 128 School of Law students will be hooded. Ceremonies can be viewed online via live stream. Each ceremony will be held at United Supermarkets Arena, 1701 Indiana Ave. The schedule is as follows: 2 p.m. Friday (May 13) College of Arts & Sciences 7:30 p.m. Friday (May 13) Graduate School 8 a.m. Saturday (May 14) Edward E. Whitacre Jr. College of Engineering College of Architecture University Programs College of Education Noon Saturday (May 14) Davis College of Agricultural Sciences & Natural Resources College of Human Sciences College of Media & Communication 3:30 p.m. Saturday (May 14) Jerry S. Rawls College of Business J.T. & Margaret Talkington College of Visual & Performing Arts (TCVPA) Honors College 7 p.m. Saturday (May 14) School of Law The Clear Bag Policy is in effect for all commencement ceremonies. For more information about commencement, including maps and parking, visit the commencement web page. Research and enrollment numbers are at record levels, which cement Texas Tech's commitment to attracting and retaining quality students. In fall 2020, the university achieved a goal more than a decade in the making, reaching a total student population of more than 40,000. In 2018, the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education again placed Texas Tech among its top doctoral universities in the nation in the “Very High Research Activity” category. Texas Tech is one of 94 public institutions nationally and 131 overall to achieve this prestigious recognition. Quality students need top-notch faculty. Texas Tech is home to a diverse, highly revered pool of educators who excel in teaching, research and service. The university strives to foster an environment that celebrates student accomplishment above all else. Texas Tech is large enough to provide the best in facilities and academics but prides itself on being able to focus on each student individually.
