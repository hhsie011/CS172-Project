We offer both virtual and in-person Clemson Honors prospective student meeting options. How to Request a Meeting Please use the online meeting form to request a meeting with the Clemson University Honors College. The online meeting form must be completed at least three days prior to your requested date. During this meeting, you will speak with an Honors staff member to learn more about the Honors experience. A meeting with Honors typically lasts 30 minutes to 1 hour. In-person meetings are scheduled on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays at 9:45 a.m. (EST) in the Honors Center. Meetings are not offered on weekends and University holidays. You will receive a confirmation email after you schedule your visit, as well as email reminders 24 hours and 1 hour before your scheduled visit. If you have any questions prior to your visit, please contact Megan Wong, Honors Advisor and Recruitment Coordinator, and/or Jenna Perry, the Honors Student Ambassador Coordinator. Can't Make a Scheduled Meeting? No worries; we understand that you have a busy schedule! Both virtual and in-person private appointments are available upon request, subject to availability. Please email Megan Wong, Honors Advisor and Recruitment Coordinator, to schedule an appointment. Connect With a Current Honors Student You are welcome to email our Honors Student Ambassadors to ask questions about the student experience! Explore our Honors Student Ambassadors, including viewing their individual profiles, to learn about their major, interests, and favorite things about Clemson and Honors! Please allow 2-3 business days for a reply. An extended response timeframe may be expected over student holidays. Campus Visits If you are interested in scheduling a campus visit, consider a virtual visit of Clemson University, where student tour guides will give you an overview of the University as well as the ins and outs of campus life. Limited capacity in-person campus visits are also being offered. Self-Guided Virtual Tours Interested in exploring academic and campus life on Clemson in the comfort of your own home and at your convenience? Consider a self-guided virtual tour.
