It is as easy as 1-2-3. 1. Apply for admission When you apply to OSU, you're automatically applying for scholarships as well. OSU application Transfer scholarships 2. Complete the FAFSA The Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) opens October 1 for the following school year. OSU's school code is 003170. Certain funding is limited and earliest applicants receive priority. FAFSA website Step-by-step guide 3. Stay connected Some items need follow-up action from you for your aid to be processed. Make sure you check for updates in these places. Activate your O-Key account Check your financial aid status Check your OSU email account Contact us if you need help Loan history There are loan limits for undergraduate and graduate students. You can view your borrowing history at all schools at studentloans.gov. Loans offered at OSU Satisfactory Academic Progress Our office is required by federal regulations to determine whether you are progressing toward your degree requirements. Check out our policy for information on GPA, Completion Rate and Maximum Hour requirements. Scholarship deadlines Fall-entering students Feb. 1 for priority scholarship consideration; May 1 is College Decision Date. Some scholarships must be accepted by this date or they may be cancelled. August 19 is the last day to submit application materials, such as updated test scores or transcripts for final scholarship consideration. Spring-entering students Oct. 15 for priority scholarship consideration; the Friday before spring classes begin is the final scholarship deadline.
