Credit: © Harvard Theatre Collection, Houghton Library Amiri Baraka and "Dutchman" cast, 1964 Credit: Alix Jeffry © Harvard Theatre Collection Morgan Freeman, 1968 Credit: Alix Jeffry © Harvard Theatre Collection Shirley Verrett, 1974 Credit: Alix Jeffry © Harvard Theatre Collection Through Friday, Jul 22, 2022 Open to the public Location Amy Lowell Room, 2nd Floor Houghton Library Harvard Yard Cambridge, MA 02138 See All Hours Directions to Houghton Library At the height of the civil rights movement, New York Times photographer Alix Jeffry captured the premieres of two revolutionary plays by Adrienne Kennedy and Amiri Baraka. Drawing on Jeffry’s archive of over sixty thousand images, In Black and White explores the intersection of the arts and activism at a pivotal moment in Black theater history. The exhibition features original photographs from Dutchman and Funnyhouse of a Negro that Jeffry took during rehearsals and live performances in 1964. In addition to production photos, portraits by Jeffry of pioneering Black artists of the 1960s and ‘70s will be on view in Houghton's ground floor gallery. Notable subjects include Harry Belafonte Morgan Freeman James Earl Jones Leontyne Price Shirley Verrett This exhibition was curated by Dale Stinchcomb, Assistant Curator of the Harvard Theatre Collection, from the Alix Jeffry Archive, a gift of Frederick R. Koch and Jeffry’s partner, Mary Alice Morris. Download the exhibition brochure. Planning to visit? In Black and White is on view March 21 – July 22, 2022. The exhibition is open to all visitors with proof of vaccination and booster; face coverings are recommended.
