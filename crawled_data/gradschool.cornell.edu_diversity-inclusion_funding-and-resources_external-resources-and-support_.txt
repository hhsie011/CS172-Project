Below is a list of resources available through outside organizations. See Cornell-specific resources. Academic Diversity Search Academic Diversity Search, Inc. (ADS) is a nationwide employment resource specializing in connecting women and minorities with academic institutions that truly value diversity. American Association of University Professors The American Association for University Professors assists in assuring the freedom and standards of teaching in higher education and has listings of committees devoted to underrepresented faculty issues, articles, and news on diversifying campuses. American Association of University Women The American Association of University Women is a membership-based society promoting equal education for women and girls and professional educators. American Council on Education The American Council on Education unites 1800 colleges and universities with the objective of promoting equal opportunity within the higher education system. American Educational Research Association The American Educational Research Association (AERA), a national research society, strives to advance knowledge about education, to encourage scholarly inquiry related to education, and to promote the use of research to improve education and serve the public good. Association of American Colleges and Universities DiversityWeb is a project of AAC&U’s Office of Diversity, Equity, and Global Initiatives (ODEGI). Central to the office’s mission is the belief that diversity and global knowledge are essential elements of any effort to foster civic engagement among today’s college students. To support those goals, the office helps colleges and universities establish diversity as a comprehensive institutional commitment and educational priority. Black Doctoral Network The Black Doctoral Network (BDN) is a multidisciplinary clearinghouse for black and Latino scholars and professionals in the social sciences, STEM, and humanities. Center for Equal Opportunity The CEO addresses issues of equality across all institutions receiving government funds. Center for the Integration of Research, Teaching, and Learning The Center for the Integration of Research, Teaching, and Learning (CIRTL) maintains a discussion group for academic career and job postings. Diverse Issues in Higher Education Diverse seeks to be a catalyst for change, and our ultimate objective is to contribute to the building of educational, cultural, social and economic structures that will allow every individual to achieve his or her full human potential and contribute to the greater good of the community and the nation. FairTest The National Center for Fair & Open Testing (FairTest) works to end the misuses and flaws of standardized testing and to ensure that evaluation of students, teachers and schools is fair, open, valid, and educationally beneficial. Ford Fellowship Program Through its fellowship programs, the Ford Foundation seeks to increase the diversity of the nation’s college and university faculties by increasing their ethnic and racial diversity, to maximize the educational benefits of diversity, and to increase the number of professors who can and will use diversity as a resource for enriching the education of all students. Guide for Undocumented Students on Fellowships and Scholarships Up-to-date information about scholarships available for immigrant students that don’t have U.S. citizenship or legal permanent residency. Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities The Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities (HACU) which represents 450 colleges and universities committed to the Hispanic higher education success in the U.S., Puerto Rico, Latin America, Spain, and Portugal. Hispanic Outlook in Higher Education A biweekly publication devoted to constructive discussion of Hispanic and non-Hispanic issues in higher education. Immigrants Rising Immigrants Rising empowers undocumented young people to achieve educational and career goals through personal, institutional, and policy transformation. Institute for Broadening Participation The Institute for Broadening Participation created an online mentoring manual with the goal of highlighting the value of mentoring and to help students, faculty, and administrators be better mentees and become better mentors within the science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) fields, especially on the mentoring process for underrepresented students. Low-Income Student Guide This guide from AccreditedOnlineSchools.org details how universities are supporting low-income students and the opportunities available on and off campuses. Making College Affordable: An Online Guide for Hispanic Students While this guide contains information valuable to all current and future college students, it focuses on data and resources specific to Hispanic students looking to further their education. Mexican Legal Defense and Educational Fund Founded in 1968 in San Antonio, Texas, the Mexican American Legal Defense and Educational Fund (MALDEF) is the leading nonprofit Latino litigation, advocacy, and educational outreach institution in the United States. MinorityPostdoc.org MinorityPostdoc.org feature articles, resources, and events about career advice, professional development, jobs, funding, fellowships, mentoring, and diversity issues. National Association for Equal Opportunity in Higher Education The National Association for Equal Opportunity in Higher Education promotes the advancement of minority students and minority educators. National Center for Faculty Development and Diversity Cornell is an institutional member of the National Center for Faculty Development and Diversity, a nationally recognized, independent organization providing online career development and mentoring resources. The NCFDD provides members with access to tools to increase research and writing productivity and improve work-life balance. See instructions on how to set up your free individual membership. Preparing Future Faculty Cooperative program involving 43 doctoral institutions and other partners interested in the promotion of doctoral students pursuing faculty positions. The Black Collegian Career site/online journal targeting minority graduates seeking professional positions. UCLA GRAPES – Scholarship & Fellowship Database The Graduate School at the University of California, Los Angeles maintains one of the most extensive and up to date university databases on graduate school funding opportunities. Their database is called GRAPES and can be used to search for hundreds of funding opportunities. UNIGO An organization that provides students with access to well-organized details on thousands of funding opportunities for college and graduate studies. You can create a profile with Unigo that will help facilitate your search for scholarships for which you might be eligible to apply, or you can forgo creating a profile and explore information on funding focused on specific areas of study, interests, and/or identity groups. Land Acknowledgement Cornell University is located on the traditional homelands of the Gayogo̱hó꞉nǫ' (the Cayuga Nation). The Gayogo̱hó꞉nǫ' are members of the Haudenosaunee Confederacy, an alliance of six sovereign Nations with a historic and contemporary presence on this land. The Confederacy precedes the establishment of Cornell University, New York state, and the United States of America. We acknowledge the painful history of Gayogo̱hó꞉nǫ' dispossession, and honor the ongoing connection of Gayogo̱hó꞉nǫ' people, past and present, to these lands and waters. This land acknowledgment has been reviewed and approved by the traditional Gayogo̱hó꞉nǫ' leadership.
