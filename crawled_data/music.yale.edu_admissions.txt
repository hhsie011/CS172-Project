Degrees + Programs The Yale School of Music awards the following graduate-professional degrees: Master of Music (M.M.) Master of Musical Arts (M.M.A.) Doctor of Musical Arts (D.M.A.) The School also offers the following programs: Certificate in Performance Artist Diploma program, for performers holding a minimum of a high school diploma Bachelor of Arts/Master of Music (B.A./M.M.) program, offered in conjunction with Yale College Fellowship opportunities at the School include: String Quartet Fellowship Collaborative Piano Fellowship Key Admissions Dates September 15: Applications open December 1: Applications due January 14 (latest): Prescreening results emailed to all applicants February 19 – 27: Live auditions and DMA Exams by invitation only March 18: Admissions decisions sent by email April 15: Deadline for admitted students to respond to admission offers more about admissions Degrees + Programs learn more Toggle Complete information on YSM's degree and program offerings. Learn More Students, Faculty + Alumni Andy Akiho, Anne Leilehua Lanzilotti named Pulitzer Prize finalists Yale School of Music alums Andy Akiho ’11MM and Anne Leilehua Lanzilotti ’08MM were finalists for the 2022 Pulitzer Prize in Music. Learn More Students, Faculty + Alumni Winners of the 2022 Woolsey Hall Concerto Competition announced This year’s Woolsey Hall Concerto Competition, which took place on April 2, yielded three winners: oboist Soo Min Ha ’21MM ’22MMA, who performed Vaughan Williams’ Oboe Concerto in A minor, IRV 50; violinist Anna Lee ’22MMA, who performed Beethoven’s Violin Concerto in D major, Op. 61; and cellist Jakob Taylor ’22MMA, who performed Prokofiev’s Sinfonia concertante, Op. 125. Each competition winner will perform as a soloist with the Yale Philharmonia during the 2022-2023 season.
