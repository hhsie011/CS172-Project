Applying for Graduation To become a candidate for the Master of Arts, Master of Science, Master of Philosophy in Mathematics or Doctor of Philosophy degrees in a given term, a student must meet all degree and program requirements and complete an application for graduation. (You will need your PennKey and password to access the secure application website.) View the student checklists to start the process: PhD Graduation Checklist PhD Formatting Checklist Research Master's Graduation Checklist Graduate Group Coordinator Checklist Graduation Requirements and Deadlines The specific deadlines relevant to the graduation process are listed in the degree calendar. Graduate groups must submit completed and approved electronic forms to the Office of the Provost via Penn Graduate Forms, to record completion of requirements including: Language exams or requirements AM/MS Masters research and final exams PhD qualification evaluations and preliminary examinations Dissertation oral defense Acceptance of Dissertation View the checklist for Graduate Group Coordinators here. Before the deposit appointment, the PhD candidate must also: Format and paginate each section of the dissertation accurately, per the Dissertation manual Submit the final, committee-approved dissertation to ETD Administrator for approval Complete the following Surveys: Survey of Earned Doctorates PhD Exit survey Upload the completion certificates for both surveys to ETD Administrator under "Administrative Documents" Clear all bursar balances and registration/transcript holds View the PhD Student Graduation Checklist for more details. Before the deposit appointment, the Research Master's candidate must also: Format and paginate each section of the dissertation accurately, per the Master's Thesis Style Guide Submit the final, committee-approved dissertation to grad-degree@provost.upenn.edu for approval Clear all bursar balances and registration/transcript holds View the Research Master's Student Graduation Checklist for more details. Diploma Information Diplomas will be mailed 8-10 weeks after the conferral date by the Office of the University Secretary. For more information, please visit the Office of the Secretary's website. Submatriculation Submatriculants, enrolled in both undergraduate and graduate schools, follow special graduation procedures. Three months prior to the anticipated graduation, a submatriculant must declare candidacy for the Master's degree by completing undergraduate and graduate forms within their school.
