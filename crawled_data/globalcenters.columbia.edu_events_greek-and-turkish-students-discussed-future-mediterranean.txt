The session entailed a fascinating discussion on the future Turkish - Greek relations with Ipek Cem Taha, Volkan Vural, Soli Özel, Ionnis Grigoriades, Dimitrios Triantophyllou and Rana Zincir Celal as well as 15 Turkish and 15 Greek graduate students and faculty members from reputable universities in Turkey and Greece. The project aims to open the way for the future leaders of both countries to achieve a better understanding of one and other through dialogue and joint projects on security. The symposium is an initiative of the Kadir Has University's Center for International and European Studies, the Thessaloniki-based Navarino Network and NATO's Public Diplomacy Division. Ipek Cem Taha, the Director of the Columbia Global Centers | Istanbul, opened the meeting and talked about the Cem Papandreou International Peace Award by emphasizing the importance of the spirit of peace and intercultural dialogue. Volkan Vural, retired ambassador, spoke about Greek-Turkish relations in the context of the European Union. Associate Professor Ioannis N. Grigoriadis, SIPA’02, talked about his new research project on the prospects for a peaceful settlement in Cyprus. Participants discussed the EU’s impact on Turkish-Greek relations and reconciliation, the importance of the role of civil society and political figures for the rapprochement process. Speakers: Ipek Cem Taha, Director, Columbia Global Centers | Istanbul Volkan Vural, Member of the Turkish - Greek Forum Soli Özel, Kadir Has University, Member of the Turkish - Greek Forum Ioannis N.
