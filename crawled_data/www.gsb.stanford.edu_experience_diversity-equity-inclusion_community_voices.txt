Voices of the Community Hear from members of the community as they share experiences or personal perspectives on diversity, equity, and inclusion. My mom always said: ‘Surround yourself with people who raise you higher.’ Conor O’Meara MBA ’22 It‘s clear from my work that we experience disagreement, especially in the domain of politics, in a deeply personal way. Charles Chu PhD ’23 I want to help women in tech make their voices louder, to be a supporter for them. Prao Suchato MBA ’22 Our tribe elects me to represent them and it’s deeply gratifying work. Jennifer Fate Velaise MBA ’88 The world we live in today is all hyphenated and intertwined already — we just need the media to catch up. Marty Chen MSx Class of 2021 I’ve been focusing on and talking about these issues of social change for a very, very long time. Sarah Soule The Morgridge Professor of Organizational Behavior In Latin America, gaps of access to quality education are pervasive, and we want to change that. Daniel Uribe Villa MBA/MA in Education, ’21 I had a chance to return home with knowledge and experience and connections. Guram “Guga” Tsanava MS ’12 How can I help make this a reality, where Black women are in positions of power and are able to lift up our communities? Dara Treseder MBA ’14 I want to spend my career making technology products accessible, especially for disenfranchised groups. Areeba Kamal MBA ’21 One in five people have a diagnosed disability, but everyone has something they struggle with. Annabelle Williams SEP ’19 I want to make sure anyone who works under me understands that I accept them for who they are. Jurelle June Mendoza MBA ’21 I am focused on authenticity. I want people to be able to bring their full selves to work, whatever their identity is. Kudzi Chikumbu MBA ’16 I think for any entrepreneur, there’s a risk in being overconfident in your own decisions. That’s something I want to improve. Xiaorong (Hazel) Hu SEP ’19 Kids need to grow up seeing children who look like them, in whatever media they’re consuming. Oladoyin Oladapo MBA ’20 Diversity is about fostering individual views and capabilities. Andrew Jakubowski MBA ’20 Service will produce its own reward. Ira D. Hall MBA ’76 When you think about a marginalized individual or a child, they don’t just need food that’s nutritious, or clothes, or education, or healthcare. They need it all. Shainoor Khoja SEP ’16 Realizing you’re not alone is incredibly powerful. Beth Parker LGBTQ Executive Leadership Program ’16 This place is something else. I’ve been amazed by the caliber of the discussions. Phillipe Diego Rodriguez MBA ’20 We had no money. No social status. But every morning, my mother told us that education was our weapon. Sophia Shramko MBA ’19 These projects take the theoretical tools and apply them to real-world problems and systems in practice. Ashutosh Thakur PhD ’20 It was only because I was candid about my challenges that I got the opportunity. I learned to just tell it like I see it. Rukaiyah Adams MBA ’08 It’s one thing to consume media and another thing to create it — it is very empowering. Jess Adepoju MBA ’19 The focus of most of my research is figuring out ways to help people make better financial decisions. Wendy De La Rosa PhD ’21 You have to make the magic happen. Jessica Rodriguez MBA ’01 In some ways, all of academia hinges on this receptiveness to having your mind changed. Mohammad Akbarpour Associate Professor of Economics Investing is a lot about people and a lot about human decision-making. Owen Wurzbacher MBA ’19 The idea of having to leave the direct, hands-on work and becoming a decision-maker — it didn’t appeal to me in the beginning. JoAnn Morgan MS ’77 I exceeded my wildest expectations in finding people I connected with. Samanthe Tiver Belanger E-IPER MS/MBA ’18 If you define success narrowly, you may miss out on future opportunities. Denise Peck MBA ’85 I feel more confident and better equipped to be an ally to an identity that’s not my own. Kirsten N.J. Byron MS ’18 Why not choose to be happy and positive? Pamon Forouhar MBA ’18 I thought tech was what I wanted, but I couldn’t find a purpose in my day-to-day work. Chaitra Yarlagadda MBA ’19 How do I prepare for a job that doesn’t exist yet? Beth Berger MBA ’18 It takes tremendous time and energy and emotional toil to pretend you’re something you’re not. Zoe Dunning MBA ’93 I’d been in scrappy teams, in startup culture. I wanted to learn in a more structured way and be a more analytical thinker. Tawanda Mahere MBA ’19 If you’re not making others better, you’re not leading. José E. Feliciano MBA ’99 When you’re gone, what will your legacy be? What did you do to make the world a better place? Clinton Etheridge MBA ’74 I’m learning how to speak business so I can realize my artistic goals. Richard Lonsdorf MSx Class of 2018 Being entrepreneurial doesn’t mean you need to be the founder. Celina Johnson MBA ’08 Economics is an area that allows you to approach important issues that have a lot of impact on people. Susan Athey The Economics of Technology Professor I’d rather try my hardest and fail than play it safe and ask, ‘What if?’ Robyn Sue Fisher MBA ’07 Everybody had worked to open those doors, and I thought, ‘I have to walk through them.’ Kathryn Leary MBA ’77 It was a shock, but it was also a learning experience. It helped me open up. Eugenio Garza y Garza MBA ’97 There are small gestures that can be such big moments for people. Gabrielle Scrimshaw MBA ’17 My superpower is empathy. Justine Evirs Stanford Ignite – Post-9/11 Veterans ’17 Because I grew up in a conservative religious tradition myself, it was easier to see how much united rather than divided us.
