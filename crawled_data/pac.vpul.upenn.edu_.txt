The arts are a vibrant and integral part of the University of Pennsylvania’s culture, and The Performing Arts Council (PAC) offers you the opportunity to participate in 47 different student-run performance organizations. From dance to spoken word, jazz to stand-up comedy, theatre, a cappella and more, if you have interest we have an outlet! Student Performing Arts is one of the most diverse and exciting communities at Penn. With more than 60 performances a year, Penn is the perfect place to discover or fine tune your writing, directing, design, choreography, technical and of course, performance skills! Explore the Performing Arts Council Sub-Committees: A Cappella Council (ACK) ACK is made of fifteen a cappella groups ranging in size and style. Dance Arts Coucil (DAC) There’s something for every dancer among the twelve DAC groups, ranging from classical technique to cultural dance. Singers, Musicians, and Comedians (SMAC) PAC’s most diverse sub-committee, SMAC, is made up of thirteen groups including the instrumentalists, poets, and comedy troupes. Theatre Arts Council (TAC-e) The seven student theatre groups that make up TAC-e are driven by wide-ranging missions.
