Articulation Agreement” defines a relationship between the University of Cincinnati School of Information Technology and K-12 school districts that awards advanced standing college credit for high school students who complete the articulated courses with a minimum grade requirement. Through a bilateral agreement, students enroll in high school courses and receive college credit upon their enrollment at UC. The agreements stipulate additional credentials for the teachers and only courses taught by credentialed teachers will received the college credit. University of Cincinnati School of Information Technology has bilateral articulation agreements with many Early IT K-12 district partners. These agreements are outlined on this page. As you plan your Early IT path, consult this page, ask your high school counselor, or contact us at calhoual@uc.edu Bilateral agreements are one of several pathways to complete the Early IT program.
