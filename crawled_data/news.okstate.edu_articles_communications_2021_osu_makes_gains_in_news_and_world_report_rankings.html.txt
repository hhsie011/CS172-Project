The College of Education and Human Sciences’ education graduate program moved up 14 spots to No. 91, tying with the University of Oklahoma, Iowa State University and Texas Tech University. For more information on education graduate programs, visit the CEH site at https://okla.st/3sDJ1pk The Spears School of Business’ part-time MBA program moved to No. 52 in the 2022 rankings of 273 colleges and universities surveyed after being ranked No. 89 last year. That mark is good for second-best among Big 12 Conference schools. For more information on graduate business education at OSU, visit the OSU MBA website at https://okla.st/31DedZX OSU’s engineering programs in the College of Engineering, Architecture and Technology were well-represented. Ranked as the No. 108 overall program, engineering moved up in a slew of categories from last year. In aerospace, OSU came in at No. 51, good for a gain of five spots from last year. In agricultural engineering, OSU was highly regarded with a stable No. 18 ranking. OSU maintained its spot at No. 97 in computer engineering and No. 31 in industrial engineering, while environmental engineering moved up a couple of spots from No. 72 to 70. The material engineering program saw a rise of six spots to No. 92 — best in the state. Mechanical engineering rose three spots to No. 91. For more information on the engineering graduate programs, visit the CEAT site at https://okla.st/30pFm0a The OSU Center for Health Sciences earned a No. 10 ranking in rural care and the No. 7 spot in health professional shortage areas. In primary care, OSU-CHS is No. 42 in primary care production and No. 13 in diversity.
