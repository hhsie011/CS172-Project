A “C” (2.0) or better is required in all courses applied to the major. Language Requirement Linguistics majors are required to take two progressive semesters of any two languages or four progressive semesters of any one language. Core Requirements (15 units) LING 351 - Introduction to Linguistic Phonetics and Phonology (3) LING 406 - Descriptive Linguistics (3) LING 408 - Syntax (3) LING 412 - Sociolinguistics (3) LING 430 - Historical Linguistics (3) Electives (18 units) Two courses must be from upper-division linguistics courses other than those listed as required above; and four may be selected from other upper-division linguistics courses, or from the courses listed below: CAS 312 - Human Growth and Development (3) ENGL 303 - The Structure of Modern English (3) Modern Languages, any upper-division course (3) PHIL 368 - First Course in Symbolic Logic (3) PSYC 415 - Cognitive Processes (3) Additional Information Students must consult with an adviser in linguistics before establishing their individual programs of study. Other courses in the university may be taken as electives with the permission of the adviser.
