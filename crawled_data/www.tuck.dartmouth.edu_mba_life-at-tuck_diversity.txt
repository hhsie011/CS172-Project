Here students, faculty, and staff from all backgrounds learn, teach, and work together. Discover our action plan for advancing Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion as well as resources, news, data, and more at our DEI website. Learn more Our commitment to diversity applies to the curriculum and to our educational approach. Our global business perspective means that we value differences—cultural, historical, social. Understanding the spectrum of experiences and outlooks is essential for leaders who will manage diverse workforces. Ensuring that everyone feels comfortable in the Tuck community accomplishes more than harmony; it improves the learning process. It is a critical component of leadership development. There are many resources available to you including student clubs and partner organizations. We encourage you to utilize these resources throughout the exploration and application process. Women at Tuck Tuck is committed to bringing women into our classrooms and into business worldwide. We enroll among the highest percentages of women for top MBA programs. Tuck DivCo The Tuck Diversity Conference (DivCo), created in 1994, offers prospective students a weekend of discussion, networking, mentoring, and socializing. Military Whether sharing their unique leadership skills or demonstrating what it means to be a part of a team, veteran students contribute greatly to the Tuck community. International Students International students bring vital global, cultural, and social perspectives to Tuck. Partner Organizations See the organizations that the Tuck School partners with to support diversity and inclusion. Cultural Affinity Clubs Affinity clubs and activities at Tuck offer support, valuable resources, and opportunities to connect with and celebrate our diverse community. Class Profile Bringing together a talented and dynamic student body is critical to our mission of educating tomorrow’s wise, decisive leaders. Tuck Association of Diverse Alumni (TADA) TADA's mission centers around its commitment to promoting the interests and supporting the ongoing needs of diverse alumni and students. Women in Business Conference Tuck WIBC brings together prospective students, current students, alumni, faculty and staff for two days of workshops, panel discussions and more.
