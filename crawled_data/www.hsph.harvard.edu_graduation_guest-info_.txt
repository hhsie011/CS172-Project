Guests and graduates should visit this page prior to the event for any updates to the masking policy. Vaccination policy All guests of graduation events hosted by the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health must be prepared to provide proof of full vaccination against COVID-19 to gain admittance. Full vaccination is defined as: Either a 2-dose series of an mRNA COVID-19 vaccine (Pfizer-BioNTech or Moderna) or a single-dose COVID-19 vaccine (Janssen vaccine) AND A booster dose of one of the above-listed vaccines; OR The equivalent dose of another WHO-approved COVID-19 vaccine. For entry, guests must be prepared to present one of the following: A hard copy or digital image of a CDC vaccination card; OR An image or printout of any official immunization record. We strongly encourage everyone—students, faculty, guests— to take a rapid test the morning of their ceremony. However, results of a PCR or rapid test are not valid for entry. Mask policy Masks are strongly encouraged at all Harvard Chan School events, including our convocation ceremonies and receptions. Harvard University community members are encouraged to have an acceptable face covering on their persons at all times for situations in which they may be required. At this time, we also strongly recommend voluntary masking indoors and in crowded settings outdoors. Surgical-style and KN-95 masks are available on campus and will be available at the Reggie Lewis Center. More information on Harvard University’s COVID-19 safety measures can be found on the University’s Commencement website. Please return to this page for the latest updates on masking guidance. Read the latest COVID-19 guidance for the Harvard Chan community Information on testing, masking, and what to do if you are exhibiting symptoms Security and bag policy Graduates and guests will walk through metal detectors. If you have a medical condition that prevents you from being able to enter through a metal detector, please show your Medical Device ID to a security officer and they make alternative arrangements. All persons, bags, and personal items are subject to inspection. No backpacks of any type will be allowed on campus or at the Reggie Lewis Center. No bag or item larger than 12 x 12”x 12” will be permitted excepting medical equipment. No luggage may be brought or stored at a ceremony location, please make other arrangements. Backpacks are prohibited in all ceremony locations and all bags and personal items are subject to inspection. Transportation and shuttles For transportation options for each event, please visit the transportation and shuttles page. Accommodations for guests with disabilities Any graduate or guest seeking accommodations should contact Colleen Cronin. Guest tickets A ticket is required to gain entry into all ceremony locations. All children over 2 years of age require their own ticket. Strollers are not permitted in ceremony locations. How do I request tickets? Graduating students may request tickets through the appropriate Ceremony Information and Response Form (CIRF): Class of 2022 CIRF Classes of 2020 and 2021 CIRF Convocation and Commencement are ticketed events and those without a ticket will not be admitted into the ceremony. Only attending graduates will receive guest tickets. Graduates may request up to two (2) guest tickets for the Harvard Chan Convocation Ceremony on Wednesday May 25. Graduates may request up to two (2) guest tickets for the University Commencement ceremony on Thursday May 26. Graduates do not need a ticket for themselves, only their guests. All children over 2 years old must have their own ticket. The sale of graduation tickets is prohibited, students are welcome to forward their unwanted guest tickets to other students and their guests. Tickets for May 25 are electronic and will be emailed to graduates directly. Tickets for May 26 are physical and will be distributed to graduates at the School in May. How many tickets can I request for each event? Harvard Chan Convocation 2 (two) guest tickets Harvard University Commencement 2 (two) guest tickets I don't have enough tickets for all my guests. Can they still watch the ceremonies? Additional indoor viewing for both Chan Convocation ceremonies on Wednesday May 25 and Saturday May 28 is available in the FXB Ground Floor at 655 Huntington Ave. Who can I contact with additional questions? You can reach out to the Graduation Team anytime throughout the year at convocation@hsph.harvard.edu. You are also welcome to contact the Office for Student Affairs at StudentAffairs@hsph.harvard.edu or 617-432-1036, visit our main website at https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/student-affairs/, or stop by the office in the Kresge G-4 suite. Please Note: All graduating students wishing to attend the ceremony must have filled out the CIRF form (information above under “How do I request tickets”) Students do not need to request separate guest tickets for themselves.
