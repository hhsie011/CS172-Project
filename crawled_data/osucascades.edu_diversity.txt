Chat with Us Report Bias Instagram Facebook Our Approach Our work is guided by social justice principles, including: Focusing on overcoming institutional structural barriers Counteracting unfairness and inequity Paying attention to how people, policies, practices and every part of the organization can collaborate Recognizing the importance and impact of historical and institutionalized social categories including race, class, gender, sexual orientation, ability and others Actively addressesing dynamics of bias, oppression and privilege Recognizing that every member of the university plays a key role in advancing the goals of diversity, equity and inclusion Raising awareness Working to identify the root causes for injustice Creating capacity Building networks and partnerships Influencing decision-making across the institution Monitoring and regularly reporting outcomes Our Work Our work is guided by the OSU Office of Institutional Diversity and tailored for the Central Oregon community. This work includes: Conducting climate assessments Facilitating workshops and other educational programming Hosting cultural and other events Collaborting with community partners Supporting the success of students and staff from historically marginalized and underrepresented backgrounds Preventing and responding to bias Protected Statuses and Identities OSU-Cascades, in compliance with state and federal laws and regulations, does not discriminate on the basis of age, color, disability, gender identity or expression, genetic information, marital status, national origin, race, religion, sex, sexual orientation, or veteran status in any of its policies, procedures, or practices.
