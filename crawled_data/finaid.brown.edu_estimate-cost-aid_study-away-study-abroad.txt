Brown students may apply to study away from Brown, either abroad or away at another U.S. institution, and to transfer their coursework back to Brown. Programs may be summer, fall, spring or full academic year. Rules regarding transfer credit are complex; students must obtain pre-approval for the institution at which they wish to study as well as for the courses they wish to take. Study Abroad Students may explore study abroad options by attending the study abroad fair in the fall, going to an information session in the Office of International Programs (OIP) or browsing the OIP website. Financial aid information and requirements: Study Abroad during the academic year Summer Study Abroad Study Away With the appropriate permissions, Brown students are allowed to study and earn credit at other colleges and universities as part of their degree. U.S. citizens and permanent residents of the United States may petition to study at a four-year college or university in the U.S. and students studying on F-1 visas may petition to study at a four-year institution in their home countries. Students must obtain pre-approval in order to transfer course credit back to Brown.
