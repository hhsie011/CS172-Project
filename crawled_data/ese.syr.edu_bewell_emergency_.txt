Emergency Services Information Link Syracuse University Ambulance Link If you are experiencing a life-threatening emergency or you are in immediate danger, call 911. Contact Syracuse University Ambulance Call 315.443.4299 Call #78 using a cell phone Syracuse University Ambulance (SUA) provides basic life support and emergency medical services to the Syracuse University community, 24-hours a day during the academic year. Using multiple emergency response vehicles and teams, SUA is capable of providing basic life support (BLS), advanced emergency medical technician (AEMT) services, rapid cardiac defibrillation and transport to area hospitals and trauma centers. To provide advanced life support (ALS) services, or provide service when SUA is not available to respond, SUA will request American Medical Response (AMR) in the city of Syracuse. In some critical situations, advanced interventions are required to help ensure your health. In situations like these, you may receive a bill from American Medical Response for the cost of having a paramedic assist you. When required, an ALS unit will provide advanced life support interventions on the University ambulance as outlined by Central New York Emergency Medical Services protocol. Please note, Medical Transport is not a 24-hour service. When referred by the Barnes Center at The Arch health and wellness team, Medical Transportation (MTS) provides students transport to local doctor appointments and hospitals. Additionally, MTS provides transportation to and from student housing/classes when a student is temporarily disabled due to injury. Certain restrictions do apply, so please contact MTS for an explanation of the service. To contact Syracuse University Medical Transport, please call 315.443.4566.
