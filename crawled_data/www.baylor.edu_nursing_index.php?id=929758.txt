Work and Education Dr. Prater has taught nursing at the Baylor University Louise Herrington School of Nursing for over 30 years as an Instructor, Lecturer and Senior Lecturer. Her major teaching areas include: professional development, foundations for practice, Undergraduate; Clinical internship; OB Clinical, Undergraduate; OB Theory, Undergraduate; Women’s Health Care in Great Britain and Argentina, Undergraduate Nursing Elective; Professional practice laboratory, Nursing skills, Undergraduate and Servant leadership, Graduate program. Previous to LHSON, Dr. Prater was a Clinical Instructor of Maternal-Child Nursing at the University of Texas at Austin, School of Nursing. Her active certifications include: Certified Nurse Educator (CNE); Neonatal Resuscitation Program (NRP); Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS). She was also certified through 1999 as an ASPO Certified Childbirth Educator (ACCE). Dr. Prater’s additional experience other than education is comprehensive. She worked as a Dallas Staff Nurse in Labor and Delivery for Methodist Medical Center since 2008 and has served as an expert witness/nurse consultant for various law firms since 1999. Previously, she was a Clinical Associate and Clinical Examiner for Excelsior College; Staff Nurse for Labor & Delivery, Mother-Baby Care and Instructor, Sibling Preparation for Mesquite Community Hospital; Contract Instructor-Childbirth Educator for The Family Way in Dallas; CPR Instructor for the American Heart Association; Perinatal Education Coordinator, Nurse Epidemiologist, Clinical Nurse Liaison in Maternal Child Nursing with Methodist Medical Center and Sibling Class Instructor and Childbirth Instructor with St. David’s Hospital in Austin.
