Harvard-Yenching Library The Tibetan Collection at Harvard-Yenching Library centers on Buddhism but also includes holdings in history and literature. Strengths of the collection include printings of the 17th- and 18th-century editions of the Kanjur and the Tanjur. The Harvard Library also subscribes to the Tibetan Buddhist Resource Center, a digital library that contains about 17,000 volumes of Tibetan texts. Accessing These Materials Both Harvard-Yenching Library and Widener Library hold Tibetan-language materials. New publications from China, India and Tibet are regularly added at Widener Library and are supplemented by a growing collection of older materials in electronic form. All materials can be located through Romanized records in HOLLIS.
