The partnership was founded on a promising opportunity to advance college readiness in Regions 16 and 17 and increase cross-institutional collaboration. After six years, Texas Tech University will no longer be offering OnRamps courses and teacher training directly; however, those opportunities remain available through UT Austin. Following the 2021-2022 academic year, active district partners can expect a seamless transition and high-quality experience working alongside UT OnRamps' Partnerships team. The team offers a proven track record of success in service to, and support of, campuses and can continue to meet the needs of these communities. Additionally, UT OnRamps will continue to be an option for school districts in Regions 16 and 17 interested in joining anew. OnRamps offers: 16 dual enrollment courses in Math, Science, Technology, Arts, and Humanities through distance education, Year-long Professional Learning and Development for OnRamps Instructors and Professional Development opportunities for non-OnRamps educators, Comprehensive partner support, including guidance on OnRamps implementation, recruitment resources, assistance with student and teacher retention, and fellowship with 196 school districts from across the state. To learn more about OnRamps at UT Austin, visit https://onramps.utexas.edu, or email the Partnerships team at partnerships@onramps.zendesk.com. We are grateful for the extensive partnership that developed between Texas Tech University and the University of Texas at Austin to advance OnRamps' mission of increasing the number and diversity of students who engage in learning experiences aligned with the expectations of leading research universities.
