The general philosophy program requires the following: Argumentation and Writing Requirement (3 units) PHIL 315 - Philosophical Argument and Writing (3) Required Courses in the History of Philosophy Before 1900 (9 units) PHIL 290 - History of Philosophy: Greek Philosophy (3) PHIL 300 - History of Philosophy: Rationalism and Empiricism (3) PHIL 301 - History of Philosophy: Kant and the 19th Century (3) Additional History of Philosophy Requirement (6 units) PHIL 291 - History of Philosophy: Medieval Philosophy (3) PHIL 323 - Existentialism (3) PHIL 350 - Asian Philosophy (3) PHIL 379 - American Philosophy (3) PHIL 380 - Analytic Philosophy: 1900 to 1950 (3) PHIL 382 - Marx and Marxism (3) PHIL 383 - Postmodernism (3) Other Course Requirements (9 units) Ethics and Society: PHIL 343 - Philosophy of Feminism (3) PHIL 345 - Social and Political Philosophy (3) PHIL 377 - Philosophical Approaches to Race, Class, and Gender (3) PHIL 410 - Ethical Theory (3) Metaphysics, Epistemology: PHIL 420 - Metaphysics (3) PHIL 425 - Phenomenology (3) PHIL 430 - Epistemology (3) PHIL 435 - Philosophy of Language (3) PHIL 440 - Philosophy of Mind (3) Any additional course Any additional course liste in Ethics and Society or Metaphysics, Epistemology or PHIL 368 - First Course in Symbolic Logic (3) Seminar Requirement (3 units) PHIL 447T - Seminar in Selected Problems (3) PHIL 455 - Seminar in Values (3) PHIL 470 - Seminar in Metaphysics and Epistemology (3) PHIL 480 - Seminar in the History of Philosophy (3) PHIL 490 - Seminar in Contemporary Philosophy (3) Electives (9 units) Choose nine units of philosophy courses, no more than six units of lower-division, which have not been used to fulfill other requirements. Recommended Coursework for Philosophy Majors Students are advised to supplement their studies in philosophy with coursework offered in fields closely related to their philosophical interests. Graduate School Students who are planning to attend graduate school in philosophy are urged to acquire proficiency in a foreign language and include in their programs as many of the following as possible: PHIL 303 - Introduction to the Philosophy of Science (3) PHIL 345 - Social and Political Philosophy (3) PHIL 368 - First Course in Symbolic Logic (3) PHIL 375 - Meaning and Mind (3) PHIL 380 - Analytic Philosophy: 1900 to 1950 (3) PHIL 420 - Metaphysics (3) PHIL 430 - Epistemology (3) PHIL 440 - Philosophy of Mind (3) PHIL 455 - Seminar in Values (3) Law School Students interested in pursuing admission to law school are advised to include in their programs as many of the following as possible: PHIL 312 - Business and Professional Ethics (3) PHIL 313 - Environmental Ethics (3) PHIL 314 - Medical Ethics (3) PHIL 345 - Social and Political Philosophy (3) PHIL 355 - Philosophy of Law (3) PHIL 368 - First Course in Symbolic Logic (3) PHIL 410 - Ethical Theory (3) PHIL 455 - Seminar in Values (3) Total (120 units) Back to Top | Print Degree Planner (opens a new window) | Print-Friendly Page (opens a new window) Facebook this Page (opens a new window) Tweet this Page (opens a new window) All catalogs © 2022 California State University Fullerton.
