In addition, the Pre-Professional Advisor serves as the primary advisor for students interested in pursuing a career in one of the following Allied Health fields: Cytotecnology, Dental Hygiene, Health Informatics and Management, Medical Technology, Occupational Therapy, and Respiratory Care. Benefits of Utilizing the Pre-Health Resource Center: Guest speakers and panel discussions from health science professionals and current health science students Opportunity to meet with admissions officials from the UT Health Science Center, the Southern College of Optometry, East Tennessee State University College of Medicine, and other health science professionals Catalogs and resource information from professional schools across the country Assistance with the admissions application to professional school Information on internships, job/volunteer opportunities, scholarships, and fellowship programs University of Memphis Partnerships with Health Colleges: (Contact the Pre-Health Advisor for additional information and requirements.)
