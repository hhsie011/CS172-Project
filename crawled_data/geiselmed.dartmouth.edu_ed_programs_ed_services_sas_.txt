We believe that students with disabilities are a crucial element of a diverse healthcare workforce, and it is our experience that many individuals with disabilities are qualified to study and practice medicine. Read More about Getting Started Click here to learn more or start a request for an accommodation. Accommodations are determined on an individual basis through an interactive process that includes the student and the Geisel Office of Student Accessibility Services (GSAS). Learn more Rights and Responsibilities Students' rights and responsibilities are a priority at the Geisel School of Medicine. Learn more at the link below. Learn more Concerns or Grievances Dartmouth is committed to providing equal opportunities for students, faculty, staff, and visitors with disabilities to access and participate in academic, social, cultural, and recreational programs at Dartmouth. Learn more Injuries and Temporary Conditions Temporary conditions such as broken bones, concussions, surgeries, or acute illnesses are generally not regarded as disabilities but may still be eligible for academic accommodation. Students are encouraged to contact GSAS to discuss possible remedies. Learn more Confidentiality Disclosure of disability is voluntary and protected under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA).
