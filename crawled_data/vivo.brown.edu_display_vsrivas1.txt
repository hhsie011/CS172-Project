Reisman Assistant Professor of Engineering Overview Dr. Srivastava is currently the Howard M. Reisman Assistant Professor of Engineering at Brown University. He received his Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from MIT in 2010. Following his Ph.D., Dr. Srivastava worked at ExxonMobil Upstream Research and Corporate Strategic Research organizations in various roles, including Senior Technical Professional Advisor – Mechanics of Materials, Mechanics Team Lead, Marine Team Lead, Fitness for Service Research Area Lead, and Worldwide Deepwater Drilling Coordinator. He joined Brown University in 2018. Dr. Srivastava'a research focuses on continuum solid mechanics theories, models, and experiments for engineering structures, clean energy, and mechanobiology. Topics covered will include factorial design and analysis of experiments, modeling of infectious disease spread and dynamics, drug delivery, and cell and tissue mechanics. Learn statistical methods, factorial design of experiments, transport models, numerical methods, nonlinear and time dependent response, soft materials modeling and applications of these methods in the biomedical systems. ENGN 1210 Biomechanics Spring (Undergraduate course) Important foundations of continuum biomechanics, properties of biomaterials, muscle biomechanics, mechanical properties and response of soft tissues and bones, three-dimensional concepts of strains and stress, linear isotropic elasticity, anisotropic response, yield, fracture, fatigue, and viscoelastic response of biological materials/structures, and biomedical implants/devices are taught. Students learn the physical basis, theory and applications of each of these topics from a biomechanics perspective.
