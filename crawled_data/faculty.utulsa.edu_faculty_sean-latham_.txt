He is the author or editor of nine books that explore topics like snobbery, genre fiction, libel law, and modernism. For the last six years, he has also served as the Director of the Oklahoma Center for the Humanities, where he has helped organize over 150 public scholarship projects that seek to address pressing challenges in our communities. The Center now serves as a hub for collaborative, interdisciplinary research that explores topics like indigenous rights, racial profiling, artificial intelligence, and food insecurity. Latham also serves as Editor of the James Joyce Quarterly and Director of the TU Institute for Bob Dylan Studies. Currently, he is at work on a book that explores Dylan creative life.
