issue of research safety (click photo above) The term Safety Culture was first introduced by the International Atomic Energy Agency as a result of their analysis of the tragic nuclear reactor accident at Chernobyl: The Safety Culture of an organization is the product of the individual and group values, attitudes, perceptions, competencies and patterns of behavior that determine the commitment to, and the style and proficiency of, an organization’s health and safety management. Environmental Health & Safety actively develops partnerships with research laboratories to evaluate, maintain and improve safety culture.
