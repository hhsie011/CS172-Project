Interdisciplinary Learning Take courses that explore the interrelation between the humanities, social sciences, and physical sciences. Primary Texts Consider the messy questions that modern disciplines set aside by examining foundational works of literature, theology, and philosophy, and politics. Seminar-style Classes Participate actively in the exposition, analysis, and criticism of texts, arguments, and experiments. Independent Research Engage in independent research with guidance and encouragement from our nationally recognized faculty through the completion of a senior thesis. Click here for more details. Extracurricular Opportunities Attend lectures and join book or film discussion groups specifically designed for Honors College students. Scholarship/Fellowship Opportunities Pursue, with the aid of academic advisors, a range of scholarship and fellowship opportunities for post-undergraduate study. Click here for more details.
