Close Search / Commencement / Commencement FAQs Commencement FAQs COMMENCEMENT Information for Graduates Commencement Registration Final Bell Commencement FAQs Commencement Speaker Webcast Commencement FAQs a What time do students need to check-in? Student check-in begins at 9:15 a.m. in the Reynolds Center Practice Gym. Please bring your cap and gown with you and leave your coats and purses at home, there is no place to store your personal belongings. The Processional will begin promptly at 9:45 a.m., so your family and friends will want to be in their seats prior to this time. a What is The Final Bell Party? A special celebration is in order to welcome the newest members of the TU Alumni Association! The Final Bell Party will take place in Bayless Plaza on Friday, May 6, 2022. Please contact the TU Office of Alumni Engagement at tualumni@utulsa.edu or 918-631-2555 with questions about Final Bell. a What is the Countdown to Commencement? Countdown to Commencement will be from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. on April 26 in the Great Hall of the ACSU. You will be able to pick up cords and tassels of participating groups, have your picture taken with Goldie, and more exciting things we are working on. Stay tuned! a How do I order regalia? Visit the Oak Hall ordering website to order your cap and gown and have it shipped directly to you. a How much does regalia cost? Bachelor – $34 + tax and shipping Master – $64 + tax and shipping Doctoral – $125 + tax and shipping Law – $125 + tax and shipping a Can we rent regalia instead of purchasing it? No, all regalia must be purchased through Oak Hall. a Is registration required for student participants? Yes. Register to attend Spring 2022 Commencement a Is registration required for guests of graduates? No, not currently. a How many guests can I bring? There is not a limit as to the number of guests you may bring. a Where should I park? Students and guests are welcome to park in any unattended lot on campus on a first-come, first-served basis. A limited number of ADA and VIP parking spaces are available in the Lorton Village Apartment Lot and the Reynolds Center Lot. Review a map of campus. a How do I request a sign language (ASL) interpreter? Sign language interpreting services for guests who are deaf or hard of hearing will be available. Please contact commencement@utulsa.edu with your request. a How do I request an ADA accommodation and/or accessible seating? Due to a limited number of wheelchair ADA seats, graduates can request accommodation by contacting commencement@utulsa.edu. ADA & VIP parking is available in the Lorton Village Apartments and the Reynolds Center Lot. Review a map of campus. a Will the ceremonies be live streamed? Yes, the links will be provided on the webcast page. a Is there a bag policy? The clear bag policy will be in effect for graduation ceremonies. A detailed list of permitted items is available in the clear bag policy. Carried-in items may be searched by personnel prior to entering the venues. Prohibited items must be returned to the owner’s vehicle or discarded. a Are there hotels near TU for my out-of-town guests? We keep a list of hotels located near the university, many of which offer TU visitors discounts on rooms. Please visit our area hotels page for a full list. a If I am earning a master’s degree, how should I wear my hood? For a tutorial on how to arrange a master’s hood for Commencement, click https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6KW_2AClkU 800 S.
