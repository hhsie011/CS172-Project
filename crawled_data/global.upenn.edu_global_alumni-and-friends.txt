Support Penn's Global Faculty Research Initiatives Go Back event Attend an Event Perry World House is Penn's home for global engagement on campus, with a focus on bringing Penn's academic knowledge to bear on some of the world's most pressing global policy challenges. Annually, Perry World House organizes a large range of public events where attendees from around the globe engage with experts from Penn and beyond, in academic, government, and the public sector. In addition, Perry World House offers a comprehensive suite of workshops and colloquia around their two research themes: the Future of the Global Order: Power Technology, and Governance and Global Shifts: Urbanization, Migration, and Climate Change. Notable guest speakers have joined these conferences, including: former United States National Security Advisors, Susan Rice and General H.R. McMaster, former UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Zeid Ra'ad Al Hussein, and Syrian Journalist Zaina Erhaim. Follow Penn Global on Twitter Learn About Penn's Global Mission Throughout the course of its nearly 300 year history, the University of Pennsylvania has sought to follow the example of its founder, Benjamin Franklin, in its commitment to global engagement. Over time, Penn's global vision has deepened and expanded, culminating in the transformation of Penn as a comprehensive global institution. Go Back view_column Penn's Global Strategic Framework The Strategic Framework for Pennâ€™s Global Initiatives aligns institutional priorities with constituent needs to pursue a coordinated, sustainable approach to global engagement, ensuring the University continues as a preeminent university in the U.S. and world in global engagement and academic diplomacy. The University's first global strategic framework was adopted in 2012 and subsequently updated in 2018. At the core of the five-year strategic framework are three pillars: 1) educate global citizens, 2) catalyze transformative ideas, and 3) bring the world to Penn and Penn to the world.
