Access Workshops Online Workshops are opportunities to increase different coping skills and wellness habits aimed towards improving your mental health and wellbeing. You can learn practical tools to effectively manage concerns related to depression, anxiety, difficult emotions, relationships and more. CAPS is offering virtual workshops that will be facilitated by staff over the span of two to three weeks with opportunities for participants to ask questions and engage with the workshop materials. We also offer workshops specifically for our graduate students. Learn more about those options and our embedded counselor in the offices of Graduate and Professional School. Feb. 8, 1:00 - 2:00 p.m. March 9, 9:00 - 10:00 a.m. March 31, 1:00 - 2:00 p.m. April 21, 1:00 - 2:00 p.m. April 26, 1:00 - 2:00 p.m. Workshop YouTube link: https://youtu.be/K8_FFS_drlc Art of Coping Workshops Description These workshops are offered as single sessions and provide various artistic activities. The benefits of taking time to practice mindfulness, utilize creativity, and engage in self-reflection through art can expand students’ coping skills and give them a space to engage with their mental health in a way that is more relaxed and less intimidating. The best students for this group are those who want to challenge perfectionism, practice socializing in a low-stress environment, or want to better understand themselves and their emotions using creativity. It would be suitable to combine with ongoing therapy or as a solo workshop for those who are at lower risk. Who Am I? Art Exercise This workshop will provide you with space and time to reflect on how you view yourself. You can create an artistic picture that represents your traits, identity, and self-image and have the chance to share it with others. Surfing the Wave: Art Exercise The goal of this workshop is to provide you with space and perspective around how you navigate stress, emotions, and difficulty with your life. You will create an image that allows you to be honest with yourself and reflect on how you surf the waves of life. You will also have the chance to share with others. Our Emotions: Art Exercise This workshop provides an honest space of reflection where you can create an image to represent your emotional world, including but not limited to how you feel currently or how your emotions are experienced in your daily life. You will also have the chance to share with others. Artful Affirmations: Art Exercise The goal of this workshop is to help you create beautiful, no-cost self-affirmation cards to encourage you when life stressors or emotions are difficult. There will also be a conversation around areas we feel we need affirmation the most and a space to share with and learn from others. Workshop Dates Tuesday, April 5, 10:00 - 11:00 a.m. Who am I? Monday, April 11, 4:00 - 5:00 p.m. Surfing the Wave Thursday, April 14, 2:00 - 3:00 p.m. Our Emotions Monday, April 18, 4:00 - 5:00 p.m. Artful Affirmations Tuesday, April 19, 10:00 - 11:00 a.m. Self-esteem Wall Monday, April 25, 4:00 - 5:00 p.m. Cup of Self-Compassion Thursday, April 28, 2:00 - 3:00 p.m. Calming Collage Self-Esteem Wall: Art Exercise This workshop provides an honest space of reflection around your sense of self-esteem, what impacts it, how solid it is, and how that influences your daily life. There will be space to create the image and share it with others. Cup of Self-Compassion: Art Exercise The goal of this workshop is to create a cup of self-care and self-compassionate things to help foster well-being and a positive mood. You can get creative in envisioning what things fill you up when life gets draining. You will also have the chance to share with and learn from others. Calming Collage: Art Exercise This workshop provides an artistic space to reflect on what places, people, and things bring you calmness and comfort and create an image or collage to remind you in times of overwhelm. You will also have the chance to share with and learn from others. Self-Compassion, Jan. 21- In this workshop, participants will learn the definition of self-compassion, its three elements and some of its benefits. Participants will also engage in several self-compassion practices. Assertiveness, Jan. 28- This workshop will review two of the components of assertiveness: respecting the needs of others while clearly and respectfully communicating your wants, needs, positions, and boundaries to others. Some of the benefits of being assertive, the costs of not being assertive, and examples of assertive behaviors will also be reviewed. Stress-Management, Feb. 4- Stress is a part of life; however, often we do not incorporate stress relief into our daily routines which, over time, can cause stress to build up and adversely affect our well-being and productivity. This workshop will review several effective stress relieving techniques to enhance stress management and overall well-being.
