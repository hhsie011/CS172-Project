In this new role, Mikele will work to develop a strategic plan for parent and family engagement with the University and develop resources to help parents and families navigate the campus structure. In collaboration with stakeholders within the Division of Campus Life and other University departments, Mikele's work will create programs and services that partner with parents and families to enhance the student experience, especially with respect to Brown's Liberal Learning Goals for its Open Curriculum and autonomous, self-directed, and critically-conscious students. The aim is to build a strong campus-wide network of professional staff to engage parents and families from matriculation to graduation Mikele began her career at Brown in 2009 as a member of the Event Management team within the University Event and Conference Services office. In 2015, Mikele was named Associate Director of the department. As Associate Director, Mikele built a highly respected team of Event professionals dedicated to the strategic management of conferences, lectures, and third-party business. During her tenure, she had direct oversight of over 100 university-sponsored events per year and led the planning and management of important annual celebrations and ceremonies including Convocation, Family Weekend, Mid-Year Completion, and Commencement, an event that includes 50+ ceremonies and typically draws over 10,000 alumni, families, and guests each year. Other highlights of her event work include Brown's 250+ Anniversary Celebration, the Inauguration of President Christina H. Paxson, the Achebe Colloquium on Africa, the Casey Shearer Memorial Lecture series, the [email protected] Celebration, the university’s successful attempt at breaking a Guinness World Record, and “A Conversation with Jessica Meir ‘99” broadcast live from the International Space Station. In 2018, Mikele was awarded Brown University’s Excellence Award in Leadership, and in January of 2020, she was appointed Interim Director of the University Event & Conference Services Department. During the Covid-19 pandemic, she successfully implemented tools and procedures to facilitate virtual and hybrid events, shared best practices with event-producing staff across campus, and worked to ensure in-person events were conducted with careful attention to health and safety protocols. To assist with the new challenges of producing virtual and hybrid ceremonies, she helped to found the Ivy+ Commencement group with peers across the Ivy League. Mikele holds both an MBA and a Bachelor’s Degree in Journalism from the University of Rhode Island.
