This year, the Graduate Student Government has organized a virtual student orientation and welcome. On Thursday August 19th, this event will include special guest speakers; information sessions with different UC offices, organizations, and departments; and networking sessions and presentations. You can attend the GSG Student Orientation and Welcome on Thursday, August 19th from 10:00AM-2:30PM. Welcome Session Special Guest Speakers Thursday, August 19th: 10:00AM-11:15AM right arrow down arrow 10:05AM : Official welcome from UC to graduate students 10:15AM : Official welcome from UC graduate school 10:25AM : Resources for Students 10:35AM : Safety during Pandemic 10:45AM : Introduction to GSG by the GSG Executive Board 11:15AM : Wrap-up Information Session Student Funding and Financial Literacy Thursday, August 19th: 11:30AM-12:15PM right arrow down arrow This session will guide graduate students to understand the importance of financial wellness and literacy. Topics discussed will include: concerns during the COVID crisis; campus and community need-based resources; merit awards; taxes for graduate students, and more. Please refer to the resource links below for more information. Student Resources Thursday, August 19th: 12:00PM-12:45PM right arrow down arrow This workshop will focus on informing students of various resources available to graduate students. Topics to be discussed can include: the bearcat pantry (BCP), Langsam Library Resources, preparing future faculty, and more. Student Resources Graduate Student Experience Thursday, August 19th: 12:30PM-1:15PM right arrow down arrow This workshop will focus on having an open discussion about graduate student experiences, for domestic, international, and non-traditional students, at UC. Graduate Student Experience, Pt 1 Graduate Student Experience, Pt 2 Diversity, Mental Health, and Work-Life Balance Thursday, August 19th: 1:00PM-1:45PM right arrow down arrow This workshop will focus on having an open discussion with graduate students about diversity and inclusion, balancing graduate school and life, and prioritizing mental health and overall wellness. AACRC and UC Wellness Center UC Women's Center Housing and Community Thursday, August 19th: 1:30PM-2:15PM right arrow down arrow This workshop will focus informing graduate students about various housing options as well as where to find supportive communities for families.
