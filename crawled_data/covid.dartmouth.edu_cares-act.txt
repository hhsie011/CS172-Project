The Quarterly Report as of March 31, 2022, appears below. The Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security Act (CARES), Pub. L. No. 116-136, 134 Stat. 281 (March 27, 2020), the Coronavirus Response and Relief Supplemental Appropriations Act, 2021 (CRRSAA), Pub. L. No. 116-260 (December 27, 2020), and American Rescue Plan Act, 2021 (ARP), Pub. L. No. 117-2 (March 11, 2021) direct institutions receiving Higher Education Emergency Relief Funds (HEERF) to submit reports to the U.S. Secretary of Education describing the use of the funds. As required by CARES, CRRSAA, and ARP, and in compliance with guidance from the U.S. Department of Education (the Department), Dartmouth College reports here its use of the Emergency Financial Aid Grants to students. The Department’s May 13, 2021 notice in the Federal Register (86 Fed. Reg. 26213) identified seven topics on which each institution receiving funding must report. The Department’s requirements appear enumerated in bold/italics below and Dartmouth's response appears immediately afterwards. An acknowledgement that the institution signed and returned to the Department the Certification and Agreement and the assurance that the institution has used the applicable amount of funds designated under the CRRSAA and ARP (a)(1) and (a)(4) programs to provide Emergency Financial Aid Grants to Students. Dartmouth College acknowledges that it signed and returned to the Department the Certification and Agreement and the assurance that the institution has used, or intends to use, funds received under CARES to provide Emergency Financial Aid Grants to students. By accepting additional HEERF under CRRSAA and ARP, Dartmouth certified to the Department that it has used, or intends to use, the funds received to provide Emergency Financial Aid Grants to students. 2. The total amount of funds that the institution will receive or has received from the Department pursuant to the institution’s Certification and Agreement for Emergency Financial Aid Grants to Students under the CRRSAA and ARP (a)(1) and (a)(4) programs. Dartmouth College received $1,714,675 from the Department pursuant to the institution's Certification and Agreement for Emergency Financial Aid Grants to Students under CARES. Dartmouth College received $857,338 from the Department for Emergency Financial Aid Grants to Students under CRRRSAA. Dartmouth College received $4,412,662 from the Department for Emergency Financial Aid Grants to Students under ARP. 3. The total amount of Emergency Financial Aid Grants distributed to students under the CRRSAA and ARP (a)(1) and (a)(4) programs as of the date of submission (i.e., as of the initial report and every calendar quarter thereafter). As of the date of this report, Dartmouth College has distributed $5,835,450 of Emergency Financial Aid Grants to students from CARES, CRRSAA, and ARP. Dartmouth has communicated with eligible students multiple times to ensure that they are aware of the availability of the funds and to answer questions about the distribution of the funds. Dartmouth will continue to communicate with eligible students, as appropriate. 4. The estimated total number of students at the institution that are eligible to receive Emergency Financial Aid Grants to Students under the CRRSAA and ARP (a)(1) and (a)(4) programs. Since the U.S. Department of Education expanded the student population eligible to receive HEERF, approximately 4,556 Dartmouth UG students became eligible to receive Emergency Financial Aid Grants under the HEERF program for the Fall 2021 Term. 5. The method(s) used by the institution to determine which students receive Emergency Financial Aid Grants and how much they would receive under the CRRSAA and ARP (a)(1) and (a)(4) programs. UG Students enrolled in the fall 2021 term who had an estimated institutionally calculated parent contribution of $20,000 or less received amounts on a sliding scale ranging from $900 for each student with no parent contribution to $400 for students with a parent contribution between $15,001 and $20,000. 7. Any instructions, directions, or guidance provided by the institution to students concerning the Emergency Financial Aid Grants. a. Students eligible to receive the funds received communications from Dartmouth's Financial Aid Office that included the following information about the funds and how to access the funds: What is the HEERF? The Higher Education Emergency Relief Funds (HEERF) provide students with emergency financial aid grants to help cover expenses related to the disruption of learning due to coronavirus. Grants will be distributed to students with exceptional need as determined through the 2021-2022 application for financial assistance. Who is eligible to receive emergency assistance funds? Eligibility for Undergraduate Students will include students who were enrolled in the fall 2021 term. Grants will be based on a parent contribution as determined by the Financial Aid Office for the 2021-2022 academic year. Students with a parent contribution of less than, or equal to, $20,000 will be offered a cash grant funded by HEERF. How do students claim these funds? To claim these funds, certify below you are incurring expenses that meet the criteria for receipt of a cash grant and provide a current mailing address for funds to be sent if direct deposit is not an option. How will the funds be processed? After you have certified through DartHub, Dartmouth will directly issue the funds to you by disbursing the funds to your student account. Cash grants will not be applied against any existing balance due to Dartmouth. Students should make separate arrangements to resolve any outstanding balances on their Dartmouth student account. The cash grant will be identified on the student account as "HEERF." Campus Billing will be processing payments the week of February 20. How do I set up direct deposit? Please read the eRefund Setup for Students (PDF) instructions. What are the tax implications of accepting these funds? Students may wish to review the Internal Revenue Service guidance available. Dartmouth cannot provide tax advice to students. Students should seek advice from a tax professional if they have questions. a. After logging into DartHub, eligible students can view the following certification statement and instructions: Certification Statement By claiming this emergency grant through the Higher Education Emergency Relief Funds (HEERF), I acknowledge that I am incurring allowable expenses due to coronavirus during my enrollment in the fall 2021 Term of at least, or in excess of, the amount received. Examples of allowable expenses include eligible expenses under my "cost of attendance" or for emergency costs that arise due to coronavirus, which are defined by the U.S. Department of Education to cover expenses such as tuition, food, housing, and health care (including mental health care) or child care.
