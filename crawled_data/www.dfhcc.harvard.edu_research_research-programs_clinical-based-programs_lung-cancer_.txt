The Program has continued to make strides in areas of lung cancer susceptibility using molecular epidemiology. Investigators in the Program are carrying out systematic genomic characterization of lung cancers to identify therapeutic targets, employing structural biology and medicinal chemistry to effectively target the genomic changes, and defining mechanisms of resistance to develop therapeutic strategies to overcome the acquired resistance. Currently, there are more than 60 program members based in multiple institutions, departments, and disciplines with expertise in smoking cessation, genetic susceptibility to tobacco smoke, the genetic changes in lung cancer, mRNA expression patterns, and novel therapeutic approaches, including pre-clinical modeling, conducting clinical trials of novel therapies, and characterization of acquired resistance.
