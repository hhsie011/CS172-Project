The application for the 2023-2024 academic year will open on September 1, 2022. To learn more about social work, the MSW at Baylor, and application process, fill out our inquiry form here. Please allow up to 72 hours of processing time for all documents. Two documents are required as part of the residential MSW application, the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) or Financial Aid Profile (for international students) and our GSSW Financial Aid Form. Any application not including these two documents will be considered incomplete. The School of Social Work offers tuition remission, graduate stipends, and departmental scholarships to its graduate students enrolled at the residential campus in Waco, Texas. Awards are made based on both need and merit. Students do not need to apply to specific scholarships. Scholarship matching will be done by the Associate Dean. Students in the standard MSW program at the residential campus in Waco, Texas, may apply for additional aid for the second year of the program by completing the returning student financial aid application. To discuss the application or available aid please contact Dr. Crystal Diaz-Espinoza. For more information regarding Tuition and Fees click here. To discuss options of Direct Loans, Texas Tuition Equalization Grant, Federal Work-Study Program, and Private Alternative Loans Programs, contact the Student Financial Aid Office: Website: www.baylor.edu/sfs Online: contact the Student Financial Aid office Phone: (254) 710-2611 or toll-free within the U.S. at 1-800-710-BAYLOR-U, option 8-2 Mail: One Bear Place #97028 Waco, TX 76798-7028 Departmental Scholarships As part of the application process, all students applying to the residential campus in Waco, Texas, are required to submit the GSSW FA Form and Free Application for Federal Aid (FAFSA) or CSS International Profile for international students. Students will automatically be considered for all departmental scholarships. Click here to find a complete list of Social Work departmental scholarships.
