Home / Enrichment Enrichment Newcomb-Tulane College helps students find and fund a variety of opportunities to take their education beyond the classroom through programs that provide experience, perspective and even life-changing educational moments that enrich your college experience. Within Newcomb-Tulane College, there are a variety of funding sources to help you pursue your dreams. The Center for Academic Equity and the Office of Academic Enrichment both offer several types of grants and scholarships to help fund internships, original research, and more! Interested in a unique international learning experience? Check out the Center for Global Education and the Office of Study Abroad to take your Tulane experience worldwide! Courses Engaging in these classes will help students develop an understanding of the research process as it is carried out within and across scholarly Events We offer guest speakers, seminars, campus-wide events, free daily coffee in Cudd Hall and Front Porch Friday on the first Friday of every month Programs NTC programs create intellectual and academic opportunities that foster faculty and student engagement inside and outside of the classroom. Global Education The Center for Global Education supports international students and scholars and develops opportunities to have intercultural experiences. Undergraduate Research Research is a high impact practice with strong, proven outcomes for undergraduate student enrichment. Grants for Academic Engagement NTC grants fund academic-based research, conference travel, internships (travel, transportation, and housing), and other projects. First Year Experience The first year experience is a set of programs, events and resources to help students thrive in their first year as NTC students. Sophomore Year Experience Sophomore year is a time for declaring a major (or two), trying new things and setting goals. We will guide you through it all. Residential Faculty Programs The Residential Faculty Mentor program increases faculty engagement with students in res halls and fosters academic life.
