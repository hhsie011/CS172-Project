Whether you want to become a physician, medical scientist or “physicianeer” we have a program to fit your needs, including numerous dual degree programs to blaze your own unique career path. Medical degrees, programs and professional certificates Doctor of Medicine (MD) College of Medicine An innovative Doctor of Medicine curriculum was recently introduced to bring more flexibility and individualization to the education process, to create lifelong learners and bring better health care to all. The curriculum integrates basic science content more closely with clinical context, provides clinical exposure earlier and reinforces basic science content in clinical rotations. Bryan-College Station Dallas Houston Round Rock Temple medicine professional doctorate More » about the MD degree Medical Science (PhD) College of Medicine The Doctorate of Philosophy in Medical Science program provides cutting-edge research training with a flexible and interdisciplinary curriculum with six areas of research (biochemistry & structural biology, cardiovascular and lymphatic biology, cell and molecular biology, clinical and translational science, microbial pathogenesis & immunology and neurosciences) that allow students a highly personalized training program. Bryan-College Station Houston medicine doctorate More » about our Medical Science (PhD) program Medical Science (MS) College of Medicine The MS in Medical Science curriculum is flexible and designed to provide a strong foundation in biological sciences along with scholarly activities that will encourage team-oriented projects, enhance interdisciplinary communication, develop leadership skills, and provide the foundation for a broad understanding of scientific, medical and regulatory issues. Bryan-College Station medical science masters More » about our Medical Science (MS) program Education for Healthcare Professionals (MS) College of Medicine Students in the master’s program, which is designed to develop skills to become excellent teachers in their areas of specialty, may pick a 36-hour non-thesis track that requires a project, or students can complete 34 hours with a thesis. With either option, students will learn how to conduct research to improve and develop innovative educational programs in health professions education. Online medicine masters More » about our Education for Healthcare Professionals (MS) program Education for Healthcare Professionals (CERT) College of Medicine For students who wish to expand their knowledge of teaching and curriculum development, the certificate program prepares students to teach in a variety of settings with an interdisciplinary team. Coursework may be applied to the master's degree if the student wishes to pursue the advanced degree in the future. Online medicine certificate More » about our Education for Healthcare Professionals certificate program MD/PhD College of Medicine This combined training program leading to both Doctor of Medicine and Doctorate of Philosophy degrees provides research training for highly motivated medical students planning careers in academic medicine. Such training produces medical scientists with a valuable, and well-balanced, ability to relate their study of clinical medicine with basic biomedical science and provide unique insights into human disease processes. Bryan-College Station Houston medicine professional doctorate More » about our Professional + Doctorate (MD/PhD) program MD + Business Administration (MD+MBA) College of Medicine The MBA curriculum is designed to develop the knowledge and expertise students need to meet the challenges of an increasingly complex and rapidly changing medical field. The skills acquired are easily applied in a hospital or clinical setting. Bryan-College Station medicine professional masters More » about our MD + MBA program MD + Public Health (MD+MPH) College of Medicine Students that complete a Master of Public Health gain knowledge on the science and art of preventing disease, prolonging life, and promoting health through the organized efforts and informed choices of society, organizations, public and private, communities and individuals. The MPH’s emphasis on health at the population level adds an important dimension of insight and understanding of the dynamics of health and wellness to the practice of medicine. Bryan-College Station medicine professional masters More » about our MD+MPH program MD + Master of Science (MD+MS) College of Medicine The MS in Medical Science degree is available through the Clinical & Translational Science (CTS) track of the College of Medicine graduate program. The overall educational goal of this track is to prepare future physician-scientists to bridge the interface between patient care and bench research, interact effectively with the spectrum of healthcare providers and community members, and function efficiently within large research teams. Bryan-College Station Houston medicine professional masters More » about MD+MS program EnMed: Engineering Medicine (MD+ME) College of Medicine The new Engineering Medicine (EnMed) program allows students to obtain an engineering degree along with their medical degree in an integrated four-year course of study. Graduates will be well poised to develop new therapy paradigms and pharmaceuticals and solve a variety of health care problems. Houston medicine professional masters More » about our EnMed, MD+ME program MD + Education for Healthcare Professionals (MD+EDHP) College of Medicine The Master of Science in Education for Healthcare Professionals (EDHP) will provide the opportunity for individuals to develop the background and skills necessary to be excellent teachers in their areas of specialty and conduct research to improve and develop innovative educational programs in health professions education. Students who have an interest in academia are encouraged toward this program. Online medicine professional masters More » about our Professional + Master’s (MD+EDHP) program MD + Science and Technology Journalism (MD+STJR) College of Medicine The MS in Science and Technology Journalism (STJR) provides students with skills to communicate with various audiences about medicine, science, and technology, both through the mass media and by other means. Students also explore the broader contexts of such communication in order to best serve society. Much of the learning can aid in communicating in clinical settings.
