Office of Human Resources! The HR Team is committed to the University’s mission and to supporting a community of individuals who each play an important role to further the mission to advance knowledge, educate future leaders, and have a positive impact on the wider community. As one of the University’s most valuable resources, Princeton’s employees demonstrate daily that they are motivated and engaged by the challenging and stimulating work they do, no matter their role. We value and belong to a community where respect, teamwork, collaboration, diversity, and inclusion matter. HR is committed to preserving the confidentiality and privacy of information entrusted to its care. We hope staff, faculty, candidates, and prospective candidates find the information on this website about HR services, benefits, programs, time off, and other content informative and that it conveys what it’s like to work at Princeton! Ricciardi Assistant Vice President Talent Services Mission The Office of Human Resources forms strategic and proactive partnerships to recruit, develop, and retain a highly skilled, diverse, and engaged workforce. As a service delivery organization, we: Understand Princeton’s business operations Represent the interests of the University and its employees through fair, equitable, and compliant practices and decisions Produce high quality, effective, and efficient results Overview The Office of Human Resources provides comprehensive workplace services to Princeton University’s administrative and support staffs in the areas of benefits and work life; compensation; talent acquisition; administrative and engagement services; service recognition; diversity and inclusion; employee relations, labor relations, organizational development; learning and development; and human resources information systems. The office also serves Princeton’s faculty and research staff in the areas of benefits and work life, employment eligibility, background checks, and employment verifications; and collaborates and coordinates closely with Human Resources at the Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory. Romy Riddick serves as the vice president for Human Resources. For more information about HR, refer to our Contact Us page. Departmental Functions Office of the Vice President (OVP) In addition to leading HR’s various functions, the OVP provides continuous support and counsel to the University’s Cabinet. Vice President Sullivan-Crowley also serves on numerous University committees, including staffing the Trustee Committee on Compensation, Executive Compliance Committee, Benefits Committee, Housing Committee, and Enterprise Systems Planning Group. Benefits & Work Life Designs and administers medical, retirement, and work life programs that are comprehensive, competitive, fiscally responsible, and compliant with applicable state and federal regulations. Communications The Human Resources Communications Team serves as a bridge between the Office of Human Resources and Princeton University faculty and staff. Through the strategic use of print and digital publications, social media, and the HR website, the team connects employees with the University’s mission and brand, highlights human interest stories, and ensures that employees receive benefits and policy information. The HR Communications Team oversees employee appreciation efforts including the Service Recognition Luncheon and President's Achievement Award, and facilitates elections for staff representatives to the Council of the Princeton University Community (CPUC). Compensation Creates and administers compensation programs that are internally equitable and externally competitive, ensuring compliance with applicable state and federal regulations, and administering programs that support a pay-for-performance culture. Diversity & Inclusion Coordinates internally within HR and externally with administrative and academic departments to design programs, plans, and actionable steps that help foster a positive, inclusive, and productive work environment. Employee Relations, Labor Relations, & Organizational Development Responsible for building strong relationships throughout the University community while providing advice and counsel on workplace matters, including performance management and workforce engagement; management support; employee conflict resolution, including union and nonunion grievance handling, mediation, and arbitration; and union contract negotiations and labor-management relations. Human Resources Information Systems (HRIS) Administers HR information systems, which includes several enterprise software applications that manage critical data for Princeton’s employees, retirees, and job applicants, and provides advanced reporting and analytical support for HR and University management while acting as stewards to restricted and confidential data. Learning & Development Focuses on developing and enhancing the competencies and behaviors of managers and employees to support a motivated, knowledgeable, and engaged workforce; facilitates departmental retreats and other learning experiences in various departments. Princeton Writes Enhances the practical communication skills of the University community through classes, tutorials, assessments, and an interactive online presence, among other forms of outreach. Talent Acquisition Supports hiring managers throughout the recruitment process, helps employees seeking internal transfer or promotion, administers the University’s background check program for faculty and staff, manages staff relocation, and coordinates the orientation process for hiring departments and new employees. Assists all employees with employment eligibility verification (Form I-9), employment and income verifications, and searching and applying for jobs and promotional opportunities.
