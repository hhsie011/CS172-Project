There are numerous possibilities for you to gain a sense of what it's like to live as a Coyote. You can also achieve the type of experience you want by visiting campus in a variety of ways. Campus Tours Campus Tours are offered to prospective first-year, prospective transfer students and students who have been admitted. This visit will acquaint you with diverse aspects of the campus and the university. Group Tours Group Tours are offered to our 6th through 12th grade students, community college students, or other community organizations or non-school affiliated organizations. Self-Guided Tour Explore campus whenever you like with our beautiful 360 images of campus. Drag your cursor to move the image in a full circle. Use the arrows to choose a different area of campus! VIP Tours VIP Tours are reserved for campus partners only who wish to have prospective employees, special guests, or new employees receive a tour of the CSUSB San Bernardino campus. Coyote Ambassadors Our Coyote Ambassadors are an elite group of student leaders here at CSUSB. Learn how you can become part of our team! CSUSB Traditions and Landmarks Discover & learn how CSUSB has grown & evolved for 50-plus years, passing many milestones & forging traditions along the way. History of CSUSB CSUSB has a long history of academic success dating back to its foundation in 1965. Discover how our first three-building campus welcomed its first 293 students in 1965 and how we have evolved since! History of Cody the Coyote Learn more about the history of CSUSB's mascot, Cody the Coyote! Did you know, that our mascot could have been the St. Bernard? Visit San Bernardino Discover the beauty of suburban living and come and enjoy in our Southern California weather, adventure, hiking, biking, trails and wonderful entertainment. Accommodations Request Individuals with disabilities who need accommodations to attend our visits may contact a Campus Tours and Visitor Center representative by emailing tours@csusb.edu. It is requested that individuals requiring accommodations or auxiliary aids such as sign language interpreters and alternative format materials notify us at least 7 days prior to their visit. Every reasonable effort will be made to provide reasonable accommodations in an effective and timely manner.
