Print Degree Planner (opens a new window) | Print-Friendly Page (opens a new window) Facebook this Page (opens a new window) Tweet this Page (opens a new window) (120 units) This concentration prepares students for post-baccalaureate professional studies and eventually a career in their chosen field. Students who complete the Concentration in Social, Moral and Legal Philosophy for the Professions while earning their Bachelor of Arts in Philosophy should be equipped with the intellectual skills (i.e., critical reading and analysis skills) necessary to successfully apply to, enter and graduate from competitive professional graduate programs such as law, business and administration. Argumentation and Writing Requirement (3 units) PHIL 315 - Philosophical Argument and Writing (3) Pre-1900 History Sequence (9 units) PHIL 290 - History of Philosophy: Greek Philosophy (3) PHIL 300 - History of Philosophy: Rationalism and Empiricism (3) PHIL 301 - History of Philosophy: Kant and the 19th Century (3) Ethics, Social/Political Philosophy (6 units) PHIL 345 - Social and Political Philosophy (3) PHIL 355 - Philosophy of Law (3) PHIL 382 - Marx and Marxism (3) PHIL 410 - Ethical Theory (3) Applied Ethics, Social/Political Philosophy (3 units) PHIL 312 - Business and Professional Ethics (3) PHIL 313 - Environmental Ethics (3) PHIL 314 - Medical Ethics (3) PHIL 320 - Contemporary Moral Issues (3) PHIL 377 - Philosophical Approaches to Race, Class, and Gender (3) Core Topics in Philosophy (6 units) PHIL 303 - Introduction to the Philosophy of Science (3) PHIL 323 - Existentialism (3) PHIL 348 - Philosophy of Religion (3) PHIL 380 - Analytic Philosophy: 1900 to 1950 (3) PHIL 381 - The Analytic Tradition: 1950 to the Present (3) PHIL 383 - Postmodernism (3) PHIL 420 - Metaphysics (3) PHIL 430 - Epistemology (3) PHIL 440 - Philosophy of Mind (3) Additional (6 units) PHIL 405 - Advanced Topics in Applied Legal Reasoning (3) PHIL 493 - Senior Internship in Ethics and Society (3) Electives (6 units) Six units of philosophy courses that have not been used to fulfill other requirements. No more than six lower-division units may count toward electives in the major. Recommended Coursework for Philosophy Majors Students are advised to supplement their studies in philosophy with coursework offered in fields closely related to their philosophical interests. Graduate School Students who are planning to attend graduate school in philosophy are urged to acquire proficiency in a foreign language and include in their programs as many of the following as possible: PHIL 303 - Introduction to the Philosophy of Science (3) PHIL 345 - Social and Political Philosophy (3) PHIL 368 - First Course in Symbolic Logic (3) PHIL 375 - Meaning and Mind (3) PHIL 380 - Analytic Philosophy: 1900 to 1950 (3) PHIL 420 - Metaphysics (3) PHIL 430 - Epistemology (3) PHIL 440 - Philosophy of Mind (3) PHIL 455 - Seminar in Values (3) Law School Students interested in pursuing admission to law school are advised to include in their programs as many of the following as possible: PHIL 312 - Business and Professional Ethics (3) PHIL 313 - Environmental Ethics (3) PHIL 314 - Medical Ethics (3) PHIL 345 - Social and Political Philosophy (3) PHIL 355 - Philosophy of Law (3) PHIL 368 - First Course in Symbolic Logic (3) PHIL 410 - Ethical Theory (3) PHIL 455 - Seminar in Values (3) Total (120 units) Back to Top | Print Degree Planner (opens a new window) | Print-Friendly Page (opens a new window) Facebook this Page (opens a new window) Tweet this Page (opens a new window) All catalogs © 2022 California State University Fullerton.
