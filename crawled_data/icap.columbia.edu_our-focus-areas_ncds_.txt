Diseases Health Systems Human Resources for Health Quality Improvement Infection Prevention & Control Laboratory Strengthening Surveys NCDs The global burden of non-communicable diseases (NCDs), including diabetes, cardiovascular disease, cancers, chronic lung disease, and mental illness, is rising and health systems are adapting to these demographic and epidemiologic changes. Successful prevention, diagnosis, and management of chronic diseases require coordination of services over time and across disciplines and health systems designed to provide continuity of care. ICAP’s Focus on NCDs Applying lessons learned from HIV scale-up, ICAP is working with countries around the world to accelerate programs for a range of chronic health conditions, leveraging investments in HIV services to improve prevention, care, and treatment of NCDs.
