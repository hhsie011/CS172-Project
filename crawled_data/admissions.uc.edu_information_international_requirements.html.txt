We also provide assistance to high school counselors, international agents, and community members who support students from around the world. First-Year Students First-year applicants (sometimes referred to as freshman) are students who have completed high school and have not earned college course credit since graduating. How to Apply Transfer Students Transfer applicants are students who have earned college course credit after graduating high school/secondary school. You are welcome to transfer to UC at any point in time to continue your degree with us. How to Apply Accelerated College English (ACE) Program UC's Accelerated College English (ACE) Program is for students who need to strengthen English language proficiency during the first year by taking both English as a Second Language (ESL) courses and degree program courses. How to Apply Conditional Admission Conditional Admission is for students who meet academic coursework requirements to be admitted, but need to improve English language proficiency before beginning a degree program. How to Apply Other Student Types If you want to pursue a master's degree or higher, UC's Graduate School will help you through the application process. Get more information about Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) students at Cincinnati. Located just outside of the city of Cincinnati, UC's Blue Ash and Clermont campuses are a nice alternative to UC main campus. Learn more about the advantages of IB, AP, A-levels, GAC, and other Advanced Credit. Find short-term or non-degree programs like exchange student semesters. Sending Documents and Test Scores Send your documents in the mail, via email to your Admissions Counselor or via email to our general email account: international.admissions@uc.edu Our Mailing Address: University of Cincinnati International Admissions 2618 McMicken Circle University Pavilion, Suite 430 PO Box 210123 Cincinnati, OH 45221-0123 Official test scores must be sent to UC directly from the testing agency. You may select UC as a recipient of your scores by using the codes below when you register for an exam, or you may send scores directly from the testing agency's website. ACT (code: 3340) SAT (code: 1833) TOEFL (code: 1833) We also accept Duolingo (DET) and IELTS for proof of English scores for select majors. See requirements for first-year and transfer students above.
