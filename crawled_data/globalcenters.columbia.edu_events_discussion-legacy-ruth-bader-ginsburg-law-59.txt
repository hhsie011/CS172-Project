Bader Ginsburg (LAW ’59) Events Past Event A Discussion on the Legacy of Ruth Bader Ginsburg (LAW ’59) Share Add to Calendar: Google Yahoo December 18, 2020 4:30 PM This is a virtual event A Discussion on the Legacy of Ruth Bader Ginsburg (LAW ’59) The Columbia University Club of France wishes to pay tribute to the late Justice Ginsburg, who was the second woman to serve on the U.S. Supreme Court and the first female tenured professor at Columbia Law School. Taking a Franco-American perspective, the panel will explore some of the themes that were of interest to Justice Ginsburg as part of her work on the Supreme Court and in academia. A discussion between: Noëlle Lenoir, first woman to serve on the Conseil constitutionnel of France (1992-2001), former French Minister for European Affairs (2002-2004) and Visiting Professor at Columbia Law School (2001-2002) and Jane C. Ginsburg, Morton L. Janklow Professor of Literary & Artistic Property Law, Columbia Law School Moderated by: Jan Dunin-Wasowicz (12LAW), member of the Paris, New York & District of Columbia Bars, associate at Hughes Hubbard & Reed LLP, Paris Upcoming Events Columbia Global CentersAmman / Beijing / Istanbul / Mumbai / Nairobi / Paris / Rio / Santiago / Tunis Follow Us Facebook Twitter Instagram Youtube Columbia University ©2022 Columbia University Accessibility Nondiscrimination Careers Built using Columbia Sites Back to Top Close
