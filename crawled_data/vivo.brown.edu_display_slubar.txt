Previously: Director of the John Nicholas Brown Center for Public Humanities and Cultural Heritage, 2004-2014. Director of the Haffenreffer Museum of Anthropology, 2010-2012. Before coming to Brown, Chair of the Division of the History of Technology at the Smithsonian's National Museum of American History. Guggenheim Fellow, 2015. Author or co-author of Inside the Lost Museum: Curating, Past and Present; Legacies: Collecting America's History at the Smithsonian; InfoCulture: the Smithsonian Book of Information Age Inventions; History from Things and Engines of Change: The American Industrial Revolution, and many articles on museums and on the history of technology. Exhibits at the Smithsonian include "America on the Move," "Smithsonian's America," and "Engines of Change." Exhibits at Brown include oversight of student exhibitions at the public humanities center, the Haffenreffer Museum, and the John Hay Library. Present interests include the history of museums and memorials, material culture studies, public humanities, and the history of skill. Students learn to build a traditional boat. AMST2650, a seminar designed to introduce new masters students in Public Humanities to the public humanities, including issues of history and memory, culture and community, and presentation and representation. AMST25400, a practical introduction to the work of museum curators and others who conserve and interpret culture.. AMST1903, Museum Histories, an overview of the history of museums AMST2661, Visualizations in the Humanities: From the Cabinet of Curiosities to the Geoparser consdered the ways that digital tools can be used to visualize humanities data, from timelines, graphs, and maps to topic modelling AMST1510, Museum Collecting and Collections, offered students an overview of the way that museums collect and use their collections, and three in-depth case studies of collecting at Brown: the Annmary Brown Memorial, the stamp collections, and scientific instrument collections. I also teach more specialized courses on museum exhibition and collecting, digital humanities, the history of museums, and the history of technology, as well as individual reading and project courses.
