Bus routes run both on- and off-campus throughout the fall and spring semesters and on football gamedays. Bus routes and service times vary depending on the day of the week and the time of the year. Aggie Spirit bus Brazos Transit District Students, faculty and staff possessing a Texas A&M University or Blinn College ID may ride the Brazos Transit District buses operating throughout town for free. Riders must be prepared to show their ID to the driver when boarding at off-campus stops. Brazos Transit District Car Share & Rideshare car share Zipcar Zipcar lets you drive a variety of cars on demand, by the hour or the day, around campuses, cities and airports worldwide. Zipcar rideshare Rideshare Solutions The rideshare program hosted by Transportation Services allows users to give a ride or get a ride to help share the cost of commuting. Users can find a carpool or bus that matches their commute and search for other nearby commuters. Rideshare Solutions rideshare Non-affiliated Providers Multiple non-affiliated public car share and rideshare providers operate in the community. Keep these in mind when considerting transportation options around town. Park & Ride Bikes bike lease Transportation Services Leasing a bicycle through the Texas A&M Sustainable Transportation (ST) office will allow you to save time and money. The ST office offers durable bikes for your transportation needs in and around the Bryan-College Station community. All registered students are able to lease bicycles on a semester long basis. Bike Lease private bike ownership Bike Ownership Transportation Services offers a comprehensive bicycle program designed to meet the varied needs of students, staff and faculty at Texas A&M. All personal bicycles on campus must be registered with Transportation Services and display a permit. Visit the link to find out where bike lanes, racks and maintenance stations are located throughout campus to serve your biking needs.
