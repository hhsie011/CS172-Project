Yin and restorative yoga poses will stretch the body gently. You might want to bring pillows, blankets, or rolled towels for the sessions. Hosted by: TTU Libraries Zoom Registration Link Credible by Caity-Shea Violette: Centering on Chronic Pain & Gender Biases in the Medical World March 10-12th 7:30 PM Free Event Living at the intersection of the opioid epidemic and #MeToo movement, credible is a one-woman show that follows the journey of a woman relearning how to live in her body after years of mysterious chronic pain, complex trauma, and over prescription. Credible intertwines humor and vulnerability to chronicle a decade-long discovery process from the inside out, illuminating the impact of gender bias in the medical system and emphasizing the life-saving power of having your pain seen and believed. Panel members include faculty, staff, and students from the Student Counseling Center, the Psychological Services Clinic, the Department of Psychological Sciences, the Staff Senate, the Office of LGBTQIA Education & Engagement, and RISE. The goal of this panel is to share advice from faculty, staff, and students and connect members of our campus community with resources to benefit their mental health and well-being.
