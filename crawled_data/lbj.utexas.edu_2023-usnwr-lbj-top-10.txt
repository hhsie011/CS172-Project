Seven LBJ programs are top ranked, with three in the top 10: Overall (#10), Environmental Policy & Management (#9) and International/Global Policy & Administration (#9). Two other specialties, Public Policy Analysis (#15) and Social Policy (#18), made gains as well. LBJ also ranked in Public Management & Leadership (#21), and held steady in Nonprofit Management (#23). U.S. News & World Report graduate rankings are among the most widely regarded ratings in higher education. RANKINGS 2023 Overall: #10 Environmental Policy & Management: #9 International/Global Policy & Administration: #9 Public Policy Analysis: #15 Social Policy: #18 Public Management & Leadership: #21 Nonprofit Management: #23 "I'm proud that the LBJ School remains in the top 10. Most of all, I'm proud of our amazing faculty, students, alumni and staff." —LBJ School Dean JR DeShazo The rankings of public affairs programs are based solely on peer assessment survey results. In fall 2021 and early 2022, U.S. News surveyed deans, directors and department chairs representing 270 master's programs in public affairs and administration. The lists of schools, individuals surveyed and specialty areas evaluated were provided by the Network of Schools of Public Policy, Affairs, and Administration (NASPAA) and by the Association for Public Policy Analysis and Management (APPAM).
