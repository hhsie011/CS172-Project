Public Law 111-32, the Marine Gunnery Sergeant John David Fry Scholarship, amends the Post-9/11 GI Bill® (chapter 33) to include the children of service members who died in the line of duty after Sept. 10, 2001. Using VA benefits for the first time: Complete the online application VA form 22-5490: www.vets.gov/education/ Submit Certificate of Eligibility to the VA Benefits Office in the Office of the Registrar. Complete and provide a Request for Certification Form to the VA Benefits Office after you have registered for the semester. Using VA benefits for the first time at Baylor University: (If you previously used VA benefits at another school) Complete the online Request for Change of Program or Place of Training Form (22-5495) if transferring to another institution and not changing benefits: www.vets.gov/education/ Submit copy of Form 22-5495 AND Certificate of Eligibility to the VA Benefits Office in the Office of the Registrar Complete and provide a Request for Certification Form to the VA Benefits Office after you have registered for the semester. Benefit Payments: Under the John Fry Scholarship, students receive the same benefits as a student under Chapter 33 Post 9/11 GI Bill® at 100%. Students using the John Fry Scholarship are eligible for the Yellow Ribbon Program beginning August 1, 2018. For more information about this benefit, visit https://www.va.gov/education/survivor-dependent-benefits/fry-scholarship/.
