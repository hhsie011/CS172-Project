Jan Švankmajer, Czechoslovakia, 1988) Introduced by Dr. Aga Skrodzka Open Houses CAAH will be hosting both in-person and virtual open houses starting in the fall. Information on how to register will be posted here as dates are finalized. Students who have already applied to Clemson will automatically be invited to our Open House events via their Clemson Slate accounts. We can’t wait to see you! In the meantime, to learn more about CAAH majors and to schedule virtual visits with faculty or students in your desired major, please email visitCAAH@clemson.edu. Departmental Visits Prospective students who would like to learn more about a particular major are invited to email VisitCAAH@clemson.edu to discuss available options. Currently, we do not offer in-person visits, but you can still chat remotely with a professor or one of our undergraduate Ambassadors. We’ll be happy to set that up for you! Please note that the Architecture department schedules their visits separately. See the next tab for more information. School of Architecture Undergraduate Visits To learn about any upcoming virtual visits or events for students interested in the study of architecture, please contact Gabby Lollis, Student Services Coordinator, at 864-656-3938 or glollis@clemson.edu Graduate Student Visits To learn about any upcoming virtual visits or events for a graduate program, please call or email the program contact on our departments and degrees page. Students specifically interested in City Planning and Real Estate Development or Historic Preservation should contact the following individuals: For City and Regional Planning, contact the program director, Jackie Galbreath. For the Master of Science in Historic Preservation, contact the program director, Amanda Tucker. This is a joint program with Clemson and the College of Charleston. Students take their classes at the Clemson Design Center in Charleston, which is located on Meeting Street. For the Master of Real Estate Development, contact the program coordinator, Amy Matthews, 864-656-4257. General Campus Tours Clemson University tours are run and scheduled through The Visitors Center. To learn more about virtual options, please visit the Visitors Center web page.
