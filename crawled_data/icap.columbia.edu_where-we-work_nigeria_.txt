RISE is implemented by a Jhpiego-led consortium of local partners, including ICAP. RISE in Nigeria focuses on five key strategic objectives: To optimize the efficiency and yield of HIV testing services in four states in Nigeria, including index testing and self-testing services To strengthen linkage and retention in care including same-day initiation of ART, transition to TLD and differentiated service delivery models To optimize efficient and timely laboratory services including rapid turnaround time for sample analysis and return of results To support robust monitoring, evaluation, and learning To contribute to health system strengthening, coordination and quality improvement Tracking with Recency Assays to Control the Epidemic (TRACE) Project Details With support from PEPFAR through the CDC, ICAP is supporting the roll-out of rapid HIV recency testing that promises to advance efforts to characterize where and among whom new HIV Project Details With support from PEPFAR through the CDC, ICAP is supporting the roll-out of rapid HIV recency testing that promises to advance efforts to characterize where and among whom new HIV infections are occurring, and to inform prevention efforts to curb ongoing HIV transmission. Trainings, supportive supervision, and continuous quality improvement during health information system development will enable the Ministry of Health to capture data and conduct epidemiological analysis and characterization to support robust surveillance and targeted interventions for recent HIV infections. He joined ICAP in 2017 as the Technical Director for ICAP in Sierra Leone, where he oversaw a complex health system strengthening portfolio that consisted of projects focused on immunization, malaria, HIV/AIDS, QI, M&E, D4D, and infection prevention and control. Prior to this role, Dr. Akinjeji was a technical officer in health systems strengthening at the World Health Organization (WHO) in Sierra Leone. In Nigeria, he has worked in various capacities at the Clinton Health Access Initiative (CHAI) and the Institute of Human Virology Nigeria (IHVN). Dr. Akinjeji graduated from University of Ibadan Medical School and he also holds an MBA in International Health Care Management from the University of Aberdeen, UK.
