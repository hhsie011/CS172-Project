Sub Navigation In This Section Recent Events Series: The Early Modern World in U.S. Libraries and Collections 2020–21 Events 2019-20 Events 2020–21 Events Productive and fulfilling, the 2020–21 academic year featured several lectures, many exploring new media and formats, and a new series, "The Early Modern World in United States Library and Collections." The program began with a lecture by Connie Scozzaro entitled "Love Is the Drug: Sex, Pharmacology, and Early Modern Feminism" followed on October 7 by a talk from Nick Jones of Bucknell University: "Staging 'Habla de Negros' in the Iberian Early Modern." A special series of presentations aimed at graduate students and early career scholars began in 2021: The Early Modern World in United States Libraries and Collections: Orientations and Opportunities. This has featured rich and informative presentations from the Folger, the Newberry Library, and the Benson Latin American Collection at UT-Austin.
