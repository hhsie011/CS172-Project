Home to the state’s first entrepreneurship program. Holds dual accreditation, through AACSB International, in business and accounting—placing it in the top 5 percent of business schools worldwide to hold dual AACSB accreditation. Soon to break ground on Woolsey Hall, a brand-new $50 million home. Exclusive study-abroad connections in countries like Austria, France, Germany, Sweden, China, Spain and more. Barton School internships and co-op placements range from Microsoft and Koch Industries to the Mayo Clinic and the White House. Students of the Barton International Group create innovative business strategies for companies at home and abroad in locations such as Silicon Valley, Malaysia and Taiwan. Students receive more than half a million dollars in annual scholarships, including the prestigious $48,000 Clay Barton Scholarship, two $25,000 Fran Jabara entrepreneurship scholarships, and Koch scholarships valued from $30,000-$60,000.
