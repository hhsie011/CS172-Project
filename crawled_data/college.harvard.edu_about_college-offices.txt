Helpful Links FAQs Applicant Portal Events Contact Us my.harvard Breadcrumb About College Offices On this page Overview Academics Student Life Overview Enriching your College Experience The staff of Harvard's campus offices ensure you always have somewhere to turn for help navigating college life. Improve your study skills, take the first step on the path to career success, or enliven your day with an infusion of arts and culture. Academics From small, focused seminars to hands-on lab experiences to field-based research, unparalleled academic opportunities open students’ eyes to new modes of thinking and allow them to engage deeply with topics they’re passionate about. The common thread in the community is never-ending curiosity. Academic Offices Office of Undergraduate Education The Office of Undergraduate Education focuses on curricular planning and the implementation of new Faculty-led programs for undergraduates. Academic Resource Center (ARC) Accessible Education Office Advising Programs Office Freshman Seminar Program General Education Lemann Program on Creativity and Entrepreneurship Mindich Program in Engaged Scholarship Office of Career Services Office of International Education Office of Undergraduate Research and Fellowships Writing Program Office for the Arts The Office for the Arts supports student engagement in the arts and integrates the arts into university life. They help administer undergraduate arts courses, manage performance venues on campus, and stage events and activities that engage Harvard and our surrounding community. FAS Registrar's Office The Registrar's Office coordinates course registration and exam schedules. They can provide assistance if you need help signing up for a course, completing course-related forms, or resolving scheduling conflicts. Academic Integrity and Student Conduct Academic Integrity is the foundation of the academic experience at Harvard College. Students at Harvard learn how to acknowledge when their work is indebted to others’, and about the value of producing original scholarship. The Handbook for Students outlines expectations for student conduct. Griffin Financial Aid Office The Griffin Financial Aid Office works closely with Harvard families to ensure that their education is affordable. Our need-based financial aid program meets the financial need of each student. Learn more about the Griffin Financial Aid Office Student Life Students are introduced to the College community through unique programming that helps them establish a strong foundation, build meaningful connections, as well as engage in the living and learning community. From student organizations, athletic teams, and the residential experience: students develop a strong sense of belonging at Harvard. Student Life Offices Dean of Students Office The Dean of Students Office (DSO) helps shape the academic, residential, and co-curricular dimensions of students’ lives. The DSO encompasses several programs and offices that range from student housing to BGLTQ support: Harvard College Title IX The Harvard Foundation Housing and Residential Life Office of BGLTQ Student Life Office of First-Year Experience Office of Parent and Family Engagement Office of Student Engagement Women's Center Phillips Brooks House Center for Public Service & Engaged Scholarship The Phillips Brooks House Center for Public Service & Engaged Scholarship is the hub that provides support for all public service efforts at Harvard College. It is home to the following programs, and collaborates closely with the Institute of Politics (IOP)and Office of Career Services (OCS) to provide students with a rich variety of volunteer, internship, coursework, and postgraduate opportunities in public service. Phillips Brooks House Association Center for Public Interest Careers Mindich Program in Engaged Scholarship Groups Peer Education Peer education groups help to extend health and wellness education beyond the walls of University Health Services by promoting health & wellbeing to their fellow classmates.
