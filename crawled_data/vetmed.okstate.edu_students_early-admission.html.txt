Students accepted to the program agree to complete their bachelor’s degree with the pre-veterinary required courses and maintain a GPA of 3.5 each semester of their undergraduate education. Eligibility Applicants must be enrolled as full-time students in a four-year comprehensive university in the state of Oklahoma, regardless of residency status. Eligible applicants must have achieved an ACT score of at least 28 (SAT 1300 without writing portion). Applicants must be able to demonstrate strong interest in veterinary medicine, effective leadership and communication skills, emotional maturity, and other non-technical skills which reflect knowledge and aptitude necessary for successful practice of veterinary medicine. Qualified applicants will participate in interviews as part of the selection process. Applying Applicants apply as entering college freshmen (students who graduated high school in May.) Complete the online application. Upload your high school transcript and official college transcript showing any concurrent courses. Two letters of reference are also required. Letters of reference must be sent directly from the evaluator either by mail to the below address or email directly to dvm@okstate.edu. Oklahoma State University College of Veterinary Medicine Student Services Office - Re: Early Admission 110 McElroy Hall Stillwater, OK 74078-2003. The Early Admission application is closed. Check back here August 1, 2022 to apply for the next cycle. For more information or questions, contact the Student Services Office at 405-744-6961 or dvm@okstate.edu.
