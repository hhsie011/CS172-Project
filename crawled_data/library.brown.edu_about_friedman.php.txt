Friedman Study Center at the Sciences Library is a central gathering place for students with more than 27,000 square feet of study and social space. Level A, staffed by expert library and technology consultants, is dedicated almost entirely to study spaces — both collaborative and individual. In addition to 65+ computer workstations, Level A includes 8 private group study rooms and an assistive technology room. The entire area has wireless internet access and comfortable furniture. There is a cafe in the Lobby which extends out onto the south terrace. Study spaces are located on Level A and the Mezzanine. The Quiet Center on Level 4 is currently closed.
