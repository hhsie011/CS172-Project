It does this by facilitating collaboration between organizations offering student support and increasing campus awareness of these programs. The five points of the star are food, finances, clothing, housing, and connections. If you are a student in need or know someone who needs assistance, here are a list of ways we can help: Campus Closet Klemis Kitchen Housing Financial Assistance Campus Closet Campus Closet lends appropriate clothing to those who are prospective hires without proper business attire. Website: campuscloset.gatech.edu Main contact: gtcampuscloset@gatech.edu Klemis Kitchen Klemis Kitchen serves as a food pantry for Georgia Tech students with dietary needs and financial concerns that limit their access to proper nourishment. As of February 2016, the functioning and volunteer organization of Klemis Kitchen has been absorbed by Campus Kitchen. Social media: www.facebook.com/klemiskitchen Campus Kitchen: www.facebook.com/CampusKitchens.GT/ Main contact: Steve Fazenbaker, steve.fazenbaker@gatech.edu Housing Georgia Tech offers temporary housing to students who become unexpectedly displaced or put under financial stress. If you are in need of housing assistance, complete the Crisis Housing Petition or for more information, contact Steve Fazenbaker, steve.fazenbaker@gatech.edu Dean Griffin Hip Pocket Fund The Dean Griffin Hip Pocket Fund offers interest-free emergency loans to students for academic or personal matters. For more information or assistance, contact the Vice President and Dean of Student's office at studentlife@studentlife.gatech.edu How to Donate Please consider supporting the STAR Program today. Your gift will go directly to helping students in need through our multifaceted STAR Program. Follow the links on the right to make an impact on Georgia Tech’s student in need. Thank you for your donation to the STAR program!
