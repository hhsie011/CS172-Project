The minor requirements are as follows: At least 18 credits, chosen as follows: 1. Required introductory course: EAS 2250 The Earth System 2. At least three selections from the following core course options: EAS 3050 Climate Dynamics EAS 3410 Atmospheric Thermodynamics and Hydrostatistics EAS 3420 Atmospheric Dynamics EAS 3090 Earth Materials EAS 3880 Global Geophysics EAS 3010 Evolution of the Earth System EAS 3030 Biogeochemistry EAS 3450 Environmental Geophysics EAS 3530 Physical Oceanography 3. Additional EAS courses at the 3000-level or higher. These courses may include, but are not limited to, additional courses from the above list, undergraduate research courses, and outdoor field courses. Academic Standards At least C- in each course taken towards the minor and an average GPA > 2.0 in all courses taken towards the minor.
