Events & Guest Services Meet the Team Main Content Region The Office of Corporate and Foundation Philanthropy Office of Corporate and Foundation Philanthropy (OCFP) California State University, San Bernardino (CSUSB) fosters a climate in which corporate and foundation partnerships result in long-term philanthropic gains and advance university priorities and programs while enhancing corporate and foundation visibility and community impact. The CSUSB Office of Corporate and Foundation Philanthropy (OCFP) can identify campus-wide potential partnership opportunities that are in alignment with your vision of giving and corporate social responsibility. Committed to building a stronger community and region, CSUSB invites you to explore a partnership with the university to address your business needs, drive forward positive change and give back to the region. Our Partners Entrepreneurship center awarded $500,000 to support Inland Empire BIPOC small business owners and entrepreneurs The funding will go toward supporting ongoing work in providing technical assistance to BIPOC-led small businesses that have been disproportionately affected by the unprecedented health, social and economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic. San Manuel Band of Mission Indians awards CSUSB with $25K to support logistics program The grant will be used for CSUSB’s Pathways to Logistics program, which prepares high school students in San Bernardino and Riverside counties for careers in managerial and professional positions in the logistics industry. CSUSB receives $120,000 grant for Crankstart Re-Entry Scholarship Program The Crankstart Foundation gifted CSUSB $120,000 in support of the Crankstart Re-entry Scholarship Program CSUSB receives $120,000 grant to support Undocumented Student Success Center The funds will be used exclusively to support the implementation and enhancement of programming and services for undocumented students and their families CSUSB Palm Desert Campus receives $20,000 grant for neurofeedback program CSUSB’s Neurofeedback Center will partner with school districts in the Coachella Valley to provide innovative mental health services to children whose mental well-being has been adversely affected by the global pandemic. CSUSB receives $960K grant to increase Native American student enrollment The San Manuel Band of Mission Indians has awarded CSUSB to increase the college-going rates and success of Native American students. The Importance of Private Support Private support plays a vital role in advancing the university’s mission of teaching and service. Partnerships with corporations and foundations ensure that Cal State San Bernardino continues to recruit and train stellar faculty, educate the next generation of leaders and impacts the community through innovation and high quality educational programs.
