Analytics Minor Print-Friendly Page (opens a new window) Facebook this Page (opens a new window) Tweet this Page (opens a new window) Return to: The Collins College of Business Director John Polonchek The business analytics minor is designed for business students interested in complementing their major with understanding and application of data management and business-analytic techniques. The proliferation of data affects business professionals in all areas of the organization, and the need for data-driven decision making and the insight gained from data analysis has never been more critical, whether used to inform high-level strategy or to improve day-to-day operations. Students must fulfill the prerequisites and complete the 15 hours with a minimum grade point average of 2.0. Required Courses (12 hours) CIS 2013 Business Programming Concepts I CIS 4043 Database Design and Applications QM 5003 Business Decision Models QM 5063 Data Mining and Predictive Analytics Elective Courses (3 hours) One course chosen from the following list. It is recommended that the elective course be chosen from areas outside of the student’s major.
