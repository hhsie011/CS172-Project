Check-Out Timeline Check-Out dates are published on the Housing calendar. Extensions are not permitted. Fees are assessed for late Check-Out. Steps to Check-Out Complete online maintenance requests to resolve issues in your room. Pack and remove all personal belongings from your room, kitchen, and common areas: double-check drawers, closets, and corners carefully. Items left behind in the room will be discarded. Do not leave personal belongings in the hallway unattended. Remove all trash to designated locations (locations provided below.) Trash bags are available from your RA if you need them. Trash chutes are closed starting May 2 so please bring your garbage to the designated areas. Clean and sweep/vacuum your room, suite, kitchens, and common areas, where applicable. Remove message boards, door tags, posters, and tape from your door(s) and walls. Close and lock your door and windows. Use Web Check-Out in the Housing Portal. Notify your RA that you have checked out. If you have any questions, consult the Check-Out procedures outlined in the Guide to Living or contact the Hospitality Desk by email at [email protected] or phone at 212-854-2779. Guests Each student is allowed to have two guests to assist with their Check-Out. Guests must comply with the University's vaccination policy and should be prepared to show vaccine documentation. Storage and Shipping If you need help packing, and/or need to have your belongings stored for the summer months or shipped to you, please refer to the storage page in the Guide to Living. Special Access During Check-Out To facilitate Check-Out, the East Campus B3 Level Entrance and Amsterdam Avenue via the Hartley-Wallach Gate will be accessible to residents. Dates and times that these entrances will be open will be posted here when available. Bin Loans Bins will be available from various locations to assist with moving. Bins may be borrowed for 2 hours at a time and should be returned to the location from which they were rented.
