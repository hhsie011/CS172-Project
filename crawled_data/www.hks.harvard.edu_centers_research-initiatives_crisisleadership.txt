About Us Our Program People Contact Us Search Program on Crisis Leadership Ash Center Taubman Center In This Section Centers & Initiatives Program on Crisis Leadership Publications Projects Education Events About Us You are here Home Program on Crisis Leadership Through research, teaching, and work with governments and practicing professionals, the Program on Crisis Leadership (PCL) seeks to improve society’s capacity to deal with natural disasters; infrastructure, technology, and systems failures; emergent infectious disease; and terrorism. Jointly affiliated with the Ash Center for Democratic Governance and Innovation and the Taubman Center for State and Local Government, PCL takes a comprehensive perspective, looking at risk reduction strategies, emergency preparedness and response, and disaster recovery. Featured Research and Teaching Organizational Leadership and Covid-19 PCL researchers offer insights on crisis management fundamentals for leaders responding to the COVID-19 pandemic (Photo source) Observations from the Gulf Coast PCL Senior Fellow Doug Ahlers explores why people choose to remain in disaster-vulnerable communities. The National Guard and Homeland Security PCL partners with the National Guard on training programs for large-scale, multi-organizational response operations.
