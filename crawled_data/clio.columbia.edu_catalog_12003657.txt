Title Wolf Dance: The Transformation of the Eagles into Wolves (recto); An Editor's Sanctum, from Charley and Eva's "Home in the West," published by Lee & Shepard, Boston (verso). Produced Alaska, United States, 1890s. Physical Properties 1 Drawing ; 6 3/16 x 9 3/8 in. (15.7 x 23.7 cm) Pencil, brown and red ink, and wash on paper (recto). Description Four women stand behind male dancers as they transform from eagles into wolves. Notes This drawing is part of a set made by the Inupiat people to document their ritual traditions. They likely were commissioned by missionaries, and the drawings were made on the backs of prints derived from children's books and educational manuals. Exhibitions Messages from Across Time and Space: Inupiat Drawings from the 1890s at Columbia University (Center for the Study of Ethnicity and Race, Hamilton Hall, September 22-November 20, 2015); Skin, Sand, Paint and Metal: A Visual Exploration of Cross-Cultural Encounters in North America (Center Gallery, Bucknell Univ., Lewisburg, PA, August 29-November 1, 1992) Provenance Acquired by Wendell Ter Bush (1867-1941), professor of philosophy; gift to Columbia, Dept. of Religions, from Prof. Bush ca. 1940. Credit Line The Bush Collection of Religion and Culture. Subjects (Genre) Drawings (visual works) > Inupiaq > 19th century. Figures (representations) > Inupiaq > 19th century.
