Events Past Event On the Margins of American Cinema Share Add to Calendar: Google Yahoo January 6, 2016 - January 12, 2016 6:30 PM - 9:30 PM Columbia Global Centers | Beijing Please save the date for one of the coolest ways to ring in the New Year of 2016 with Professor Richard Peña from Columbia University School of the Arts. Professor Peña had served as the Director of the New York Film Festival for 25 years from 1988 to 2012, and he is still its Director Emeritus. He is a Visiting professor in Film Studies at Harvard University this year. Columbia Global Centers | Beijing will host in collaboration with Columbia University School of the Arts a series of events themed on “On the Margins of American Cinema” in which Professor Peña will share his expert insights on the American cinema, cutting-edge film theories and the current international film business from Jan. 6 to Jan. 14, 2016. Please click here to see more information as it becomes available. Agenda: Jan. 6, 6:30pm-8:30pm International Film Business with Alibaba Pictures Group Ltd. Jan. 7, 6:00pm-8:00pm Sensual Cinema --- From Cinema of Narration to Cinema of Physical Sensations, at the lecture hall on the 2nd floor of Animation School, Communication University of China (North Gate). Jan. 8, 9:30am-11:30am Seminar: The Return of Latin American Cinema: 1996 - 2016 with Institute of Latin American Studies (ILAS), China Academy of Social Sciences Jan. 9, 2:00pm-5:00pm Salt of the Earth (1954) --- The Impact of Italian Neorealism in The American Cinema with Ullens Center for Contemporary Art Jan. 11, 2:30pm-4:30pm Seminar with Tsinghua University Jan. 11, 6:30pm-8:30pm The Beginning of Experimental Film in the United States with Beijing American Center.
