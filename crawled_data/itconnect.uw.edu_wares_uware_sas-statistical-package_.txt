& Hardware Software for the UW (UWare) SAS SAS The SAS System is a comprehensive statistical software package from SAS Institute for data management, graphics, analysis, and presentation. How to Get SAS Students Students have two options for accessing SAS software for free: SAS OnDemand for Academics, which allows access to SAS Software through a web browser UW students who pay the Student Technology Fee are eligible for a free account on the Center for Studies in Demography and Ecology (CSDE) SAS Server, accessible through a remote Windows terminal server connection. For more information and to apply for an account go to CSDE. Faculty and Staff Faculty and staff may use SAS OnDemand for Academics for free, which allows access to SAS Software through a web browser. Licenses for SAS Workstation for Windows are available for purchase by Faculty and Staff Purchase requires a valid UW budget number and authorization from a budget approver. No Procurement cards or direct sales to individuals are available. SAS Workstation for Windows is available through an annual subscription, with the license period running from July 1 through June 30. UW-IT cannot prorate or refund the cost of the SAS Workstation for Windows license subscription UW-IT will automatically bill for the SAS Workstation for Windows license in the next license cycle. Order SAS Workstation for Windows License Details SAS Workstation for Windows Licenses are assigned to an individual and may be installed on up to two devices. A SAS Workstation for Windows license can not be assigned to a shared UW NetID. Once purchased, you will be automatically billed for your SAS Workstation for Windows license on an annual basis unless you opt out of the renewal before the opt-out deadline. For more information about the opt-out deadline or to submit an opt-out request form, visit: https://itconnect.uw.edu/wares/uware/uw-software-renewal-opt-out-request/. The SAS license purchased via UW IT Software Licensing service may be used for academic, non-commercial uses only. Any other use is expressly prohibited.
