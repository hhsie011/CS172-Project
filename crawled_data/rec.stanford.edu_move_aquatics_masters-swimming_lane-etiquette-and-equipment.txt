Dues Lane Etiquette and Equipment Announcements Resources Swim Away from Home Main content start Lane Etiquette Masters swimming can be great fun and exercise, and here are some tips to help make the most of this wonderful experience: Swim in a lane where you can make the intervals Do the prescribed set, unless you warn others Keep paddle-wearing to a minimum on non-pull sets Arrive early for morning workout to help set-up the pool Help pull lane lines and/or covers after workout when necessary Put away your kickboard and pull bouy Be conscious of those swimming in front of and/or behind you Equipment Suggested equipment for workouts: Swim suit Goggles Towel Swim cap (if you wear one) Kickboard (provided at workout) Pull buoy (provided at workout) Training fins / "zoomers" (NOT dive fins - workout fins should extend no more than 4 inches past your toes). Our current favorites are Hydro Tech 2 fins. Hand paddles (only if your shoulders can handle them). Strokemaker brand paddles are great.
