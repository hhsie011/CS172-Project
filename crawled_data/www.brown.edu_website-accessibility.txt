In support of this objective, the University has policies, procedures and practices related to digital accessibility. The University aims to ensure the information on its websites is available to the broadest possible audience, meeting the standards in the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. If you encounter web content that you are unable to access due to a web accessibility issue, please contact Student and Employee Accessibility Services (SEAS) to report the issue. Web Accessibility Policy This University policy establishes accessibility standards to ensure access to Brown’s web and online content, regardless of an individual’s physical or developmental abilities. Web Accessibility Training New and existing online content created by Brown’s faculty, staff and student employees must comply with web accessibility standards. All website content editors are required to watch a training video. WAVE (Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool) WAVE is a free, third-party tool that website content editors can use to evaluate the accessibility of webpages. Take the Training All web content editors are required to watch this training video.
