US-China Economic interdependence after COVID-19 Share Add to Calendar: Google Yahoo May 14, 2020 7:00 PM - 8:00 PM Columbia Global Centers | Beijing cordially invites you to an online panel discussion about US-China Economic interdependence after COVID-19. This event will happen in Eastern Standard Time. Points to be covered: Economic forces before COVID-19 and future directions The impact of COVID-19 on the US-China geopolitical relationship The evolution of the supply chains and global technology collaboration over time in the context of US-China interdependence Panelists Wei Jiang Arthur F. Burns Professor of Free and Competitive Enterprise, Columbia Business School Avril Haines Deputy Director, Columbia World Projects and former Assistant to the President, Principal Deputy National Security Advisor, and Deputy Director of the CIA Christopher Thomas Global Executive, former partner of McKinsey & Company and former General Manager of Intel China View the event video here.
