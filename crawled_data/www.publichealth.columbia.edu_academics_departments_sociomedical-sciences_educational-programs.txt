Educational Programs Sociomedical Sciences sets public health challenges in broad social, historical, and political contexts for students in MPH, MS, and doctoral programs. Our programs study social forces' impact on human health to further social justice and alleviate health discrepancies. Master of Public Health MPH students apply social science theories and methods to current public health issues. Find out about degree requirements, course descriptions, certificates, practicum information, financial aid, and other resources. Accelerated MPH The Accelerated MPH is an intensive, one-year program designed for highly motivated professionals seeking to enhance their career with a degree in public health. Doctoral Programs At the heart of Sociomedical Sciences’ intellectual community, our doctoral programs offer cutting-edge training. Master of Science The thirty-credit MS in Sociomedical Sciences degree trains students to apply social science theories, concepts, and methods to public health practice and policymaking.
