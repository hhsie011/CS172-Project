Here are some resources and guidelines to help you get the most out of your remote learning, Zoom-driven experience. Aside from attending classes, here are other ways you can utilize Zoom: Group projects and virtual meetings with classmates Attending online office hours Virtually meeting with your advisor Tutoring sessions Staying Secure With the increasing use of Zoom for remote teaching and learning comes the advent of trends such as "Zoombombing," where an individual joins a Zoom meeting with the sole intent of disruption. To help prevent this from happening in your meetings, check out our Zoom security tips and recommendations for how to properly configure your meetings, set up a waiting room and mute or block unwanted participants. Zoom Security Getting Started Create your Zoom account Visit zoom.oregonstate.edu Click "Login to Zoom" Complete OSU single sign-on/DUO process Once you've successfully signed in, your Zoom account is automatically established Download and run Zoom on your computer Visit zoom.us/download Download and Run the "Zoom Client for Meetings" Upon opening Zoom, select "Sign in with SSO" Type "oregonstate" for the company domain Click continue and a webpage with the OSU sign-in will appear Sign in and select "Open zoom.us" from the pop-up window Download and run Zoom on your mobile device Install the app for iOS or Android Tap Sign In Tap the SSO option When prompted to enter your company domain, enter oregonstate A browser window will open to the OSU login page — enter your OSU login information and click log in, and complete DUO authentication if required Resources for getting started Getting Started - Desktop (zoom.us): Getting Started on PC and Mac How to Use Zoom’s Desktop App (v 4.3) Zoom Web Client Getting Started - Mobile (zoom.us): System Requirements for iOS and Android Getting Started with iOS Getting Started with Android Getting Started with BlackBerry Joining a meeting Join a meeting Meetings can be joined through an email invite, from a browser through OSU's Zoom web portal https://oregonstate.zoom.us, from the Zoom desktop and mobile app, by phone, and with a video conferencing system (H.323 or SIP device). Each meeting will have a unique meeting ID that is required to join a Zoom meeting. If you are joining by phone, you will need the teleconferencing number provided in the invitation. Joining a Meeting (zoom.us) Join and configure your audio There are two ways to join meeting audio, joining via computer or joining via telephone. Follow these sections to test your audio before or after joining a meeting. You can also join a test meeting if you want to test your audio and familiarize yourself with meeting controls. Testing Computer or Device Audio (zoom.us) Test your video You can test your video camera with Zoom before joining the meeting to ensure that the camera is working correctly. You can also start a test Zoom meeting. Testing your Video (zoom.us) Schedule a meeting and invite participants Schedule a meeting Visit zoom.oregonstate.edu and click "Login to Zoom" Click "My Meetings" Click "Schedule a Meeting" Configure the meeting to your specifications Click "Schedule" Add the scheduled meeting to your calendar and/or copy the meeting invitation and share with students Invite participants to your meeting After copying the meeting invitation, you can share it with your participants Paste the meeting invitation into an email or preferred messaging platform Additional resources Scheduling Meetings (zoom.us) Scheduling a Meeting (OSU KnowlegeBase) Schedule Meeting on Other's Behalf (OSU KnowledgeBase) Invite others to your meeting (zoom.us) Start and run your meeting Start your Zoom meeting Login at zoom.oregonstate.edu Click "My Meetings" Under Upcoming Meetings, click "Start" next to the meeting you want to start start-meeting-zoom.png The Zoom client should launch automatically to start the meeting Run your Zoom meeting Within a Zoom Meeting, there are a number of controls along the bottom of the screen Click "Join Audio" to connect using your speakers/microphone or a telephone Click "Start Video" to start your video Click "Manage Participants" to see and manage your attendees Click "Screen Share" to share your screen/documents Click "Chat" to engage with your students if they are unable to hear you Click "Record" to record your meeting to the cloud Host and Co-Host Controls in a Meeting: You can find more details in Zoom's support article about host controls. How to screen share Zoom allows for screen sharing on desktop, tablet and mobile devices running Zoom. You can share a screen, websites, Powerpoint presentations, and much more. You can find more details in Zoom's support article on sharing your screen. Leverage breakout rooms Breakout rooms allow you to easily place meeting attendees into sub-meetings for group discussion, activities, projects and more. You can find more details in Zoom's support article: https://support.zoom.us/hc/en-us/articles/206476313-Managing-Breakout-Rooms After the meeting Locate your recorded meeting Record your Zoom meeting in the cloud Once you've conducted your meeting, click "End Meeting" The recording will automatically transfer to My Media in either MediaSpace (media.oregonstate.edu) or Canvas (canvas.oregonstate.edu) You will receive an automated email once the recording has been processed Visit My Media in either MediaSpace or Canvas Your most recent recording should display at the top of this page From there you can edit your recording, caption it, and make it shareable Additional resources Record a Meeting (OSU KnowlegeBase) Locate a Zoom Recording (OSU KnowlegeBase) Meeting Reports (OSU KnowlegeBase) Additional Resources FAQs KnowledgeBase articles Media tutorials and help videos Student Media Services' weekly Zoom basics sessions Zoom Web Conferencing FAQ (zoom.us) Zoom for Higher Education (PDF from zoom.us) Student Tips for Participating in Online Learning (PDF from zoom.us) How to Keep the Party Crashers from Crashing your Zoom Meeting (Zoom Blog) ‹ Passcode Requirement up Teach/Learn/Work › Contact Info Information Services Office of the Vice-Provost Kerr Admin Bldg.
