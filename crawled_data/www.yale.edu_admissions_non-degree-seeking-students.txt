From online courses to on-campus experiences, there are a range of learning opportunities available for non-degree seekers. Yale College Audit a class or take courses for credit without matriculating as a Yale undergraduate. The Non-Degree Students Program Yale Alumni Auditing Program Yale Summer Session Graduate & Professional Schools The following schools offer non-degree programs ranging from research opportunities to continuing education. Graduate School of Arts & Sciences David Geffen School of Drama School of Management School of Medicine School of Music Office of International Affairs The Office of International Affairs offers a suite of global leadership programs on important topics and trends for professionals, experts, and life-long learners from around the world. Leadership Programs Certificates Our certificate programs feature multiple courses organized in an integrated curriculum. Access the expertise of Yale faculty and deepen your networks by joining small cohorts of working professionals. Climate Change and Health Financing and Deploying Clean Energy Tropical Forest Landscapes Online Learning Free and for-credit Yale courses covering topics from game theory to modern poetry are available to undergraduates, graduate students, and the general public. Online Learning World Fellows Program This leadership development program brings 16 innovative thinkers and doers to campus for four months of learning, collaboration, and research; they go on to become part of a global network of World Fellows. World Fellows Program Yale for Life The Yale for Life program for alumni invites participants to New Haven for a one-week, immersive learning experience. All courses are led by Yale professors and focus on a particular subject. Yale for Life Young Global Scholars In this academic summer program, exceptional high school students from the United States and abroad who are interested in politics and international affairs live and learn on campus.
