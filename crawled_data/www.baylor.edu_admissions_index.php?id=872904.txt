Students may desire to take classes for personal enrichment or one of the following reasons: Exchange: An exchange student is one who is pursuing a degree at another school and will be participating in one of Baylor's exchange programs. These students apply for admission with the help of the exchange program director. Questions may be directed to International Student and Scholars Services at ISSS_Support@baylor.edu. Study Abroad: Students desiring to participate in one of Baylor's study abroad programs but are pursuing a degree at another college or university may apply as a non-degree seeking student. Students should submit an application for admission via goBAYLOR and write the name of the study abroad program they plan to participate in. Questions may be directed to Study Abroad at Study_Abroad_Interest@baylor.edu. Visiting Students: Students currently attending another university who want to take Baylor classes and have these credits transferred to their current school. Students who have not previously applied to Baylor should submit an application for admission via goBAYLOR as well as a current college transcript or letter of good standing to Undergraduate Admissions. Students who previously applied to Baylor should submit a new application for admission via goBAYLOR and a current transcript or letter of good standing from your current college or university to Undergraduate Admissions. On the application, select Non-Degree Seeking/Visiting as your student type. There is no need to select an intended program or major. After being accepted, submit a Health Form with documentation of bacterial meningitis vaccination and review the Registration Checklist. No enrollment deposit is required. Accelerate: Baylor University's Accelerate Pre-College Program gives high school students the opportunity to begin their academic pursuits and get ahead on core course requirements that will apply to a range of Baylor degrees, but are also transferable to other universities. This is a unique opportunity to get a jump start that will prepare students for college-level rigor. Learn more about Accelerate. Questions may be directed to accelerate@baylor.edu. Other non-degree seeking students: Students not currently attending a college or university, or are participating in a Study Abroad Program, or International Exchange Program. Submit an application for admission via goBAYLOR. Additional steps might be required based on individual circumstances. After being accepted, submit a Health Form with documentation of bacterial meningitis vaccination and review the Registration Checklist. No enrollment deposit is required. Degree Seeking Students International ACCP students: The ACCP (Advanced College Credit Pathway) is a pathway program that allows senior high school or first-year international college students to earn one year of general education college credits at their home institution and then transfer to Baylor or another consortium university as a sophomore student. This program gives international students a great start in preparing for college. Baylor University partners with IPERC, which oversees the development and administration of the program abroad. Learn more about the ACCP at iperc.org/accp. Post-baccalaureate students: Students who have already earned a bachelor's degree may seek a second degree if they are seeking a different degree with a different major from the first degree or major. Applicants who have not previously applied to Baylor should submit an application for admission via goBAYLOR. Students who previously applied to Baylor should submit a new application for admission via goBAYLOR. On the application, select Post-Baccalaureate as your student type. All applicants should submit a transcript to Undergraduate Admissions showing the degree already earned. After being accepted, submit a Health Form with documentation of bacterial meningitis vaccination and review the Registration Checklist. No enrollment deposit is required.
