Success Repayment Educational loans need to be repaid once a student graduates or is no longer enrolled at least half-time. Grace Period Information Loans offer borrowers a grace period before repayment begins: Federal Direct Subsidized/Unsubsidized Loans, Federal Graduate, and Professional PLUS Loans allow the borrower a six month grace period after the student graduates or ceases to be enrolled half-time, before repayment begins. Federal Perkins, Health Professional, and Disadvantage Student Loans allow the borrower a nine month grace period after the student graduates or ceases to be enrolled half-time before repayment begins. Federal Parent PLUS Loans begin repayment 60 days after the loan is fully disbursed. If a deferment was selected, the loan will begin repayment six months after the student graduates or ceases to be enrolled half-time. Private/Alternative Loans may have various repayment start dates. Borrowers should contact their individual lenders for repayment start dates. Repayment Information Borrowers can discuss their questions or concerns regarding their loan repayment with an advisor in the Office of Student Financial Success.
