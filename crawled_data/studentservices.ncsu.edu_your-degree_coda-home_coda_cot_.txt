Requirements and preferred qualifications to transfer (as a current NC State student) into specific Wilson College of Textiles majors are listed below. This includes students who desire to change their major, as well as those who want to add a Textiles major. Students must meet the listed “Minimum Requirements” in order to submit an online CODA application to transfer (see CODA Application). These requirements must either be complete, or the courses in progress, at the time the application is submitted. Candidates who meet the preferred qualifications outlined below have a higher probability of being approved for transfer. All admission is competitive and based on space availability in the specific program. Meeting the preferred qualifications does not guarantee admission to the desired major. For students interested in any Wilson College of Textiles major, it is advisable to take T101 to be more certain of that interest. If you have questions about transferring to programs in the Wilson College of Textiles, contact the Academic Programs office: Students with last names A-G: Meggie Metcalf, Academic Advisor: meggie_metcalf@ncsu.edu Students with last names H-Z: Alyssa Jennings, Academic Advisor: anjenni3@ncsu.edu Department of Textile Engineering, Chemistry, and Science NOTE: The requirements listed below must either be complete, or the courses in progress, at the time the application is submitted. End of current semester grades will be included in the application review. Polymer and Color Chemistry (BS): Minimum to apply and be considered: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.4 or better Completion of CH 101 & 102 AND MA 131 or MA 141 with grades of C- or better Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Cumulative GPA of 2.6 or better, or Completion of PCC 101/104 AND PCC 106 with a grades of B- or better Textile Technology (BS): Minimum to apply and be considered: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.4 or better Completion of CH 101 & 102 AND MA 131 or MA 141 with grades of C- or better Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Cumulative GPA of 2.6 or better, or Completion of TT 105 (Textile Technology) with a grade of B- or better Textile Engineering (BS): See information on the College of Engineering CODA page. Department of Textile and Apparel Technology and Management NOTE: The requirements listed below must either be complete, or the courses in progress, at the time the application is submitted. End of current semester grades will be included in the application review. Fashion and Textile Management (BS): Minimum to apply and be considered: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.6 or better Completion of MA 131 or 141 or CH 101 & 102 Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Cumulative GPA of 2.8 or better, or Completion of MA 131 or 141 AND CH 101 & 102 Fashion Textile and Design (BS): Minimum to apply and be considered: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.6 or better Completion of MA 131 or 141 or CH 101 & 102 Students interested in transferring to Fashion and Textile Design must submit an electronic application (CODA) and portfolio by November 1. Applicants are reviewed annually for admission for the fall semester. The Fashion and Textile Design program requires a four-year enrollment regardless of the number of college credits completed at the time of entry. For this reason, interested students should contact the academic program office to discuss program details, student credentials, and the application process.
