View our upcoming classes, office hours, and events Image above: Sagittal brain sketch and notes by Harvey Cushing circa 1935 Welcome to the Center The Center for the History of Medicine is open to all Harvard University ID (HUID) holders. We anticipate being closed to the public through Spring 2022. HUID holders should email the Center to make appointments in advance. Learn more about scheduling your visit. We continue to provide online reference services and remote research consultations to the public. Please email us your questions! The Center for the History of Medicine in the Francis A. Countway Library of Medicine is one of the world's leading resources for the study of the history of health and medicine. Our rare books, manuscript and archival collections, artifacts, and audiovisual materials attract a global audience of researchers. Our mission is to enable the history of medicine and public health to inform healthcare, the health sciences, and the societies in which they are embedded. Follow us on Instagram and Twitter @HarvardHistMed. Contact Us Have questions? Want to schedule a remote consultation? Contact us. Meet Our Staff Programs & Initiatives View More Archives for Diversity and Inclusion Ensuring that the Center's collections reflect the diversity of the Harvard Medical, Dental, and Public Health communities by acquiring the teaching and professional records of underrepresented faculty and Longwood Medical Area researchers. Brigham and Women’s Hospital Archives Preserving the history of the Brigham and Women’s Hospital, a teaching affiliate of Harvard Medical School, by collecting and making accessible historic records from BWH and its parent hospitals. Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health Archives Charting the evolution of one of the oldest and most influential schools of public health in the United States through its faculty collections and departmental records.
