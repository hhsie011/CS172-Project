Mark Nikont, D.Min Instructor Chaplain, United States Army Email Mark_Nikont@baylor.edu Phone 210.221.6171 High Res Photo CV Download CV Professional Summary: Rev. Dr. Mark Nikont is an Instructor at the MHA/MBA Army Baylor Graduate Program since 2020. He holds an MBE from the University of Pennsylvania, and a D.Min. from Erskine Theological Seminary. Rev. Dr. Nikont is also a Chaplain in the United States Army serving in ministry, leadership, and tactical roles for over 22 years. Mark’s research has been in the areas of self-care and moral injury. He holds certifications in Ethics Instruction and multiple counseling modalities. He is ordained and endorsed by the Christian Church (Disciples of Christ) and is a member of the Association of Professional Chaplains. Education: 2020 - Master of BioEthics, University of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia, PA 2016 - Doctor of Ministry, Erskine Theological Seminary, Due West, SC 2007 - Master of Divinity, Asbury Theological Seminary, Wilmore, KY 2003 - BA - Leadership, Bellevue University, Bellevue, NE Courses: HCA 5105 Introduction to Healthcare Ethics (2021-Present) HCA 5356 Organizational Ethics (2020-Present) Research Interests: Ethics Moral Injury Spiritual Growth Professional Affiliations: Disciples Chaplains Association Association of Professional Chaplains Army-Baylor Programs Admissions Admissions Contact About Army-Baylor Program Overview Program Philosophy Competency Based Education Student Outcomes Army-Baylor Program History Residency Program Residency Sites Residency Manual Preceptor Training Curriculum MHA Curriculum MHA/MBA Curriculum ECL MHA Curriculum ECL MHA/MBA Curriculum Faculty Directory Teresa S.
