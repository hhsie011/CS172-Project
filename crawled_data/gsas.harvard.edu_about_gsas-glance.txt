The GSAS was established in 1872 and is the only School at Harvard to award the PhD. The Harvard Library is the largest in the academic world, holding 18.9 million volumes, 174,000 serial titles, an estimated 400 million manuscript items, 10 million photographs, 56 million archived web pages, and 5.4 terabytes of born-digital archives and manuscripts. Each year, 100 GSAS students live in Harvard’s houses and freshman dorms as resident tutors and proctors.
