Tested Positive Exposed Search this site search Updated Guidelines for COVID-19 May 23, 2022, 4:00 p.m. CDT Texas A&M University continues to monitor the COVID-19 pandemic via data available from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, Texas Department of State Health Services and the Brazos County Health District. The COVID-19 Community Level in Brazos County continues to remain low. Following are the updates to the university's COVID-19 policies and guidelines that are being implemented immediately. Students, faculty and staff are no longer required to complete the COVID-19 Self-Reporting Form. However, everyone is required to continue to follow the Texas A&M COVID-19 Protocols if you are symptomatic, test positive or are exposed to someone who has tested positive. PCR testing will remain available on campus at multiple locations. Visit the COVID-19 Testing Sites page for testing information and to schedule an appointment. Free vaccines, including all CDC-recommended boosters, continue to be offered for students, faculty and staff through the A.P. Beutel Student Health Center. Schedule a COVID-19 vaccination through the Student Health Services Patient Portal. The Texas A&M COVID-19 Reporting Data will cease to be collected or published on our website. Visit covid.tamu.edu for additional guidance and information. In the event that the COVID-19 Community Level rises, the university will update our policies and guidelines to help mitigate the spread of COVID-19 within our community.
