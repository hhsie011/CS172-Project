Harvard Offices GSAS and University resources exist to help students navigate their academic careers, including: Academic Resource Center Accessible Education Office Derek Bok Center for Teaching and Learning GSAS Fellowships & Writing Center GSAS Office of Equity, Diversity, Inclusion & Belonging GSAS Office of Financial Aid GSAS Student Center GSAS Office of Student Services GSAS Title IX Resource Coordinators Office of Career Services Harvard-based Resources Many Harvard programs, faculty, and graduate students have developed advising documents and guides that can be adapted or be used to start discussions about effective advising and expectations. Kramer Lab Expectations—Professor Elena Kramer outlines her expectations for graduate students and lists what graduate students can expect from her on her lab's website. Goodrich Lab Best Practices—Professor Lisa Goodrich employs a strategy to ensure transparency and create a positive lab culture by recommending best practices designed to benefit all members of the research community. Department of Sociology Doctoral Advising Conversation Guide—PhD students and faculty in the Department of Sociology worked together to develop a Doctoral Advising Conversation Guide that aims to outline questions students might ask at different phases of the doctoral program. GSAS Crimson Folder—GSAS has prepared guidance and resources for administrators, faculty, or staff members to assist GSAS students in distress. External Resources Professional societies and academic institutions have developed guides focused on the advising relationship that can be useful in developing and improving an advising practice. The National Academies of Science, Education, and Medicine has a user-friendly online guide that provides evidence-based resources relevant to all disciplines, including advice for institutions, programs, advisors, and advisees. The University of Minnesota provides an online training module titled Optimizing the Practice of Mentoring. The University of Michigan Rackham Graduate School provides many excellent advising documents including: Quick Tips for Promising Practices: Mentoring How to Mentor Graduate Students: A Guide for Graduate Faculty How to Get the Mentoring You Want: A Guide for Graduate Students The Modern Language Association (MLA) issued a report on ethical conduct in graduate education.
