Brown is a leading research university and the seventh-oldest college in the U.S. At the heart of the University’s teaching, research and scholarship is a commitment to academic excellence, intellectual freedom and making an impact to better serve people, communities and society. The University is renowned for its distinctive undergraduate experience rooted in its flexible yet rigorous Open Curriculum. Its campus in vibrant Providence, Rhode Island, is home to a wide array of graduate programs, plus the Warren Alpert Medical School, School of Public Health, School of Engineering and School of Professional Studies. 2,477 courses 1,429 faculty members 7:1 student-to-faculty ratio 635 Fulbright Scholars to date 80+ undergraduate programs 84 doctoral and master’s programs Undergraduate Education Brown has earned a global reputation for its innovative undergraduate educational experience, rooted in the academic rigor and intellectual entrepreneurism of its Open Curriculum. The Open Curriculum Brown’s flexible yet rigorous approach to education pushes undergraduates to be deeply creative thinkers, intellectual risk-takers and entrepreneurial problem-solvers. Find a Program Brown offers more than 80 programs, what some colleges call majors. You'll sample courses in a wide range of subjects before immersing yourself in one of these focused areas. Graduate and Professional Education With innovative, student-centered academic training and a diverse and collaborative culture, Brown prepares graduate students for distinguished careers in research, teaching and leadership in their fields, both inside and outside of higher education. Doctoral and Master’s Programs Brown offers 51 doctoral programs and 33 master’s programs across the life and physical sciences, social sciences, arts and humanities.
