At the same, credible evaluations find scant evidence that technology on its own, whether in the form of SMART boards or individual computers, generates any improvement in student performance. Could we be making a mistake by focusing on technology rather than carefully considering what problems the technology is meant to solve? This half-day meeting brought together leaders from the New York City education community to participate in a discussion led by cutting-edge thinkers who distinguish themselves in the vast field of ed tech by having thought hard about the underlying science of education, and by having considered how it might fit with technological applications. They all are education experts first and technologists second, with experience integrating technologies into educational settings. Social Enterprise Leadership Forum: The Role of Technology in Teacher Development Video of Social Enterprise Leadership Forum: The Role of Technology in Teacher Development Ellen Moir CEO of The New Teacher Center The founder and CEO of The New Teacher Center, Ellen Moir, spoke at the 2014 Social Enterprise Leadership Forum about how increasing teacher effectiveness is the key to raising overall student performance in her presentation, “The Role of Technology in Teacher Development.” The forum, hosted by the Social Enterprise Program at Columbia Business School, was entitled “Fulfilling the promise of education technology.” With an introduction by Ray Fisman, Lambert Family Professor of Social Enterprise and Co-director, Social Enterprise Program. Social Enterprise Leadership Forum: Computing and the Science of the Individual Video of Social Enterprise Leadership Forum: Computing and the Science of the Individual Joel Rose CEO of New Classrooms Innovations Partners Joel Rose, co-founder and CEO of New Classrooms Innovations Partners, presented his vision for redesigning the classroom in “Computing and the Science of the Individual” at the 2014 Social Enterprise Leadership Forum. The forum, hosted by the Social Enterprise Program at Columbia Business School, was entitled “Fulfilling the promise of education technology.” Getting Teachers, Parents, and Technology Working Together for Student Learning Video of Getting Teachers, Parents, and Technology Working Together for Student Learning Elisabeth Stock Co-founder and CEO of CFY Elisabeth Stock, Co-founder and CEO of CFY, presented “Getting Teachers, Parents, and Technology Working Together for Student Learning” at the 2014 Social Enterprise Leadership Forum. Social Enterprise Leadership Forum: What We’ve Learned About Education Technology Video of Social Enterprise Leadership Forum: What We’ve Learned About Education Technology Emary Aronson Robin Hood Foundation At the conclusion of the 2014 Social Enterprise Leadership Forum, Emary Aronson of the Robin Hood Foundation discussed the benefits of teaching computer science at the high school level in her presentation, “What We’ve Learned (and Need to Learn) About Education Technology.” The forum, hosted by the Social Enterprise Program at Columbia Business School, was entitled “Fulfilling the promise of education technology.” With an introduction by Ray Fisman, Lambert Family Professor of Social Enterprise and Co-director, Social Enterprise Program.
