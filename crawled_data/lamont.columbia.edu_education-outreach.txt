We bring advances in the understanding of our planet and impacts of Earth processes on society gained through our groundbreaking research to students, educators, and the public through interactive programs and hands-on learning opportunities. Visitor Opportunities Core Repository The Lamont-Doherty Core Repository maintains one of the world’s most unique and important collections of scientific samples from the deep sea. Hudson River Field Station The Hudson River Field Station helps visitors explore and learn directly from the best classroom: the Hudson River itself! Open House Every October, we welcome kids, families, and science enthusiasts to meet our scientists and participate in dozens of activities and programs. Student & Educator Programs School Programs Rockland P.L.U.S. engages high school students in sustainable planning while Day in the Life of the Hudson gives K-12 students hands-on field experiences. Student Summer Opportunities Secondary School Field Research, Summer Intern, and Next Generation of Hudson River Educators programs provide students with rich, meaningful summer experiences. Professional Development Earth2Class connects K-14 teachers with scientists and the latest research to bring new tools and ideas to their classrooms Resources Polar Science Our polar science activities focus on the evidence of climate change in the polar regions and introduce students to a variety of science concepts and resources. Climate & Sustainability Our interactive programs for K-12 students, families, and educators feature experts from across Lamont and Columbia Climate School presenting on important and interesting sustainability topics. Hudson River Our collection of lesson plans, videos, data sets, and other resources helps students explore the Hudson River estuary’s uniqueness, complexity, and life.
