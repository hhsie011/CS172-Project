For members of the Baylor Family who are continuing a hybrid or remote work arrangement, these tools and resources from Human Resources, Information Technology Services, and others may help you plan your work and remain productive in a new environment: Alternate Work Location Policy This policy provides designated Baylor University employees with the opportunity to perform their work at a location other than on Campus. ITS Resources for Working Remotely Find key online resources to collaborate with colleagues and continue to support the university from a remote, Internet-connected location. Manager Tools for Remote Work Managing your team and your team’s work is a little different when everyone is remote. Managing Alternative Work Arrangements Suggestions and tips regarding how managers can enable employees to continue working Practical Tips for Managing Remote Workers Learn practical ideas and tips about how to manage remote workers. Practical Tips for Video Meetings Learn about nine practical ways to improve communication via video chat. Practical Tips for Working at Home Find helpful hints and suggestions about how to create a successful workplace in your home. Professional & Leadership Development when Working Remote Although in person professional development classes aren’t currently an option, there are several resources available for you to expand your knowledge and skills while working from home.
