Sciences Requirements and preferred qualifications to transfer (as a current NC State student) into specific College of Humanities and Social Sciences majors are listed below. This includes students who wish to change their major, as well as those who want to add a Humanities and Social Sciences major. Students must meet the listed “Minimum Requirements” in order to submit an online CODA application to transfer (see CODA Application). These requirements must be complete at the time the application is submitted. Candidates who meet the preferred qualifications outlined below have a higher probability of being approved for transfer. All admission is competitive and based on space availability in the specific program. Meeting the preferred qualifications does not guarantee admission to the desired major. If you have questions about transferring to programs in the College of Humanities and Social Sciences, contact the college’s Academic Support Center in Caldwell Lounge or via email at: chassacademicsupport@ncsu.edu. The Dean’s Office can be contacted to address questions via telephone at (919) 515-2468. Communication Minimum to apply and be considered: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.0 or better Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Cumulative NC State GPA of 3.0 or better, or Completion of ENG 101 with grade of B- or better, and Completion of COM 200, and Completion of 3 additional hours in COM at the 200-level or higher, and Cumulative GPA in all COM courses of 2.5 or better Note: COM 200 is designed specifically as an entry-point class for internal transfers, providing them with 3 hours at the 200-level. For internal transfers, COM 200 currently replaces COM 250 as a degree requirement. English Minimum to apply and be considered: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.0 or better Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Cumulative NC State GPA of 2.5 or better, AND completion of one ENG course beyond ENG 101 with grade of B- or better, or GPA of 2.0 or better, AND two ENG courses beyond ENG 101 with grades of B- or better Students typically complete their expected English course(s) from the corresponding list below to enter one of the five specific concentrations in English: * Creative Writing: ENG 281, 287, 288, 289 * Language, Writing, and Rhetoric: ENG 210, 214, 216 * Film: ENG 282 * Literature: ENG 207, 208, 209, 232, 233, 246, 248, 251, 252, 261, 262, 265, 266, 267, 305 * Teacher Education: ENG 207, 208, 209, 210, 232, 233, 246, 248, 251, 252, 261, 262, 265, 266, 267, 305 (Note: Graduation requirements specify that students are not allowed to take more than five (5) 200-level English classes.) If you have additional questions about transferring into the English major, email Dr. Jason Swarts (jason_swarts@ncsu.edu). Foreign Languages and Literatures Minimum to apply and be considered: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.0 or better Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Completion of ENG 101 with a grade of B or better, and Completion of FL* 201 and FL* 202 with a grade B- or better History Minimum to apply and be considered: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.0 or better Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Cumulative NC State GPA of 3.0 or better, or GPA of 2.5 or better, AND one HI course with grade of B- or better, or GPA 2.0 or better, AND two HI courses with grades of B- or better Interdisciplinary Studies Minimum to apply and be considered for all programs in this academic unit: Completion of 12 or more NC State graded credit hours Cumulative GPA must be 2.0 or better Arts Studies Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Completion of one course in their intended foundational art area with a grade of B- or better, from the following list: Visual – ARC 140, 261, 441, 442; ADN 414; GD 203, 303; HA 201, 202, 203, 298, 320, 395, 401, 404; LAR 423, 444; Music – MUS 103, 201, 202, 203, 230, 260, 306, 310, 315, 320, 330, 335, 340, 345, 350, 351, 360, 495; Theatre – ENG 206; THE 103, 203, 223; Film – ENG 282 Interdisciplinary Studies (degree program) Africana Studies or Africana Studies Community Studies Concentrations Preference will be given to students who meet the following: Completion of two of the following AFS courses with grades of B- or better in each: AFS 240, 241, 342, 343, 344, 345, 346, 442, 490, 491, 497. Self-Design Concentration Students must complete a formal application process. Check the program’s website for details here.
