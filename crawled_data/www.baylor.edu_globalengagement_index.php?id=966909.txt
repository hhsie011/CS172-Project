The International Student Academic Support (ISAS) office provides academic and English language support to any international student that might be struggling in their Face-to-Face and/or online classes. This is a partnership of the Center for Global Engagement, the Global Gateway Program, and Academic Support Programs. Specifically, the ISAS is designed to help international students: increase English understanding of assignments, upcoming exams, or lectures. make a plan to improve English language skills needed for academic success provide academic tools and resources to help make online learning more successful connect with other services available at Baylor (both online and on campus â€“ for example, content tutoring, mentoring, counseling, etc.). Schedule an online or Face-to-Face appointment to meet with the ISAS Specialist. International Student Academic Support 1. Schedule an Appointment You can schedule an appointment on Navigate or use the Navigate App to make an appointment with the International Student Academic Support Specialist (Debra Wainscott). For more instructions on making an ISAS appointment on Navigate, click here. 2. Available ISAS Meeting Times 3. Face-to-Face Meeting Location The ISAS office is located in Sid Rich 029 (West Wing Basement). When entering from the parking lot, go left through the Career Center doors (by the stairs). Then go down the stairs toward the Center for Academic Success and Engagement (CASE) offices. 4. Online Meeting Information Appointments for online meetings use Zoom and are based on Central Standard Time (CST) in Waco, Texas. https://baylor.zoom.us/ **You will receive Zoom meeting information once your appointment has been confirmed. 5. International Student Resources Check out the resources below for additional help and information! Academic Resources for International Students 6. Information for Faculty and Staff Faculty and staff are encouraged to refer international students that might need English language support through one of the following: Issue an Alert or Progress Report through Navigate (formerly SSC): Click here for the SSC/Navigate Log-In For Alerts, choose the category 'International Student Needs Academic Support' and add a short note explaining the situation. For Progress Reports, choose a reason that is applicable and add a short comment that might help explain the situation in more detail. Email: international_academic_support@baylor.edu 7. Questions? Email:international_academic_support@baylor.edu Phone: (254) 710-2670 International Graduate Student Language Support The Graduate School works with the Global Gateway Program to evaluate and offer instruction* to International Teaching Assistants and Teachers of Record at Baylor University. International students are evaluated based on their TOEFL scores, and those lacking sufficient language skills will be required to participate in the GBL Teaching in English Class for International Teaching Assistants before entering the classroom. Our objective is to establish guidelines, testing standards, and course curriculum to adequately prepare international graduate students for the classroom. *Course offerings can fluctuate from term-to-term.
