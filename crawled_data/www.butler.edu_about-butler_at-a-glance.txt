Inclusion Community & Engagement Office of the President Strategic Direction Give to Butler Mascot Blue IV Careers At a Glance Butler University is a private, nationally recognized comprehensive university encompassing six colleges: Arts, Business, Communication, Education, Liberal Arts & Sciences, and Pharmacy & Health Sciences. Approximately 4,500 undergraduate and 1,000 graduate and doctoral students are enrolled at Butler, representing 46 states and 22 countries. School Type: Private, Coed Year Founded: 1855 Religious Affiliation: None Mission Our mission is to provide the highest quality of liberal and professional education and to integrate the two by creating and fostering a stimulating intellectual community built on interactive dialogue and inquiry among students, faculty, and staff. Institutional Research & Fact Book Tuition & Fees Location Butler is located in Indianapolis, Indiana. When students come to Butler, they’re getting more than just a world-class education. They’re joining the bustling community of Indianapolis, nicknamed the Crossroads of America and home to five professional sports teams, the Indianapolis Motor Speedway, a vibrant arts and culture scene, a nationally recognized international airport, and many major businesses and corporations. History 1850—The charter of North Western Christian University proposed by abolitionist Ovid Butler was approved by the Indiana General Assembly. 1855—Classes began at North Western Christian University in the area of today’s 13th Street and College Avenue. 1877—The school moved to Irvington and was renamed Butler University. 1928—Butler University relocated to its current location at 4600 Sunset Avenue.
