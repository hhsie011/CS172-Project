Roger Buck W. Roger Buck Education 1974-1978 Undergraduate at the College of William and Mary. Earned B.S. with majors in Physics and Geology. 1978-1984 Graduate student in the Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and earned a Ph.D. in Geophysics. Thesis title: "Small-scale Convection and the Evolution of the Lithosphere." Honors & Awards 1978 Elected to Phi Beta Kappa 1978 'Outstanding Geology Student' at the College of William and Mary 1982-1984 Exxon Teaching Fellowship at M.I.T. 1984 Post-doctoral Fellowship at Lamont-Doherty Geological Observatory of Columbia University 1986 Atlantic Richfield Young Scholars Award at Lamont-Doherty 1989 Stork Fellowship Award at Lamont-Doherty 1999 Invited to give the 1999 “William Smith Lecture” of the Geological Society of London 2003 Gave the “Francis Birch Lecture” at the American Geophysical Union 2009 Elected Fellow of the American Geophysical Union Notable Accomplishments: 1986 First to suggest small-scale convection under rifts 1988 First mechanical analysis of rotation of normal faults to low-dips 1991 Suggested a physical rationale for 3 modes of continental rifting 1998 First numerical simulation of abyssal hill faulting 1999 First full mechanical treatment of ‘large-offset’ normal faults 2001 Suggested the now-accepted model for ‘axial high’ formation 2004 Showed how magma reduces the force needed to rift continents 2005 First treatment of the relation between magmatism and faulting at spreading centers 2006 Suggested the now-accepted model for stress evolution in sequences of dikes 2010 Most complete treatment thermal effect of dike opening during continental rifting 2015 First model of UHP exhumation driven by subduction initiation 2017 First analytic description of ‘seaward dipping reflector’ volcanic sequences 2021 First model of crevasse formation account for finite thickness of floating ice Expand all Collapse all Publications Buck, W.R. and M.N.
