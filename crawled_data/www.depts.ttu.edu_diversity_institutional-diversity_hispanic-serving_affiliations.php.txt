Participants learn about and share evidence-based practices, strategies and resources that increase Latino degree completion at the community, associate, baccalaureate, and graduate levels. Hispanic Association of Colleges and Universities Institutional Member Annual Conference (Fall) focuses on topics for Hispanic Serving Institutions related to education, policy, funding and organizational success. For the past two years at least one TTU administrator attended and TTU President is a voting member within the organization and attends or sends a delegate to cast votes during the business meeting. Annual National Capitol Forum on Hispanic Higher Education (Spring) focuses on advocacy for the higher education success to promote and shape an agenda for Congress and the country at this forum. Five administrators attended in spring 2020 and met with congressional representatives, policy makers, corporate, community and philanthropic representatives. NASPA First-Gen Forward Designation As of 2019, Texas Tech University is recognized as an inaugural First-Gen Forward Institution. A national recognition program for higher education institutions committed to First-Generation Student Success. Texas Tech University proudly serves nearly 25% First-Gen in College (FGC) students. As part of this designation, TTU is committed to sharing best practices, knowledge, and emerging research with partners across campus. Seal of Excelencia Technical Assistance 2019 One of nine institutions accepted to receive the assistance to send five administrators to the Seal of Excelencia Alignment Institute. The goal of the institute was to create goals for strategies to achieving Latino student success through intentionally serving Latino students and to learn strategies to better enhance our success story to apply to the Seal of Excelencia in the future. Texas Education Consortium for Male Students of Color Member Institution/Men of Color Initiative at TTU (2019-current) In partnership with College of Education Focuses on improving educational outcomes for Latino and African American male students by connecting partners across educational sectors. The consortium member institutions are part of a statewide learning community that is committed to implementing and sustaining effective policies, programs, and practices focused on increasing individual success and post-secondary completion for male students of color. Consortium Webinar Series provides an in-depth discussion about the social and educational experiences of male students of color. Sessions are designed for faculty, administrators and staff focusing on both Texas and National topics. Consortium Research Institute focuses on understanding the experiences of Latino males across the education pipeline to engage faculty & research affiliate dedicated to this focus, conduct research to produce high-quality publications & reports, to present research and papers at regional and national conferences. Texas Male Student Leadership Summit provides an opportunity for male students of color and professionals from school districts, community college and university from across the state of Texas to engage in leadership skill building. Black, Brown and College Bound Summit Focuses creating space for discussion and knowledge exchange surrounding the challenges of African American and Latino male students in higher education. The Division of Diversity, Equity, & Inclusion sent two staff members to the conference in February 2020 in order to bring back relevant information on best practices for serving male students of color. As a Hispanic Serving Institution, Texas Tech University fully understands the importance of and is committed to the support and promotion of diversity, equity, and inclusion in higher education. Such efforts, however, are not limited to any one culture, race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, ability or disability, economic status, age, or citizenship. With tremendous progress being made on issues of equity and inclusion at the national and state levels, it is vitally important for institutions of higher education to illuminate both the inequities and the opportunities that exist in our society. As a major university in one of the fastest growing states of the Union, Texas Tech University is uniquely positioned to positively influence the attitudes and opinions of Texans of the future, the majority of whom will be underrepresented students of color. To that end, Texas Tech will continue to expand efforts such as the ones listed above to carry out its promise to prepare leaders for challenges of the future.
