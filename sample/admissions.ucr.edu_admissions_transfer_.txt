                       <h2 class="separator-line-before" id="cost">Transfer Costs, Fees &amp; Financial Aid</h2> 
                       <p>You can afford a UC education! The <a href="https://financialaid.ucr.edu/cost" target="_blank" title="cost of attending">cost of attending</a> UCR is extremely competitive compared with other University of California campuses and private schools. Living in Riverside is also considerably less expensive than living in nearby Los Angeles, Orange County, or San Diego. Read on to learn more about the programs available to help you pay for your UCR degree and how much that degree costs.</p> 
                       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                       <h3>University of California Tuition Stability Plan</h3> 
                       <p>Beginning fall 2022, tuition will be adjusted for each incoming undergraduate class but will subsequently remain flat until the student graduates, for up to six years. <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/tuition-financial-aid/tuition-cost-of-attendance/tuition-stability-plan.html" target="_blank" title="Learn more">Learn more</a>.</p> 
                       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                       <h3>Financial Aid</h3> 
                       <p>A UC degree is more affordable than you might think! Grants, scholarships, loans and, work-study is offered to qualified students by UCR’s <a href="https://financialaid.ucr.edu" target="_blank" title="UCR Financial Aid">Financial Aid</a> office.</p> 
                       <div class="grid-x grid-margin-x grid-padding-y"> 
                        <div class="cell callout large-auto"> 
                         <h2>86%</h2> 
                         <p style="padding: 0 5%;">of UCR undergraduate students who applied received some form of financial aid.&nbsp;</p> 
                        </div> 
                        <div class="cell callout large-auto"> 
                         <h2>97%</h2> 
                         <p style="padding: 0 5%;">of financial aid recipients received gift-aid (grants and/or scholarships).</p> 
                        </div> 
                        <div class="cell callout large-auto"> 
                         <h2>80%</h2> 
                         <p style="padding: 0 5%;">of students' financial aid need was met with need-based financial aid.</p> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <p class="line text-align-center"><small>(Financial Aid Office, 2020–21)</small><br> &nbsp;</p> 
                       <div class="infoblock"> 
                        <h5>UCR Financial Aid Applications School Codes</h5> 
                        <p><strong>FAFSA</strong>: 001316<br><strong>California Dream Act Application</strong>: 001316</p> 
                        <p>To qualify for financial aid, you need to complete the <a href="https://studentaid.ed.gov/sa/fafsa" target="_blank">Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)</a>&nbsp;or <a href="https://www.csac.ca.gov/california-dream-act" target="_blank">California Dream Act Application</a> each year by the March 2 priority deadline. (Applications can still be submitted after March 2.)</p> 
                       </div> 
                       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                       <h3>Additional Aid</h3> 
                       <div class="grid-x grid-margin-x grid-padding-y"> 
                        <div class="cell large-auto"> 
                         <h6>UC Riverside Foundation Scholarships</h6> 
                         <p>The UC Riverside Foundation offers scholarships, fellowships, internships, prizes, awards and loans to qualifying UCR students. Use the online database to search for aid based on your college, major, financial need, career goals, and more. <a href="http://scholarships.ucr.edu" target="_blank">scholarships.ucr.edu</a></p> 
                        </div> 
                        <div class="cell large-auto"> 
                         <h6>Work-Study</h6> 
                         <p>Eligible students can earn money for school through an approved on- or off-campus work-study job. Funds can only be used during the academic year, and you must meet financial need, academic, and other requirements to maintain your eligibility. <a href="https://careers.ucr.edu/students/work-study" target="_blank">careers.ucr.edu</a></p> 
                        </div> 
                       </div> 
                       <hr>
                       <div class="grid-x grid-margin-x grid-padding-y"> 
                        <div class="cell"> 
                         <h3>Aid for California Residents</h3> 
                         <p>The state of California offers a number of financial aid programs for residents, including those listed below. The programs offer qualifying students grants and scholarships that do not need to be repaid to cover tuition, fees and related expenses (like books, housing and transportation). To be eligible, complete your FAFSA or California Dream Act Application by the March 2 deadline each year, and make sure your <a href="https://www.csac.ca.gov/post/cal-grant-gpa-verification-form" target="_blank">Cal Grant GPA Verification Form</a> was submitted to the California Student Aid Commission by your community college.</p> 
                        </div> 
                        <div class="cell large-auto"> 
                         <h6>Middle Class Scholarship Program</h6> 
                         <p>If your family has up to $171,000 in annual income and assets, and you meet other residency, income and academic requirements, you could have up to 40% of your UC systemwide tuition and fees covered by this scholarship. <a href="http://go.ucr.edu/mcs" target="_blank">go.ucr.edu/mcs</a></p> 
                         <h6>Cal Grant</h6> 
                         <p>If you meet GPA, family income, residency, academic, and other requirements, you can get grant money that will cover some of your educational costs and fees. <a href="https://www.csac.ca.gov/cal-grants" target="_blank">csac.ca.gov/cal-grants</a></p> 
                        </div> 
                        <div class="cell large-auto"> 
                         <h6>Blue and Gold Opportunity Plan</h6> 
                         <p>If your total annual family income is less than $80,000, and you meet other residency, income and academic requirements, you could have 100% of your UC systemwide tuition and fees covered by grants and scholarships through this plan. Students with greater financial need can qualify for additional aid to cover expenses like books, housing, and transportation. <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/tuition-financial-aid/types-of-aid/blue-and-gold-opportunity-plan.html" target="_blank">admissions.universityofcalifornia.edu</a></p> 
                         <h6>Additional Aid from the State of California</h6> 
                         <p>Check out more state of California financial aid programs on the California Student Aid Commission.&nbsp;<a href="https://www.csac.ca.gov/financial-aid-programs" target="_blank">csac.ca.gov/financial-aid-programs</a></p> 
                        </div>

                       <h2 class="separator-line-before" id="apply">Applying to UCR</h2> 
                       <p>As a <a href="https://www.ucr.edu/about/ranks-and-facts" target="_blank" title="UCR Ranks and Facts">top-ranking university</a>, UCR sets high admission standards. Incoming students are selected based on a wide range of <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/how-to-apply/applying-as-a-transfer/how-applications-are-reviewed.html" target="_blank" title="Transfer criteria">criteria</a>, including&nbsp;academic performance and the potential for success as an undergraduate.&nbsp;Prepare for admission to UCR with the <a href="https://studentdocs.ucr.edu/admissions/uc-riverside_transfer-admission-checklist.pdf" target="_blank" title="Transfer Admissions Checklist">Transfer Admission Checklist</a>.</p> 
                       <div class="grid-x grid-margin-x grid-padding-y"> 
                        <div class="cell large-auto callout small"> 
                         <h2>2.4</h2> 
                         <p class="text-align-center">Minimum Transfer GPA Requirement</p>

                        <div class="cell large-auto callout small">

                       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                       <h3>Guarantee Your Place at UCR!</h3> 
                       <p>Through the <a href="/transfer/transfer-admission-guarantee" title="Transfer Admission Guarantee">Transfer Admission Guarantee (TAG)</a> program your California Community College coursework and grades can ensure your admission to UCR. Here's how:</p> 
                       <ol>
                        <li><strong>Create Your Transfer Admission Planner (TAP).</strong> TAP is an online tool where you can track and plan the community college coursework you need to complete to be admitted to UCR. It's also your TAG application! <a href="http://go.ucr.edu/transfer" target="_blank">Build your TAP today</a>!</li> 
                        <br>
                        <li><strong>Satisfy Your TAG Requirements.</strong> To qualify for TAG, you need to complete a minimum of 30 UC-transferrable units at your community college. Those units include first and second semesters of English composition, and the minimum math required by your major. Each major also has GPA requirements, which are outlined in the Getting into Your Major section below.</li> 
                        <br>
                        <li><strong>Submit Your TAG Application.</strong> UCR offers fall and winter quarter admission to transfer students. Before you apply for admission, log in to your TAP to complete the UCR TAG application within the filing period for your chosen quarter: May 1–31 for winter admission; Oct. 15&nbsp;for fall admission.</li> 
                        <br>
                        <li><strong>Complete Your TAG Contract.</strong> Get ready to transfer to UCR by taking the classes necessary to complete a total of 60 UC-transferrable units at your community college. This will include any prep courses specified by your major. Don’t forget about your GPA requirements!</li> 
                        <br>
                        <li><strong>Apply to UCR.</strong> All that's left to do is submit your UCR application within the filing period for your chosen quarter: July 1–31 for winter admission; Nov. 1–30 for fall admission. Visit <a href="http://go.ucr.edu/apply" target="_blank">go.ucr.edu/apply</a>&nbsp;when it's time to apply.</li> 
                       </ol>

                      <h3>Getting into Your Major</h3> 
                     </div> 
                    </div> 
                    <div class="cell"> 
                     <ul class="accordion ucr-accordion" data-accordion data-allow-all-closed="true" data-deep-link="true" data-update-history="true" id="accordion-0d26ed0a7e1b6a7a9f2b679ece5fd6e7"> 
                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#for_all_majors" class="accordion-title"> For All Majors </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="for_all_majors"> 
                        <p>In general, each major has prerequisite (major prep) coursework that you must take before transferring in order to get into your major.</p> 
                        <p>Take the recommended lower-division/general education/breadth classes for your major. Some programs recommend the&nbsp;<a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/transfer-requirements/preparing-to-transfer/general-education-igetc/igetc/" target="_blank">Intersegmental General Education Transfer Curriculum</a>&nbsp;(IGETC),&nbsp;&nbsp;which is a series of courses that California Community College students can complete to satisfy first- and second-year general education requirements before transferring. Some programs want you to focus on prerequisites instead of IGETC. Use&nbsp;<a href="http://assist.org/" target="_blank">ASSIST.org</a>&nbsp;to see what your major program recommends or contact a&nbsp;<a href="/transfer#counselor" title="Transfer Admissions Counselor">UCR admissions counselor</a>&nbsp;for help.&nbsp;View the <a href="https://studentdocs.ucr.edu/admissions/ASSIST_Flyer_April-2021.pdf" target="_blank">Assist Brochure</a>&nbsp;for more information.</p> 
                        <p>Each major has a minimum GPA requirement. Some majors require a GPA higher than that required for basic admission. Programs are selective. Strive to surpass your program’s minimum GPA. Remember: All transferable coursework will be taken into account.</p> 
                       </div> </li> 
                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#marlan_and_rosemary_bourns_college_of_engineering_requirements" class="accordion-title"> Marlan and Rosemary Bourns College of Engineering Requirements </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="marlan_and_rosemary_bourns_college_of_engineering_requirements"> 
                        <p><strong>Breadth Requirements</strong><br> You are strongly encouraged to focus on preparatory course work (mathematics, science and technical work) for your desired major rather than on <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/transfer-requirements/preparing-to-transfer/general-education-igetc/igetc/" target="_blank">IGETC</a> completion.                        <h3>How to Apply</h3> 
                       <p><strong>Gather Your Information</strong><br> Before you begin, find out what <a href="https://apply.universityofcalifornia.edu" target="_blank">important information</a> is needed for an accurate admission application.</p> 
                       <p><strong>Personal Insight Questions&nbsp;</strong><br> Express who you are, what matters to you and what you want to share with us by answering one required and an additional three out of seven&nbsp;<a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/how-to-apply/applying-as-a-transfer/personal-insight-questions.html" target="_blank">personal insight questions</a>.</p> 
                       <p><strong>Complete the UC Application</strong><br> The UC&nbsp;<a href="https://apply.universityofcalifornia.edu" target="_blank">application</a> opens on <strong>July 1</strong> for winter admission and <strong>Aug. 1</strong> for fall admission. The fee is $70 for each UC. You can apply for a fee waiver — provided you have your family’s income and the number of people supported by it — when you fill out the application. The winter application filing period is <strong>July 1–31</strong>. The fall application filing period is <strong>Nov. 1-30</strong>. If you have difficulties, contact&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="mailto:ucinfo@applyucsupport.net" target="_blank">info@applyucsupport.net</a>&nbsp;or (800) 207-1710.</p> 
                       <p><strong>After You Apply</strong><br> Once you've submitted your application, there are just a&nbsp; <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/how-to-apply/applying-as-a-transfer/after-you-apply/" target="_blank">few more steps</a> to complete the process. These include printing your receipt, ordering test score reports, and updating your grades and course records.</p> 
                       <p>UCR Undergraduate Admissions will email instructions on how to access your <a href="https://my.ucr.edu" target="_blank">MyUCR</a> admission portal account. Log in regularly to check your admission status, respond to messages, and track important dates and deadlines.</p> 
                       <p>Winter admission notifications begin in September. Fall admission notifications begin in March.&nbsp;Access the next steps to UCR for admitted <a href="https://admissions.ucr.edu/admitted#fall-transfers">fall transfer students</a> and <a href="https://admissions.ucr.edu/admitted#winter-transfers">winter transfer students</a>.</p>
