                       <h2 class="separator-line-before" id="cost">International Financial Aid, Costs &amp; Fees</h2> 
                       <p>The <a href="https://financialaid.ucr.edu/cost" target="_blank" title="cost of attending">cost of attending</a> UCR is extremely competitive with private institutions and other University of California campuses. Expenses associated with living in Riverside are considerably less than those of nearby metropolitan areas, such as Los Angeles, Orange County, and San Diego. Read on to learn more about the programs available to help you pay for your UCR degree and how much that degree costs.</p> 
                       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                       <h3>University of California Tuition Stability Plan</h3> 
                       <p>Beginning fall 2022, tuition will be adjusted for each incoming undergraduate class but will subsequently remain flat until the student graduates, for up to six years. <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/tuition-financial-aid/tuition-cost-of-attendance/tuition-stability-plan.html" target="_blank" title="Learn more">Learn more</a>.</p> 
                       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
                       <h3>Financial Aid</h3> 
                       <p>Grants, scholarships, and other awards are available to qualified students to help cover some of the cost of attending UCR, including the following:</p> 
                       <div class="grid-x grid-margin-x grid-padding-y"> 
                        <div class="cell large-auto"> 
                         <h6>Achievement Scholarship</h6> 
                         <p>Admitted international first year and transfer students are automatically considered for the Achievement Scholarship with their acceptance to UCR. No additional forms, paperwork, or applications are required to receive the scholarship. Top-achieving students can receive up to $4,500 each quarter. <a href="http://go.ucr.edu/nrscholarship" target="_blank" title="Non-Resident Scholarship">go.ucr.edu/nrscholarship</a></p> 
                        </div> 
                        <div class="cell large-auto"> 
                         <h6>UC Riverside Foundation Scholarships</h6> 
                         <p>The UC Riverside Foundation offers scholarships, fellowships, internships, prizes, awards, and loans to qualifying UCR students. Use the online database to search for aid based on your college, major, financial need, career goals, and more. <a href="https://scholarships.ucr.edu/" target="_blank" title="UC Riverside Foundation Scholarships, Fellowships &amp; Awards">scholarships.ucr.edu</a></p>

                        <p style="text-align:start"><span style="color:null;"><span style="font-size:medium"><span style="font-family:Calibri, sans-serif"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="font-weight:normal"><span style="white-space:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none"><strong>High School GPA</strong>—The strength of an applicant’s high school GPA is the strongest indicator that a student will be successful within an academically challenging UC environment. The capped GPA is calculated on the basis of all completed “a-g” courses with extra points added for UC-approved honors courses taken in grades 10 and 11, capped at 8 semesters. A capped GPA considers the extra points earned for honors-level courses and may exceed 4.0 for some students.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p> 
                        <p style="text-align:start"><span style="color:null;"><span style="font-size:medium"><span style="font-family:Calibri, sans-serif"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="font-weight:normal"><span style="white-space:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none"><strong>Advanced Placement (AP) or International Baccalaureate (IB)</strong> courses—AP and IB courses prepare students for college-level coursework. Therefore, students who take and do well in these courses tend to have a high probability for success within the UC environment.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p> 
                        <p style="text-align:start"><span style="font-size:medium"><span style="font-family:Calibri, sans-serif"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="font-weight:normal"><span style="white-space:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none"><span style="color:null;"><strong>Eligibility in the Local Context (ELC)</strong>—</span><a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/freshman-requirements/california-residents/local-guarantee-elc.html" style="color:#954f72; text-decoration:underline" target="_blank"><span style="color:null;">The Eligibility in the Local Context (ELC</span></a><span style="color:null;">) program recognizes the individual accomplishments of students in light of the opportunities offered by their particular high school.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p> 
                        <p style="text-align:start"><span style="color:null;"><span style="font-size:medium"><span style="font-family:Calibri, sans-serif"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="font-weight:normal"><span style="white-space:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none"><strong>Low Family Income</strong>—Students who demonstrate high academic achievement, despite low socioeconomic status, are likely to exhibit persistence, maturity, and insight. Low family income is determined based on total family members and household income.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p> 
                        <p style="text-align:start"><span style="color:null;"><span style="font-size:medium"><span style="font-family:Calibri, sans-serif"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="font-weight:normal"><span style="white-space:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none"><strong>First-Generation University Attendance</strong>— UC-eligible applicants who are the first in their immediate family to attend college are apt to have personal strengths that will contribute to their academic success. Students whose parents have not graduated from a four-year college or university are qualified for first-generation university attendance.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></p> 
                       </div> </li> 
                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#english_language_proficiency_international_firstyear_students_only" class="accordion-title"> English Language Proficiency (International First-Year Students Only) </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="english_language_proficiency_international_firstyear_students_only"> 
                        <p>You can demonstrate proficiency by meeting any of the following exam benchmarks. Keep in mind that you must complete one of these exams by December of your final year of high school/secondary school.</p> 
                        <p>• Score 24 or higher for the ACT English Language Arts (ELA)<br> • Score 31 or higher on Writing and Language in the SAT&nbsp;<br> • Score 3, 4 or 5 on the AP examination in English Language and Composition, or English Literature and Composition<br> • Score 6 or 7 on the IB Standard Level examination in English (Language A only)&nbsp;<br> • Score 5, 6 or 7 on the IB Higher Level examination in English (Language A only)&nbsp;<br> • Score 6.5 or higher on the International English Language Testing System (IELTS)<br> • Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL) examination:<br> &nbsp; &nbsp;- Internet-based test (iBT): Minimum score of 80 or better<br> &nbsp; &nbsp;- Paper-delivered test: Minimum score of 60 or better<br> • Duolingo English Test (DET): Minimum score of 115&nbsp;</p> 
                        <p><strong>Become a More Competitive Applicant </strong><br> Improve essential academic and languages skills with UCR’s <a href="https://www.iep.ucr.edu/program/70447/AdmissionPreparationProgramAPP" target="_blank">Admission Preparation Program (APP)</a>&nbsp;!</p> 
                        <p><small>Participating in this educational program does not in itself provide preference in admission to the University of California. If you're interested in applying to UCR, view the <a href="/freshman#apply" title="Freshman Student Admissions - Requirements">first-year student admission requirements</a> or the <a href="/transfer#apply" title="Transfer Student Admissions - Requirements">transfer student admission requirements</a>. You can also contact the <a href="/contact" target="_blank" title="Contact UCR Undergraduate Admissions office">UCR Undergraduate Admissions office</a> for details about the admission process.</small></p> 
                       </div> </li> 
                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#exam_credit" class="accordion-title"> Exam Credit </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="exam_credit"> 
                        <p><strong>Advanced Placement </strong><br> UCR grants credit for all Advanced Placement exams on which a student scores three or higher. Units awarded toward UC graduation may be granted for specific subjects and/or for general education/ breadth requirements, as determined by each campus. AP exams can also be used to meet the minimum transfer admission subject requirements in English, math, and four other courses chosen from the humanities, behavioral/social sciences, and biological/physical sciences.</p> 
                        <p><strong>International Baccalaureate</strong><br> The IB organization awards either a diploma or a certificate for individual IB exams. Students who complete the IB diploma with a score of 30 or above will receive 30 quarter (20 semester) units toward their UC degree. Students who receive IB certificates with a score of five or higher on Higher Level exams will receive eight quarter (5.3 semester) units.</p> 
                        <p><strong>A-Level Examinations</strong><br> UCR grants credit for the General Certificate of Education (GCE) Advanced Levels (A-Levels), the Cambridge International A-Levels, the Singapore-Cambridge GCE A-Levels (H2), and the previously administered Hong Kong Advanced Level Examination (HKALE), in content-based subjects similar to courses offered at UC and in which grades of A*, A, B, or C are awarded. UCR grants 12 quarter (eight semester) units of credit for academic courses only for GCE, Singapore-Cambridge, and Hong Kong A-Level exams.</p> 
                        <p><strong>Exam Credit&nbsp;</strong></p> 
                        <ul>
                         <li>AP exams with a score of 3 and above</li> 
                         <li>IB HL exams with a score of 5 and above</li> 
                        </ul> 
                       </div> </li> 
                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#high_school_coursework" class="accordion-title"> High School Coursework </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="high_school_coursework"> 
                        <p>You must earn a grade of C or better in all college-preparatory (“a–g”) courses:</p> 
                        <ol type="A">
                         <li>History/Social Science: 2 years</li> 
                         <li>English: 4 years</li> 
                         <li>Mathematics: 3 years, 4 recommended</li> 
                         <li>Laboratory Science: 2 years (3 years recommended)</li> 
                         <li>Language Other than English: 2 years, 3 recommended</li> 
                         <li>Visual and Performing Arts: 1 year</li> 
                         <li>College-Preparatory Elective: 1 year</li> 
                        </ol>
                        <ul></ul>
                        <p>For country-specific admission requirements, visit <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/international-applicants/applying-for-admission/freshman-requirements-country.html" target="_blank">international admissions requirements</a> by country or educational system. For questions, visit <a href="https://admissions.ucr.edu/ask" target="_blank">admissions.ucr.edu/ask</a>.</p>

                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#academic_achievement" class="accordion-title"> Academic Achievement </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="academic_achievement">

The assessment of a qualifying GPA is based on “a–g” courses (required high school/secondary classes) completed in years 10 and 11. If the high school/secondary school offers a different curriculum, your academic achievement will be based on that educational system’s grading scale.</p>

                       <h3 id="transfer">International Transfer</h3> 
                       <p>Along with the minimum admissions requirements that California Community College transfers must meet, transfers from international colleges must also follow these <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/international-applicants/" target="_blank">instructions</a> that apply specifically to them.&nbsp;Prepare for admission to UCR with the <a href="https://studentdocs.ucr.edu/admissions/uc-riverside_international-transfer-admission-checklist.pdf" target="_blank" title="International Transfer Admission Checklist">International Transfer Admission Checklist</a>. UCR's admissions selection process is the same for both international and U.S.-educated applicants.</p> 
                       <p><strong>Transfer applicants</strong> are registered at a college/university for a regular session after high school. Transfer applicants cannot disregard their college record and apply as a first-year student.</p> 
                       <p><strong>U.S. citizens or permanent residents</strong>, who have completed coursework outside the United States, should follow the international admissions requirements for the country in which the coursework was completed. If you are transferring from a four-year, out-of-state two-year, or UC school, please see <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/international-applicants/" target="_blank">special instructions</a> that apply to you.</p>

                        <p><strong>Breadth Requirements </strong><br> You are strongly encouraged to focus on preparatory course work (mathematics, science, and technical work) for your desired major rather than on <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/transfer-requirements/preparing-to-transfer/general-education-igetc/igetc/" target="_blank" title="IGETC">IGETC</a> completion. However, the Marlan and Rosemary Bourns College of Engineering (BCOE) does accept completion of IGETC as satisfying the majority of breadth requirements. Additional breadth coursework may be required after enrollment at BCOE.</p> 
                        <p><strong>Prerequisites </strong><br> Strong technical preparation is essential for success in the admissions process and, subsequently, in all coursework at BCOE. If you intend to transfer to an engineering major, you are expected to complete the equivalent of UCR coursework required in the first two years of the programs and apply for transfer starting your junior year. Use <a href="http://ASSIST.org" target="_blank">ASSIST.org</a>&nbsp;to see the prerequisites you must take for your intended major (and to make sure your classes will transfer).</p> 
                        <p><strong>Minimum GPA </strong><br> Strive to surpass the 2.8 minimum GPA.</p> 
                        <p>Learn more about transferring to <a href="https://www.engr.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">BCOE</a>.</p>

                        <p><strong>Minimum GPA</strong><br> Strive to surpass the 2.8&nbsp;minimum GPA.</p> 
                        <p>Learn more about transferring to <a href="http://chass.ucr.edu/future_undergrads.html" target="_blank">CHASS</a>.<br> Connect with the <a href="https://chassf1rst.ucr.edu/transfers-f1rst" target="_blank">Transfers First Program</a> for support, advocacy, planning, and more.</p>

However, courses taken to satisfy IGETC may also go toward completing those requirements.</p>

                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#gather_your_information" class="accordion-title"> Gather Your Information </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="gather_your_information"> 
                        <p>Before you begin, find out what <a href="https://apply.universityofcalifornia.edu" target="_blank" title="Transfer Important Information">important information</a>&nbsp;is needed for an accurate winter admission application.</p> 
                        <p>You will need your:</p> 
                        <ul>
                         <li>Transcripts</li> 
                         <li>Test scores</li> 
                         <li>Annual income (optional)</li>

                         <li>Credit card (or check)</li>

                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#answer_personal_insight_questions" class="accordion-title"> Answer Personal Insight Questions </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="answer_personal_insight_questions"> 
                        <p>Express who you are, what matters to you, and what you want to share with us by answering one required and an additional three out of seven <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/how-to-apply/applying-as-a-transfer/personal-insight-questions.html" target="_blank" title="Personal insight questions">personal insight questions</a>.</p> 
                       </div> </li> 
                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#complete_the_uc_application" class="accordion-title"> Complete the UC Application </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="complete_the_uc_application"> 
                        <p>The&nbsp;<a href="https://apply.universityofcalifornia.edu" target="_blank" title="UC Application">UC application</a>&nbsp;for fall admission to UCR opens on Aug. 1,&nbsp;and the fall application filing period is Nov. 1–30. The <a href="https://apply.universityofcalifornia.edu" target="_blank">UC application</a> for winter admission opens on July 1, and the winter application filing period is July 1–31.</p> 
                        <p>The fee is $80 for each UC. You can apply for a fee waiver — provided you have your family’s income and the number of people supported by it — when you fill out the application. If you have difficulties, contact&nbsp;<a href="mailto:ucinfo@applyucsupport.net" title="ucinfo@applyucsupport.net">ucinfo@applyucsupport.net</a>&nbsp;or&nbsp;call<strong> (800) 207-1710 (in the U.S.) </strong>or<strong> +1 (310) 513-2715 (outside of the U.S.).</strong></p> 
                       </div> </li> 
                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#after_you_apply" class="accordion-title"> After You Apply </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="after_you_apply">

These include printing your receipt, ordering test score reports, and updating your application, if needed.</p> 
                        <p>UCR Undergraduate Admissions will email instructions on how to access your&nbsp;<a href="https://my.ucr.edu/" target="_blank" title="MyUCR">MyUCR</a>&nbsp;admission portal account. Log in regularly to check your admission status, respond to messages, and track important dates and deadlines.</p>

                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#campus_safety" class="accordion-title"> Campus Safety </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="campus_safety"> 
                        <p>Safety is a top priority at UCR. We have a 24/7, on-campus <a href="http://police.ucr.edu" target="_blank" title="Police department">police department</a>, an emergency notification system that sends text alerts, a no-tolerance policy against sexual violence, and secure residence halls with video surveillance, restricted card access, and live-in staff. At night, our <a href="https://police.ucr.edu/community-service-officers" target="_blank" title="Community Service Officers">Community Service Officers</a> patrol halls and parking lots, and <a href="https://wrc.ucr.edu/programs/campus-safety-escort-service" target="_blank" title="Campus Safety Escorts">Campus Safety Escorts</a> accompany students across campus.</p> 
                       </div> </li>

Use the Career Center's HandShake tool to search for jobs and internships on campus and off.&nbsp;<span class="reply__text">Take part in <a href="https://careers.ucr.edu/resources/career-planning/career-development-programs " target="_blank" title="Get HIRED">Get HIRED (Help International students Reach Employment Destinations)</a>, a career-development program that provides career-related, skills-building workshops, information, and resources to international students in support of their U.S. and global job searches</span></p> 
                       </div> </li>

                      <li class="paragraph paragraph--type--accordion paragraph--view-mode--default accordion-item" data-accordion-item> <a href="#for_student_parents" class="accordion-title"> For Student Parents </a> 
                       <div class="accordion-content" data-tab-content id="for_student_parents"> 
                        <p>From childcare to housing, transportation to legal services, nurture the development of your family with these campus programs and accommodations.</p> 
                        <p><a href="https://gohighlanders.com/sports/2008/12/23/CubClub.aspx?tab=cubclub" target="_blank">Cub Club</a> invites your child to cheer on the NCAA Division I Highlanders Athletics Program. The annual membership includes free admission to all regular season home athletic events, a birthday card from mascot Scotty, discounts, and more.&nbsp;</p> 
                        <p>UCR’s <a href="http://ecs.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Early Childhood Services (ECS)</a> provides a warm and welcoming environment for children between the ages of two months and five years. Qualifying undergraduate students — primarily Pell Grant recipients — can have full daycare tuition and meals covered for their children. <span class="mdi mdi-18px mdi-play-circle">&nbsp;</span> <a href="http://ecs.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Video</a></p> 
                        <p><a href="http://housing.ucr.edu/housing-options/family-student-housing.html" target="_blank">Oban Family Housing</a> is a collection of one- and two-bedroom apartment homes directly adjacent to the UCR campus. UCR’s <a href="http://ecs.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">ECS</a> and Riverside’s public and private schools are nearby.&nbsp;</p> 
                        <p>Private<a href="https://hr.ucr.edu/employee-resources/lactation-accommodation-program" target="_blank"> lactation rooms</a> are available on campus for breast-feeding mothers. Refrigerator, plush seating, and sanitary provisions are provided.</p> 
                        <p><a href="http://rkids.ucr.edu" target="_blank" title="R'Kids">R'Kids</a> is a student organization that provides support for students, faculty, and staff who have children, are expecting children, or will have children. Parents can discuss experiences, learn from each other, and offer advice, while their children are involved in activities and entertainment.</p> 
                        <p>Scotty Cubs and Parents is a student-parent run support group that creates a safe space for students with families. Discussion topics have included: birthing stories, balancing school, home and baby, parenthood, relationships, academic success, and student life at UCR. Scotty Cubs and Parents is sponsored by the <a href="https://wrc.ucr.edu/" target="_blank">Women’s Resource Center (WRC)</a> and <a href="http://rkids.ucr.edu" target="_blank" title="R'Kids">R'Kids</a> .</p> 
                        <p>The <a href="https://recreation.ucr.edu/src-child-care" target="_blank" title="SRC Child Care">SRC Child Care</a> program lets you work out at the Student Recreation Center (SRC) while your child (infant through 15 years) plays in the safe and secure child care area.&nbsp;</p> 
                        <p>The <a href="http://auxiliarydocs.ucr.edu/ecs/ucr-student-parents-guide.pdf" target="_blank" title="UCR Family Resource Guide">UCR Family Resource Guide</a> , created by a student-parent group, lists campus and community resources available to student parents, including child care, housing, health, and nutrition.</p> 
                        <p>SRC <a href="https://recreation.ucr.edu/youth-programs" target="_blank" title="SRC Youth Programs">Youth Programs</a> take place during the summer, and introduce children (ages 5–14) to sports and educational instruction.</p> 
                       </div> </li>
